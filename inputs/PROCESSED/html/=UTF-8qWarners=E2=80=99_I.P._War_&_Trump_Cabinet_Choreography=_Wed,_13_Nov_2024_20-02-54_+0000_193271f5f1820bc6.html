The Grenell Snub, Wall Street Future-Proofing, NFL Growing Pains

Welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon assortment of Puck’s best new reporting.

First up today, Eriq Gardner spotlights the latest twist in the long-running legal feud between Warner Bros. TV and the fiercely protective Michael Crichton estate over Max’s new medical drama, The Pitt , which emerged just as talks to reboot ER —the ’90s NBC juggernaut that Crichton created—fell apart. It may not be a copyright case, per se, but it could have real implications for how “derivative works” are assessed.

Plus, below the fold: Tara Palmeri details the blowback inside Mar-a-Lago as Donald Trump begins stocking his administration. Marion Maneker turns a klieg light on the Cecily Brown phenomenon. On The Town , Matt Belloni is joined by director R.J. Cutler to candidly discuss Martha Stewart ’s disapproval of his new Netflix documentary. On The Varsity , John Ourand and NBC Sports’ Mike Florio unpack the biggest issues facing the NFL today. And on The Powers That Be , Bill Cohan joins Peter Hamby to examine how Wall Street is pricing in the second Trump era—the tariffs, the tax cuts, and a fresh wave of corporate bullying.

The Reskinning of an ‘ER’ Reboot

ERIQ GARDNER

Warner Bros. TV has fired back at Sherri Crichton’s lawsuit over Max’s new medical drama, ‘The Pitt,’ which she claims is little more than an ‘ER’ knockoff. Max claims that Sherri is trying to monopolize the medical genre. But it’s not that simple.

The Grenell Snub, Rubio’s Rise & The MAGA Divide

TARA PALMERI

Tensions arise in Mar-a-Lago as Trump bypasses longtime confidant Ric Grenell for Marco Rubio as Secretary of State, sparking angst among the MAGA faithful and perpetuating questions about Trump’s loyalty.

A Cecily Brown Christmas

MARION MANEKER

A major retrospective at the Dallas Museum of Art, plus a show at the Paula Cooper Gallery, make the case for the painter as one of the most compelling, intellectually and politically engaged artists at work today.

Martha Stewart & When a Celeb Disavows Their Doc—With the Director!

MATTHEW BELLONI

Matt is joined by R.J. Cutler, director of the Netflix documentary ‘Martha’ and the upcoming film ‘Elton John: Never Too Late,’ to talk about Martha Stewart’s disapproval of the film, directors maintaining creative control over their projects, and the inherent and necessary struggle of collaborating with a major celebrity on a documentary.

NFL Growing Pains

JOHN OURAND

John is joined by NBC Sports’ Mike Florio, creator of ProFootballTalk, to contemplate the biggest dramas facing the NFL today: the debate surrounding whether (and how) to add an additional game to the schedule, the push to expand its international footprint, the league’s surprising difficulty selling NFL media, and much more.

Wall Street’s Trump 2.0 Trade

PETER HAMBY & WILLIAM D. COHAN

Bill Cohan joins Peter to examine how Wall Street is pricing the second Trump era—the tariffs, the tax cuts, and all the corporate bullying. Bill runs down the list of familiar faces jockeying for Cabinet positions (Gary, Schwarzman, Paulson…), plus some surprising names that are suddenly in contention. Then they discuss how investors are playing the market rally, and whether crypto will get a Trump bump.
