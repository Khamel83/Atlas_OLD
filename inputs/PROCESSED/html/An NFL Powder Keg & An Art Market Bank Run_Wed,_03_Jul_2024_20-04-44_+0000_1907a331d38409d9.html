TikTok Omerta, Biden Media Games & The End of Pay TV

Welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon roundup of the best new journalism on Puck.

The NFL’s $15B Sunday Ticket Problem

MATTHEW BELLONI

Matt is joined by Puck’s John Ourand to break down the multibillion-dollar NFL Sunday Ticket lawsuit. John explains how this bill could actually balloon to nearly $15 billion under antitrust law, how this will impact Sunday Ticket’s new distributor, YouTube, and whether this could impact the greater sports media rights landscape.

Why Won’t Hollywood Speak Up for TikTok?

ERIQ GARDNER

Despite its free speech history, Hollywood isn’t joining the First Amendment challenges to the U.S. government’s TikTok ban. A deep dive into why studio heads, producers, and writers might be making the wrong call.

The Biden Media Blame Game

PETER HAMBY & DYLAN BYERS

Dylan Byers joins Peter to dig into into the Washington media reaction to debate-gate—the self-righteousness, anger, frustration, and general sense that the age issue, once deemed too sensitive, is all that matters.

The Art Market Bank Run Theory

MARION MANEKER

Art advisor Jacob King offers a thesis that absolutely no one wants to hear: After a decade of collectors entering the market with an “investment mindset,” the inevitable high prices that have resulted seem to have choked off the market. So what happens if everyone decides to head for the exits all at once?

Biden Plunges in Swing States in Leaked Post-Debate Poll

PETER HAMBY

The president is losing critical support in key battlegrounds, according to data from a premier Democratic firm, and putting previously noncompetitive states in play. Also, for the first time, he’s polling behind Kamala Harris.
