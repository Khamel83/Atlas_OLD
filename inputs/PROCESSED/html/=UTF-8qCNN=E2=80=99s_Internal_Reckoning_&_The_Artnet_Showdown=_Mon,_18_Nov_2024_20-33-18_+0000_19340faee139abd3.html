Iger’s P.E. Option, Greeny’s ESPN Memoirs, The NFL-Amazon Tryst

Welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon dispatch containing Puck’s best new reporting.

First up today, Dylan Byers reveals the fresh tremors inside Hudson Yards as Mark Thompson finally unveils concrete plans for CNN’s political coverage during Trump 2.0—one week after the network averaged its lowest total day audience in a quarter of a century . But is MAGA-world anthropology actually a winning programming strategy?

Plus, below the fold: Marion Maneker chronicles the tug-of-war over Artnet between the controlling Neuendorf family and a major shareholder. Bill Cohan makes the case for WBD, Disney, and NBCU going the P.E. route as their streaming businesses finally turn a corner and they look to offload terminally declining TV assets. On The Varsity , John Ourand huddles up with ESPN’s Mike Greenberg to revisit his nearly three decades at the network and the evolving market for on-air talent. And on The Powers That Be , Jon Kelly reunites with Peter Hamby to discuss the latest dramas underpinning the media industry: fallout from the NFL-Amazon romance, the plummeting ratings for CNN and MSNBC, and more.

The Mark-est Hour

DYLAN BYERS

A gloomy company-wide meeting revealed Mark Thompson’s CNN is still caught between the linear past and the digital future, and nonpolitical news versus 24-hour Trump TV. Insiders wonder who’s paying attention to the content while others wonder if a more sinister fate beckons.
