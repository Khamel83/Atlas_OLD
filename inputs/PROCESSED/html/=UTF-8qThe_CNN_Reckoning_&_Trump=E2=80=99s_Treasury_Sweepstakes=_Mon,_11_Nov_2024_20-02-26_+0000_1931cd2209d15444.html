Art’s November Surprise, Trickle-Down Zazonomics, Brinkley’s Hard Truths

Happy Monday and welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon download of Puck’s best new reporting.

First up today, Dylan Byers digs into CNN’s looming post-election transformation as network boss Mark Thompson finally kicks off his long-awaited, high-stakes digital overhaul of the declining brand—a grueling process that will include the culling of hundreds of jobs.

Plus, below the fold: Bill Cohan scrutinizes the Trump 2.0 treasury secretary shortlist. Marion Maneker evaluates the art world’s cautious optimism surrounding the upcoming November auctions. John Heilemann chronicles the Democratic Party’s internal reckoning. On Somebody’s Gotta Win , Tara Palmeri huddles up with Peter Hamby and media executive Mosheh Oinounou to relitigate the Harris campaign’s missteps. On Impolitic , John is joined by political and cultural historian Douglas Brinkley to assess how much Biden is to blame for Trump sailing to victory. And on The Powers That Be , Jon Kelly and Peter discuss how the media can regain its credibility during a second Trump administration and whether The Washington Post ’s return-to-office mandate is really a covert effort to purge its ranks.

CNN’s Existential Post-Election Season

DYLAN BYERS

With the election in the rearview, C.E.O. Mark Thompson will finally implement his true transformation plan at the network—including the culling of hundreds of jobs. Many of CNN’s own journalists, plenty of whom were blinded by Trump’s significant victory, have evinced similar naiveté about their own fates.

Trump Treasury Prophecies & Trickle-Down Zazonomics

WILLIAM D. COHAN

News and notes on two of Wall Street’s hottest topics: speculation regarding Trump’s forthcoming treasury secretary (Steve? Gary? Paulson?) and the latest debt-servicing tea leaves in Zazworld.

The Art Market’s New November Math

MARION MANEKER

A rise in the competition for third-party guarantees and attractive presale estimates indicate that dealers and consignors are feeling good about the upcoming auctions, including Mica Ertegun’s $95 million Magritte, Sid Bass’s $50 million Ruscha, and a “value proposition” $20 million Basquiat.

The Post-Harris Blamescape & Dem Reckoning Tea Leaves

JOHN HEILEMANN

News and notes and fresh reporting on the current targets of professional Democratic rage: Harris, Team K, Biden, Biden’s people, wokeism and more.

Election Hangover, With Mosheh Oinounou & Peter Hamby

TARA PALMERI

Tara and her Puck colleague Peter Hamby join Mosheh Oinounou on his podcast, ‘Mo News,’ to discuss the fallout of the 2024 presidential election. They break down the strategies of both parties throughout their campaigns, debate Kamala Harris’s missteps, and speculate on what’s next for Team Trump.

Douglas Brinkley: Extreme Incaution, Enemies Lists & The Age of Trump

JOHN HEILEMANN

John is joined by political and cultural historian Douglas Brinkley to discuss the 2024 election and Donald Trump’s return to the White House. Brinkley explains the harsh impact of Kamala Harris’s defeat on Joe Biden’s legacy, and how Biden only has himself to blame; how Trump managed to increase his share of the vote countrywide; and why Harris’s extreme caution was her greatest weakness and Trump’s extreme incaution his greatest strength.

Media Monday: The CNN Reckoning & The Trump Effect

PETER HAMBY & JON KELLY

Jon Kelly and Peter jump into a brief recap of last week’s Election Day surprises before diving into the future of the media industry under a second Trump administration. They tackle CNN’s hundreds of impending layoffs, the Washington Post’s eyebrow-raising return-to-work policy, how media at large has grown out of touch with modern politics, and more.
