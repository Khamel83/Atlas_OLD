Good morning,

I am happy to welcome Gregory C. Allen back for another Stratechery Interview. Allen is the director of the AI Governance Project and Senior Fellow of the Strategic Technologies Program at the Center for Strategic & International Studies , a Washington D.C. non-partisan think tank that was founded in 1962.

I first spoke to Allen last year about the China Chip Ban and its historical context, and again earlier this year about the Japanese and Dutch additions to those controls.

Now there are new developments: first, Huawei shipped a new smartphone with a SMIC-produced 7 nm chip , which raised major concerns in Washington D.C.; Allen’s most recent report is about this chip and Huawei’s rising prominence in China’s response to the chip ban. Then, last week saw the release of an Update to the chip ban that closed loopholes around both Nvidia chips and rationalized the export controls on equipment with Japan and the Netherlands.

Allen and I talked about both events, and how and why Washington’s thinking has evolved over the last year, not just because of China’s action, but also because of ChatGPT. We also discussed the challenges of drawing arbitrary lines, what policies actually make sense, and what impact the hardware ban might have on the software ecosystem.
