Wallace’s Exit, The X/DJT Deal Book, A Netflix Knockout

Welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon guide to Puck’s best new reporting.

First up today, Dylan Byers gets the skinny on Chris Wallace ’s self-defenestration at CNN as the 77-year-old TV veteran forgoes a seven-figure contract to pursue opportunities outside the linear ecosystem. Is Wallace merely anticipating Mark Thompson ’s digital overhaul of the network—or is he its first victim?

Plus, below the fold: Rachel Strugatz has all the gory details of the family drama playing out inside Estée Lauder after heiress Jane Lauder , passed over for the C.E.O. gig, knifed her cousin William in a leaked letter to the board. Bill Cohan runs the numbers on the baffling rumors of a merger between Musk ’s X and Trump ’s DTJ. On Impolitic , John Heilemann rings up former U.S. Senator Claire McCaskill for a blistering assessment of Trump ’s cabinet and White House staff picks. And on The Powers That Be , John Ourand joins Peter Hamby to debate ESPN’s politics-free programming gambit and Netflix’s evolving sports strategy.

How Chris Wallace Cut the Cord

DYLAN BYERS

The backstory of Wallace’s self-defenestration at CNN—the latest shoe to drop in an era that won’t stop ending.

The Lauder Blood Sacrifice

RACHEL STRUGATZ

After being passed over for the C.E.O. job, and as she exits her family’s storied beauty company, Jane Lauder took a sharpened eyeliner pencil to her cousin William in a leaked letter calling for his ouster from the board. Is this a petty family skirmish, an overdue governance change, or the first salvo in a new era at the historic beauty company?

The X/DJT Merger Deal Book

WILLIAM D. COHAN

Crunching the numbers on a Mar-a-Lago meme stock fever dream.

Claire McCaskill: Trump Staffs Up, Dems Hunker Down & Musk Runs Amok

JOHN HEILEMANN

John is joined by former U.S. senator Claire McCaskill to discuss the fallout from the election and the transition to Trump’s second White House term. McCaskill assesses Trump’s first batch of cabinet and staff appointments; the practical and political challenges of mass deportation; the potential for (or inevitability of) friction between Trump and Elon Musk; and the internecine finger-pointing consuming the Democratic Party.

ESPN’s Political Escape & A Netflix Knockout

PETER HAMBY & JOHN OURAND

John Ourand joins Peter to examine ESPN’s efforts to sustain itself as a politics-free zone in anticipation of a second Trump administration. Then they dive into Netflix’s upcoming Tyson vs. Paul fight, signaling the streamer’s plan to expand its sports strategy.
