NFL Rights Wars, Malone Estate Planning, Balthazar’s Election Night

Happy Friday and welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon compendium of Puck’s best new reporting.

First up today, Matt Belloni breaks down Bob Iger ’s blockbuster quarter: After losing billions on streaming, Disney finally projects major profits and 10 percent margins as the once “terrible” business shows signs of life. Too bad linear TV is losing money faster than streaming can replace it…

Plus, below the fold: Lauren Sherman assesses the latest LVMH succession intrigue as Alexandre Arnault leaves Tiffany to become deputy C.E.O. at Moët Hennessy. John Ourand digs into the NFL-Amazon partnership, which TV execs see as a precursor to an inevitable live sports rights showdown between legacy media and Big Tech. And Tara Palmeri presents her post-election conversation with Republican pollster Patrick Ruffini about the future of the G.O.P. coalition beyond Trump 2.0.

Meanwhile, in the Puck podcast universe: Dylan Byers connects with CNBC’s Alex Sherman on The Grill Room to dish about the Disney succession sweepstakes, John Malone ’s estate planning, and more. On The Town , Matt is joined by Roku Media president Charlie Collier to discuss how the streamer competes with tech behemoths like Amazon and Google. On Fashion People , Lauren rings up Emily Sundberg , of Feed Me fame, to chat about bridge-and-tunnel fashion, private members clubs, and the state of fashion media writ large. And on The Powers That Be , Lauren sits down with Peter Hamby to unpack Gap’s revival and what Trump’s tariffs could mean for luxury goods.

Bob Iger’s Netflix Moment

MATTHEW BELLONI

Disney’s C.E.O. has come roaring back from billions lost on streaming to project major profits and 10 percent margins as the onetime “terrible” business shows signs of life for everyone. If only TV wasn’t losing money faster.

Arnault Boy Is All Grown Up

LAUREN SHERMAN

The LVMH succession drama heats up as 32-year-old Alexandre—last seen at the Trump rally at MSG—leaves Tiffany for the mothership’s booze business, where his celebrity connections and brand building could prop up a lagging division desperately in need of reinvention.

Is the NFL Falling Hard for Amazon?

JOHN OURAND

For the first time, a streaming service outdrew one of the NFL’s top TV partners in same-week viewership. Now, TV execs are grumbling that the NFL is setting up a future rights battle by giving streamers a taste of potential glory with the choicest matchups.

The Oracle of ’24

TARA PALMERI

A post-election conversation with veteran Republican pollster Patrick Ruffini about what Democrats got wrong, Republicans got very right, and the bizarro rainbow coalition that ushered Trump back into the White House.

Disney Succession Tea Leaves

DYLAN BYERS

CNBC’s Alex Sherman joins Dylan for the ultimate dish-fest across a series of juicy and mission-critical topics–Disney succession tea leaves, Dana Walden versus the outsiders, John Malone’s Maffei moves, NBC’s “Roundball Rock” deal blockers, Comcast’s cable trial balloon, and much more.

Digital TV Giants: Amazon, Google, and... Roku?

MATTHEW BELLONI

Matt is joined by Roku Media president Charlie Collier to talk about the most under-discussed streaming player in Hollywood, how it differentiates itself from other companies, and how they compete with tech giants Amazon and Google. He also discusses what he learned running AMC and Fox’s broadcast network and how the industry has changed, then makes the case for the return of ad-supported television on streaming.

Bridge-and-Tunnel Fashion & Balthazar on Election Night

LAUREN SHERMAN

Lauren catches up with Emily Sundberg, author of the Substack Feed Me. They discuss Barbour jacket knockoffs, Casa Cipriani, how the fashion industry measures up to other crazy industries, Taylor Swift’s favorite restaurant Corner Store, cracks in the paint at Zero Bond, and plenty more. Plus, Lauren tips off listeners on what’s happening with LVMH heir Alexandre Arnault and a W magazine acquisition.

A Fashion Comeback Story & Trump’s Luxury Tariffs

PETER HAMBY & LAUREN SHERMAN

Lauren Sherman and Peter dig into the latest dramas percolating through the fashion industry: Gap’s attempt to revive itself as America’s favorite mall brand, the Arnault family cozying up to Donald Trump, what the former-and-future president’s tariffs could mean for luxury goods, and much more.
