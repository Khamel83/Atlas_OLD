Welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon assortment of Puck’s best new reporting.

Today, we lead with Lauren Sherman ’s exclusive reporting on the emerging consensus inside Chanel that creative wunderkind Matthieu Blazy will be named the spiritual successor to Karl Lagerfeld —the surest sign yet that C.E.O. Leena Nair is angling to restructure the business to be more like Hermès.

Plus, below the fold: Tara Palmeri uncovers the growing tensions surrounding Elon Musk ’s residency in Mar-a-Lago. Bill Cohan relays the saga of Wyc Grousbeck ’s leveraged buyout of the Boston Celtics. Scott Mendelson considers whether Gladiator II and Wicked: Part One will lead to a gangbusters Thanksgiving box office. Baratunde Thurston chats with John Ourand about how A.I. is infiltrating the business of sports. And Tara gets a readout on the jockeying to lead the D.N.C. from former Democratic congressman Max Rose .

But wait, there’s more! Lauren and BMO analyst Simeon Siegel link up on Fashion People to assess the state of the American mall. On The Town , Matt Belloni and Lucas Shaw bring back their fan-favorite fantasy draft of Hollywood’s buzziest young stars. And on The Powers That Be , Matt joins Peter Hamby to crunch the numbers on last weekend’s stellar box office.
