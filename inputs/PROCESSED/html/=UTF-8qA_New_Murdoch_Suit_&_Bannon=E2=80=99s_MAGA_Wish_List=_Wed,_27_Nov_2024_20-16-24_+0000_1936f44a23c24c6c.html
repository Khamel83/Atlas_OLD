Dunne Family Ties, Crypto’s $6.2M Banana, Media M&A Chatter

Welcome back to a special pre -Thanksgiving edition of The Daily Courant, your afternoon roundup of Puck’s best new reporting.

First up today, Eriq Gardner digs into the latest lawsuit against Fox News, where angry investors are claiming that the network’s embrace of right-wing conspiracy theories saddled the company with astronomical legal fees. Does Rupert Murdoch ’s counterpunch—that free speech outweighs fiduciary responsibility—offer an unexpected roadmap for navigating the potentially hostile environment for media under Trump 2.0?

Plus, below the fold: Peter Hamby presents his sprawling conversation with Steve Bannon , the Rasputin of the MAGA movement, about his unholy wishlist for the next administration. Marion Maneker spotlights the latest art market themes after New York’s auction houses raked in $1.3 billion last week. And in a special conversation presented by Meta, Baratunde Thurston connects with John Ourand to discuss the transformative impact of A.I. on the sports industry.

Meanwhile, on Impolitic , John Heilemann rings up Hollywood triple threat Griffin Dunne to discuss his recent memoir and the legacy of his celebrated literary family. On The Varsity , John Ourand and Bill Cohan consider the possible fate of Comcast’s SpinCo and the starring role of private equity. And on The Powers That Be , Marion convenes with Peter Hamby to make sense of the $6.2 million sale of Maurizio Cattelan ’s duct-taped banana.

Griffin Dunne: All in the Family

JOHN HEILEMANN

John is joined by actor and director Griffin Dunne to discuss his memoir about his famous family. Dunne offers intimate portraits of his sister Dominique, an actress on the rise four decades ago who was strangled to death; his father, whose coverage of Dominique’s murder trial turned him into the marquee chronicler of celebrity true-crime cases; and his aunt, Joan Didion, about whom Griffin made an acclaimed Netflix documentary.

Media Merger Mania & The Celtics L.B.O.

JOHN OURAND

Then Bill previews some potential M&A activity in the media space before recounting the surprising backstory to Wyc Grousbeck’s leveraged buyout of the Boston Celtics.

PETER HAMBY & MARION MANEKER

Marion Maneker joins Peter to unpack the discourse surrounding Maurizio Cattelan’s duct-taped banana, which just sold to a crypto zillionaire for a mind-blowing $6.2 million. Plus, Marion digs into this November’s auction house sales and whether the art market is reviving from the doldrums.
