Animal House Rules, Haring’s Missing Market, Streaming’s New Math

Welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon compendium of Puck’s latest reporting.

First up today, Peter Hamby illuminates how Kamala Harris dropped the ball with Gen Z voters—and not just beer-swilling Trump bros on college campuses—despite mounting a “vibes”-based, social-media-heavy campaign largely designed to win the hearts and minds of this essential demographic.

Plus, below the fold: Abby Livingston joins Peter on The Powers That Be for a necessary post-Election Night breakdown. Eriq Gardner unpacks the legal slugfest brewing between the NBA and Amazon. Marion Maneker digs into the historic sale of Keith Haring ’s subway paintings at Sotheby’s. Tara Palmeri chats with a pair of across-the-aisle former governors about enforcing the Electoral Count Reform Act. And on The Town , Matt Belloni and veteran dealmaker John Mass dissect how the streaming model upended the way money flows through Hollywood.

Kamala’s Wasted Youth

PETER HAMBY

Sure, young white dudes broke for Trump. But Harris also underperformed with almost every kind of young person: young white women, young Black voters, and young Latinos.

Burning Down the House

PETER HAMBY & ABBY LIVINGSTON

In the first of two post-election episodes, Abby Livingston joins Peter to assess the multidimensional fallout from Trump’s decisive victory, the consequences of Republicans taking the Senate, and whether last night’s MAGA red wave will consume the House, too. Then they turn to the demographic warning signs that so many pundits missed.

Keith Haring’s Missing Market

MARION MANEKER

Haring, the iconic New York street artist, became a pop culture icon but never developed a market to rival Basquiat, his more collectible contemporary. A sale of his subway drawings, at Sotheby’s, aims to change all that.

The NBA-Amazon Prenup Contains a Legal Time Bomb

ERIQ GARDNER

Courtside seating for C.E.O. Andy Jassy is just the cherry atop a highly redacted 35-page contract that gives Amazon the right to adopt future technologies for broadcasting basketball games—a mistake similar to the one the league made with TNT in failing to anticipate streaming. Isn’t that what the lawyers are for?

A Tale of Two Swing States

TARA PALMERI

Candid conversations with two former “Blue Wall” governors—Democrat Jim Blanchard of Michigan and Republican Tom Corbett of Pennsylvania—about which way their states will swing, why this year is no 2020, and the work they’ve done to Trump-proof the results.

How Much Money a Hit TV Show Can Make (And How It’s Changed)

MATTHEW BELLONI

Matt is joined by Content Partners president John Mass to talk about how making money in TV has changed in the last 10 to 15 years. John walks us through how a TV show is financed, how the market has changed because of the streaming model, and how it has upended the way participants make money. They also talk about the bygone days of producers owning shows, the heyday of the syndication era, and if a correction is coming.
