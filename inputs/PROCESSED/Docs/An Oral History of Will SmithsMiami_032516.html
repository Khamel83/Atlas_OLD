An Oral History of Will Smith's "Miami"

GQ · by Alex Siquig · March 24, 2016

THE GENESIS

Will Smith: I wanted to do a more personal video after “Just the Two of Us,” you know, something that really would make people feel things. So, I took the tracklist of Big Willie Style and threw gold darts at it and hit “Miami,” which was a song I sort of remembered recording.

Wayne Isham (Director): Yes, of course I knew the song well. I knew I was meant to direct this dystopian hellscape of a ditty. Most directors wouldn’t touch such vapid material, but most directors are cowards. As for me, I find there is power in spitting blood.

Martin Lawrence (co-star, Bad Boys): [Editor’s note: Lawrence is mysteriously wearing his costume from Big Momma’s House.] Will told me he wanted me to do a cameo as a priest who lost his faith and now teaches flamenco/martial arts. Then that was written out. Then he called me up and asked if I wanted to do a cameo as a guy wearing an interesting hat. I asked him what kind of hat. Needless to say, I didn’t get the answer I wanted.

Jada Pinkett-Smith (wife): [laughs] Vintage Will Smith.

CREATIVE TENSION

Isham: I hated Miami. Garbage weather, smiling gutter people, the torpid sense of humid doom that waits in the darkness of every alley and behind the dull, flat eyes of every helpful cabana boy, the hostility in the shadows of every swaying palm tree. I wanted to feed that city a napalm hamburger. I still do.

Smith: I told Poke and Tone (a.k.a. Trackmasters) that they could come hang around, since they produced the track. That was my second mistake. I shouldn't talk about the first.

Tone: In the video you hear Will say “Alright, Tone, get the plane!” But the original line is “What if some day or night a demon were to steal after you into your loneliest loneliness and say to you: 'This life as you now live it and have lived it, you will have to live once more and innumerable times more.' ... Would you not throw yourself down and gnash your teeth and curse the demon who spoke thus? Or have you once experienced a tremendous moment when you would have answered him: 'You are a god and never have I heard anything more divine.’ Let’s go to Miami now.”

Poke: Will was a corny man, almost definitely the corniest man in all the land. He’d tell you knock-knock jokes and be angry when you didn’t laugh. He would do this posh British accent whenever he was getting frustrated, like he’d mess up a line in the booth and be like “Blimey!”

Willow Smith (daughter): He still says “blimey” when he gets a splinter or slams the car door on his hand or falls headfirst down the stairwell. And sometimes, instead of snoring, he screams.

Pinkett-Smith: [laughs] Sadly, night-screaming is most assuredly some vintage Will Smith.

Isham: When a man screams in his sleep he is not broken, he is merely attempting to restore order to a world that exalts only the decadence of mayhem. And furthermore, I believe the devil does exist, and that's he's probably very boring.

"They were real, but they looked garish, like some idiot’s idea of what a palm tree should look like. I knew they would ruin the video’s authenticity. So we borrowed a page from Full Metal Jacket and imported our own palm trees."

Poke: Man, when we recorded the Men In Black song I almost lost my mind. I still have no idea what that song was about. Will insisted it was based on a “true story” but that if he told me he’d have to kill me, and not in a “cool” way. As far as “Miami” is concerned, yeah, I don’t really remember that one.

Tone: It was my idea that the woman would say “Bienvenidos a Miami” which at the time Will was not in favor of as it might confuse non-Spanish speakers. It was Nas who finally convinced him.

Nas (Hip-hop legend, alleged ghostwriter): What? Hell no. I had nothing to do with that. Who told you that? Give me their name, their number, and the five things they fear most in this world. No, I ain’t fucking kidding. Give me that list. My third least favorite thing is asking someone for a list and not getting the list. You’d know that if you ever played Illmatic backwards.

Smith: The only thing Nas did was give me the idea to shoot the video for “Miami” in Miami. We were going to shoot in Bel-Air and make it look like Miami, but my Uncle Phil wouldn’t let us use his house. But Miami was better. Authenticity. That’s what the Big Willie Style is all about.

ON LOCATION

Smith: The first day on set I saw this horrible man holding a camera and I said, “Oh no, tell me that dweeby nerd is not the cameraman!”

Daniel C. Pearl (cinematographer): I said something like “Pleased to meet you Mr. Smith I am a very big fan, my name is Dan, I am the cinematographer,” and he punched a brick wall and told me to call him the Fresh Prince if I must call him anything. I went to Arby’s and wept.

Isham: We spent months preparing for Eva Mendes’ big scene.

Eva Mendes (Girl in Car): Yes, that’s me. In the car.

Will Smith: Years later I told Eva on the set of Hitch that “Miami” was kind of a prequel to Hitch but she didn’t like that. She said it didn’t make any sense.

Eva Mendes (Girl in Car): I was originally supposed to have a much larger role. I was going to be playing a battlefield Nurse that Will falls in love with, like a Florence Nightingale situation, but that plotline got cut at the last minute. My house was like half an hour away, so I just grit my teeth and got in the car, which was full of Burger King wrappers and various biographies about Lyndon B. Johnson.

Isham: Will wanted to film the opening scene on location in Philadelphia. He wanted to do an elaborate “Boondock Saints thing” as he put it. And I said, “What if instead of a gunfight we just have a scene where you and your friends are cold and have to go someplace warm and Miami is that place?” And he laughed like a maniac and then high-fived me. I have filmed videos for artists such as 98 Degrees, 3 Doors Down, and Michael Jackson.

Pearl (cinematographer): The scene when they are happily looking at the beach from the plane? That’s not really a plane. It’s a bathroom.

Isham: The red carpet that receives them when they land in Miami was actually brown and very, very dirty. We used half our budget on fixing it up in post.

Smith: There is a scene where I am looking at myself in the mirror and two women walk up to me and say “Hi Will” and they both had very bad breath. I saw Wayne laughing and realized he was pulling a prank on me. It was a good prank, but I fired them anyway.

Jada Pinkett Smith: [laughs] Anyone who has ever been fired by my husband Will Smith knows that is some vintage Will Smith.

Isham: Let the lord of chaos rule.

Pearl: We spent six weeks filming the scene where the Fresh Prince puts on a green jacket and spins around to grab a drink from a waiter. It was a complicated shot.

Michelle Chin (Girl on Beach): Will Smith would do this trick every day where he would take sandwiches from the extras and run around laughing and then throw their sandwiches in the sand and say he was just offering tribute to the Sand God.

Pearl: Shooting on the beach was very boring, like waiting for the electric chair but there are also mosquitos.

Chin: One day all these Portuguese man-of-wars washed up on the beach. They were everywhere. I heard one of the camera guys say that this was just like the Egyptian plagues of the olden days. We laughed, but then one of the crew stepped on one and the director refused to cut because Will Smith was really nailing his dance moves during that take.

Isham: After much debate, we removed the audio of the man screaming. There was a lilting frenzy in his cries of pain that I thought was compatible with the melody of the song, but in the end Will was against it, citing private views on screaming.

Tommy Mottola (former head of Sony Music): I said, “Listen, I don’t care what you do and it doesn’t matter. All I want is a goddamn video to help me move units. And if possible, I want a smörgåsbord of ass. Best of luck to you. Goodbye and don’t call again.”

Isham: I didn’t like the way the palm trees looked. They were real, but they looked garish, like some idiot’s idea of what a palm tree should look like. I knew they would ruin the video’s authenticity. So we borrowed a page from Full Metal Jacket and imported our own palm trees. I have done videos for such artists as Megadeth, Metallica, and Pantera.

Pearl: Every night I drank a bottle of whiskey and dreamed strange whiskey dreams, like of snakes and of women who could turn into snakes. Sometimes I heard the Fresh Prince laughing in the distance. His laugh is the sound of daggers on a chalkboard.

THE GREAT WAR

Isham: It was around this time that Will was very loudly telling everyone that he was really into World War One. Or, as he calls it, “The Great War.”

Smith: Wayne will try to tell you the Great War was just something I heard about one day and got obsessed with. That’s not true. My cousin Carlton loaned me a book about it back in high school. It’s informed all my work.

Isham: He wanted to start adding subtle Great War themes into the video. I had to sort of remind him who the big dog was. I have directed videos for Jon Bon Jovi, Ricky Martin, and Journey. I am the one who told Journey what to do. Will Smith did not intimidate me.

Smith: Wayne was supposed to be some mad genius, but when I told him my Great War ideas he was afraid. I told him he was just like Falkenhayn and Joffre and the other cowards. I mean, I wanted to make a fun video too. I like dancing and smiling at fit ladies. But I also thought it should mean something. It’s not impossible to do both, right?

Pearl: It is impossible to do both.

Pinkett-Smith: Will called me screaming and said they weren’t going to let him do the Great War stuff he wanted to do and I told him, “Baby, I am busy showing someone Scream 2 right now, I will call you back.”

Smith: She didn’t call back.

Isham: I forged a compromise. I said, Will, you know how we are going to film that scene on the speedboats? How about you have a “great war” with a shark or some other kind of unstoppable ocean creature? He warmed to the idea, said the shark would represent the Austro-Hungarian Empire, and that he, Will Smith, would represent little Serbia fighting for independence. I remember clearly what I said: “Forget it Jake, it’s Chinatown.” Then I slapped him softly, as a friend would.

Smith: We rented a shark but it was taking too long so we changed the fighting a shark plot into a riding a boat to go to a Latin-themed dance number plot. I still got to wear the white pants I would have worn if I were fighting the shark, so all's well that ends well.

THE SCREENING

Smith: When the Climate Change comes and Miami is destroyed, this video will teach our descendants the importance of carbon footprints and how to treat our "beautiful fever-dream of a world" as Wayne calls it.

Isham: Will arranged a screening of the video at a party he was having back in the town of Bel-Air. When the video got to the part where Will says “$100,000 cars/everybody’s got ‘em” there was a cavalcade of aggressive booing, but it subsided, and we watched the rest of the video in silence.

Pinkett-Smith: My favorite parts were when Will was walking around Miami with pants on and then he rips them off and throws them to a man riding his bike. That is just vintage Will Smith.

Smith: I was very proud of our work. We had a helluva guestlist: Jada, DJ Jazzy Jeff, Bruck Ass (Jerry Bruckhemier), Gold Ass (Jeff Goldblum), Uncle Phil, both Aunt Vivians, Carlton, Hillary, Lisa, some friends from back in Philly.

Jayden Smith: I wasn’t born yet.

Jerry Bruckheimer (Producer, Bad Boys): I’d like to know more about the woman on the rollerblades.

Jeff Goldblum (co-star, Independence Day): It’s a music video. Seriously, what am I supposed to say?

Carlton Banks (cousin): A triumph of Will!

Isham: At the end of the day, “Miami” had a larger budget than films such as Eraserhead, Reservoir Dogs, Rocky, Mean Streets, and many other classics. By sheer coincidence, it is better than all those films.

Smith: If I have one regret it’s that that we never made it clear in the video why it was so cold in Philadelphia that one day. The background was that a snow monster had taken over the Eastern Seaboard and was spreading his snow magic down the coast. Me and my friends were going to Miami not to party, but to await our deaths with dignity.

Isham: That’s not true.
