Chapel Bill, WBD’s New Zazonomics, Gabbard’s Charm Campaign

Happy Friday and welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon dispatch featuring Puck’s best new reporting.

Before jumping in, we’re thrilled to announce that the legendary Hollywood journalist Kim Masters is joining Puck! Starting in February, Kim will reunite with Matt Belloni and Eriq Gardner on What I’m Hearing, while also breaking news and participating in our ever-growing suite of podcasts. (If you’re not signed up for What I’m Hearing for some crazy reason, remedy that now .) Welcome to the team, Kim!

On that note… Don’t miss Matt’s chronicle of the turbulence surrounding “Hollywood’s silliest awards show,” the Golden Globes, two years after billionaires Todd Boehly and Jay Penske turned the organization that puts on the event into a for-profit business—a notoriously fraught deal that has been plagued by conflicts of interests and a state attorney general inquiry related to its charitable arm. More on that below the fold.

Also in today’s issue: Lauren Sherman charts an especially thrilling round of designer musical chairs after Chanel finally confirmed Matthieu Blazy as its creative director. John Ourand deciphers how ESPN might capitalize on Bill Belichick ’s voyage to Chapel Hill. Tara Palmeri connects with veterans advocate Paul Rieckhoff to assess the very real concerns surrounding Pete Hegseth . On The Grill Room , Dylan Byers is joined by Bill Cohan to dissect the financial calculus behind Zaz ’s decision to restructure WBD. On Impolitic , John Heilemann reunites with MSNBC host (and his former Circus co-star) Alex Wagner . On The Town , Matt and the Entertainment Strategy Guy sort through the state of streaming. On Fashion People , Lauren and Skims C.E.O. Jens Grede discuss the brand’s rapid rise, their sold-out North Face collaboration, and the Kim Kardashian of it all. And on The Powers That Be , Ben Landy and Tara consider Tulsi Gabbard ’s charm campaign on the Hill.

The Lingering Golden Globes Stench

MATTHEW BELLONI

Two years after the Penske-Boehly takeover, and with the publicists’ war in the rearview, a new set of cascading challenges is befalling Hollywood’s perpetually scandalous awards show: apparent profiteering, marginalized membership, and an A.G. inquiry that threatens the whole party.

A MESSAGE FROM OUR SPONSOR

Set during the 1972 Munich Summer Olympics, the film follows an American sports broadcasting team that quickly adapted from sports reporting to live coverage of a hostage situation. This decisive moment changed media coverage forever and continues to impact live news today.

The Book of Matthieu

LAUREN SHERMAN

The appointment of Matthieu Blazy at Chanel, which the company has finally confirmed, represents a legit sea change in the industry—away from brand-manager-style creatives and back toward designer-designers. The move, of course, also set into motion a few others, all of which follow the same theme.

ESPN Prepares the Deion Playbook for Belichick

JOHN OURAND

For the past two seasons, Fox executives cashed in by betting on Deion Sanders’ ratings potential. Now ESPN, which has rights to the ACC, will run the same play with Belichick’s Tar Heels.

Red Pete

TARA PALMERI

A no-holds-barred conversation with Paul Rieckhoff, a fellow Iraq War vet turned activist, about the political atom bomb effect of placing Pete Hegseth at the Pentagon.

WBD’s New Zazonomics

DYLAN BYERS

Bill Cohan joins Dylan to dissect the stark financial calculus behind David Zaslav’s decision to separate Warner Bros. Discovery’s growth assets from its declining linear TV networks—a move that’s made Wall Street practically euphoric. Then they dive into Zaz’s ultimate endgame: to build, to sell, or to spin?

Alex Wagner: Trump Gets Triggered, Luigi Gets Caught & Guilfoyle Gets Exiled

JOHN HEILEMANN

John is joined by MSNBC primetime host Alex Wagner to discuss the big news stories of this past week and cultural touchstones of this past year. Wagner explains how Trump’s efforts at appearing reasonable in his recent ‘Meet the Press’ interview were foiled by his “spikes of insanity;” analyzes his naming of Kimberly Guilfoyle to be the U.S. ambassador to Greece; and weighs in on the hero worship of accused murderer Luigi Mangione.

Which Streaming Shows Hit and Bombed in 2024?

MATTHEW BELLONI

Matt is joined by the Entertainment Strategy Guy, a former streaming exec, to sort through the murky streaming data and find out which shows were hits, which were flops, which were underrated successes, and which were bigger bombs than anyone thought.

The Him of Skims

JOHN HEILEMANN

Lauren and Skims C.E.O. Jens Grede discuss the brand’s rapid rise and so much more: the new New York flagship, the sold-out North Face collaboration, Kim Kardashian, celebrity brands, working with your spouse, and the state of the luxury industry. Then, Lauren’s husband, Dan Frommer, stops by to discuss his “Consumer Trends: 2025” report, which features fresh intel on TikTok Shops and America’s enduring love of alcohol.

Guilfoyle in Exile & More Tulsi Game Theory

BEN LANDY & TARA PALMERI

Tara Palmeri joins Ben to discuss how a cheating Don Jr. got his ex-fiancée banished to Greece, and what the non-scandal scandal portends for fellow sex fiends R.F.K. Jr. and Pete Hegseth. Then they assess Tulsi Gabbard’s charm campaign on the Hill and whether Kash Patel will be the next nominee to fall.
