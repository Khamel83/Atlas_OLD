For a while there, Detroit’s income tax might as well have been optional.

About 400,000 people—residents and anyone working inside the city limits—are
required to file a tax return to Detroit. Almost half of them weren’t doing
so. Perhaps that explains, in part, why the city filed for bankruptcy four
years ago.
