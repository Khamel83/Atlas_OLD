#### About this story

To report this story, The Washington Post carried out an analysis of bidding patterns at the District芒聙聶s annual tax lien auctions from 2005 through 2011, using methods employed by expert statisticians in pattern detection, including bid rigging.

The Post first examined the bidding of more than 800 participants at the city芒聙聶s seven annual auctions by studying the order and frequency in which bidders won liens.

Using statistical analysis, the newspaper found clusters of back-and-forth bidding among top competitors that did not appear randomly in the running of 1,000 simulations.

The Post presented its findings to Daniel S. Levy, a statistical and modeling applications expert and the national managing director of Advanced Analytical Consulting Group in Boston. The firm, founded by a top Deloitte statistical director, analyzes fraud and collusion in financial markets.

Levy has been a court-appointed expert arbitrator for the Internal Revenue Service and has testified before state and federal courts on antitrust, tax and statistical matters.

Levy and a team of economists ran their own frequency tests to determine how often bidders deviated from their expected bidding, finding that six tax lien companies stood out for winning liens in patterns that did not appear random.

The patterns, found from 2005 to 2007, have not appeared脗聽in subsequent years.
