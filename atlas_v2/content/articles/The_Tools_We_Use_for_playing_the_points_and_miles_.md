## Award bookings

Once you’ve earned points and miles, the next step of the game is to use those
points towards maximum value. Here are concepts and tools for getting more out
of your points and miles…

### Transferable Points

Transferable points, such as Chase Ultimate Rewards, Amex Membership Rewards,
Citi ThankYou Rewards, Capital One Miles, and more are awesome because they
make it possible to find the best award deals and then transfer your points to
the appropriate program to book those deals. Here are some helpful resources:

  * [Current Transfer Bonuses](https://frequentmiler.com/current-point-transfer-bonuses/): There are often bonuses when transferring points to airline or hotel programs. Use this page to see which bonuses are current and which have expired (the latter gives you an idea of whether to wait in the hopes that a similar bonus will happen again).
  * [Complete List of Transferable Points Partners](https://frequentmiler.com/transfer-partner-master-list/): Use this page to find all transfer options from major transferable points programs.

### Hotel awards

  * **Trip Planning** : 
    * **[Awayz](https://frequentmiler.com/go/awayz):** Awayz makes it easy to find points-bookable hotels for your upcoming trips. Simply enter the location where you’re going and the dates when you’ll be there and Awayz will show you all of the points options, cash prices, and will recommend whether each hotel is a better deal paying with points or cash.
    * [**PointsYeah Hotels**](https://frequentmiler.com/go/pointsyeah/): Like Awayz, this tool makes it easy to find points-bookable hotels for your upcoming trips. PointsYeah also throws in useful information that is uniquely of interest to point-collectors such as the price to book hotels through AA or United (and how many miles you’ll earn), the price for booking through Fine Hotels & Resorts (and what benefits you’ll get), and more.
  * **Hard-to-find hotel awards** : Some of the most popular hotels can be difficult to book with points or free night certificates because their base rooms fill up quickly. My favorite tools for finding these awards are Rooms.aero (which is a sister site to [Seats.aero](https://frequentmiler.com/seats-aero/)), and [MaxMyPoint](https://frequentmiler.com/go/maxmypoint).

### Flight awards

Finding flight awards can be really tough. Fortunately, 3rd party tools can
help. For a comprehensive comparison of flight award search tools, see: [Which
award search tool is best?](https://frequentmiler.com/which-award-search-tool-
is-best/)

If you’re looking for a free general purpose tool,
[**PointsYeah**](https://frequentmiler.com/pointsyeah/) and
[**AwardTool**](https://frequentmiler.com/awardtool/) offer similar
capabilities. Both offer date range searches, award alerts, and capable
discovery functions. PointsYeah has an edge because its discovery function is
very good and it isn’t limited for free users. On the other hand, if you want
to search multiple airports at once, you’ll need AwardTool. Of course, since
the tools are free, you might as well use both!

For the paid tools, again my favorites are **PointsYeah** and **AwardTool**.
As a power user I prefer AwardTool thanks to the fact that its possible to
search up to 32 airports at once, but I’m not a fan of its bugginess.

Among the other tools, [**Seats.Aero**](https://frequentmiler.com/seats-aero/)
is my go-to. I love that I can do things like find available [Qatar QSuites
awards](https://frequentmiler.com/how-to-find-qatar-q-suites-award-space/) or
[Emirates first class awards](https://frequentmiler.com/how-to-find-and-book-
emirates-first-class-awards/), anywhere in the world, in seconds.

Regardless of which of the above tools you decide to use, if you regularly use
**Google Flights** , you should consider installing **[Points
Path](https://frequentmiler.com/points-path/) **so that you’ll automatically
get award prices within your Google Flights search results.

#### More flight award tools

  * Curated Award Alerts: Several services such as [Straight to the Points](https://straighttothepoints.co/) and [Thrifty Traveler Premium](https://thriftytraveler.com/premium/?source=home) alert readers when great flight awards are available.
  * Which routes exist? To find the best awards, it helps to know your options: 
    * [FlightConnections.com](https://frequentmiler.com/go/flightconnections): Graphically displays all direct routes from any given departure airport.
    * [FlightsFrom.com](https://www.flightsfrom.com/): Shows all flight routes that depart any selected airport.
    * [Wikipedia](http://www.wikipedia.org/): Pull up the Wikipedia page for any airport, then click to “Airlines and destinations.” This will show you all of the direct flights, by airline, from that airport.
  * [Award Booking Services](https://frequentmiler.com/award-booking-services/): If you don’t have the skill, knowledge, or time to find the best awards for your needs, consider employing an award booking service instead.

### Hotel deals

## Elite perks

  * [Best Credit Card Offers](https://frequentmiler.com/go/creditcards/): Many credit cards offer elite status directly or elite benefits (such as lounge access). This page shows the top perks for each listed credit card. Look for the “noteworthy perks” section for each card.
  * [Best Big Spend Bonuses](https://frequentmiler.com/go/bestbigspendbonuses/): Many credit cards offer elite status with high spend. This page lists the best options for earning status with big spend.
  * [LoungeReview](https://frequentmiler.com/loungereview/): This is an app that details lounge options at airports. Quickly see which lounges are available and the access rules for each.
  * [StatusMatcher.com](http://statusmatcher.com/): One of the best ways to get elite status is by asking for a match from another program. StatusMatcher is a useful site for finding out what status matches work and don’t work before trying yourself.
