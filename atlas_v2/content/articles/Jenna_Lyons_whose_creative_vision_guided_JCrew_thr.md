Jenna Lyons, president, creative director, and the de facto face of the J.Crew
clothing brand, [is parting
ways](http://web.archive.org/web/20250713124259/http://investors.jcrew.com/phoenix.zhtml?c=135311&p=irol-
newsArticle&ID=2259402&ver=jc) with the company after 26 years.

“Jenna and I got together and we both agreed it was time for a change,” CEO
Mickey Drexler told Business of Fashion, which [broke the
news](http://web.archive.org/web/20250713124259/https://www.businessoffashion.com/articles/news-
analysis/jenna-lyons-exits-j-crew) (paywall).

Somsack Sikhounmuong, the current head of women’s design at J.Crew, will
become the brand’s chief design officer and oversee men’s and children’s as
well.

For J.Crew, it marks the end of an era. Lyons’s creative influence helped the
brand become a US household name in the 2000s. She had been with the company
since 1990, and rose quickly after Drexler joined as CEO in 2003. In the
decade that followed, their partnership helped J.Crew [triple its
sales](http://web.archive.org/web/20250713124259/https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/business/wp/2015/03/23/sorry-
j-crew-women-shoppers-just-arent-that-into-you/?utm_term=.4873e0571dde), and
made fans including former US first lady [Michelle
Obama](http://web.archive.org/web/20250713124259/https://qz.com/887287/the-
inspiring-story-that-michelle-obama-told-about-american-fashion-in-nine-
outfits).

Lyons had a deft touch for giving J.Crew’s preppy staples a fashion-forward
twist with energetic colors and creative styling. In 2010, she became the
brand’s president, and has come to be a fashion celebrity with her own widely
admired style.

But many of J.Crew’s formerly loyal customers believe the company strayed [too
far from its
strengths](http://web.archive.org/web/20250713124259/http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/07/25/j-crew-
ceo-apologizes_n_3654196.html), becoming too fashion-centric in its product
offering and catalogue styling, at the expense of the wardrobe basics that
made it a closet staple. Some have blamed Lyons—[by
name](http://web.archive.org/web/20250713124259/http://nypost.com/2015/04/13/j-crew-
is-floundering-and-jenna-lyons-is-to-blame/)—though J.Crew also has had [other
problems](http://web.archive.org/web/20250713124259/https://www.yahoo.com/style/jenna-
lyons-is-not-j-crews-problem-116474615628.html), including customer
allegations that the [quality and
fit](http://web.archive.org/web/20250713124259/http://www.businessinsider.com/women-
are-turning-away-from-j-crew-2015-5) of its products have deteriorated over
the years.

Sales have now been falling for years, prompting the company [to
oust](http://web.archive.org/web/20250713124259/https://qz.com/425006/in-a-
big-shakeup-j-crew-fires-its-head-womens-designer-and-trims-its-staff) its
head of women’s design in 2015—but not Lyons, which was [somewhat
unusual](http://web.archive.org/web/20250713124259/https://www.nytimes.com/2015/06/12/fashion/somsack-
sikhounmuong-of-madewell-becomes-womens-wear-designer-at-j-crew.html) in the
fashion industry. The company instead brought in Sikhounmuong, previously head
designer at Madewell, J.Crew’s sister brand, which was enjoying the [sort of
success](http://web.archive.org/web/20250713124259/https://qz.com/365108/madewells-
outsized-growth-shows-its-the-new-j-crew-for-cool-kids) J.Crew has lately been
seeking for itself.

J.Crew has also suffered from the problems [plaguing US
retail](http://web.archive.org/web/20250713124259/https://qz.com/928770/urban-
outfitters-ceo-richard-hayne-says-us-retail-bubble-is-bursting-like-housing-
in-2008) more broadly, including increasing competition from cheap fast-
fashion competitors and an overabundance of brick-and-mortar stores. In its
most recent fiscal year, [reported on March
21](http://web.archive.org/web/20250713124259/http://www.prnewswire.com/news-
releases/jcrew-group-inc-announces-fourth-quarter-and-
fiscal-2016-results-300427223.html?tc=eml_cleartime), J.Crew sales fell 3%,
while sales at stores open at least a year dropped 7%.

Sikhounmuong will now have the task of trying to turn the brand’s sales
around. Lyons will remain a creative adviser to J.Crew through the end of the
year, when her contract runs out. Her position will not be filled, the company
said. Instead her duties will be shifted to other members of J.Crew’s
executive team.
