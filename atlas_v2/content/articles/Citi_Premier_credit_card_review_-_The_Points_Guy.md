## Citi Premier® Card overview

The[**Citi Premier®
Card**](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/apply-
now/citi-thankyou-premier-card/?tid=post) (see [rates and
fees](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/apply/citi-
premier-card-rates-and-fees?tid=post)) is an under-the-radar gem with a
phenomenal range of bonus categories. While the card isn't as strong on the
redemption side as similar offerings from Chase or Amex, the ability to earn 3
points per dollar on air travel, hotels, gas stations, supermarkets and
restaurants (including takeout) for [just $95 a
year](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/citi-
premier-worth-annual-fee/) is still a pretty great deal. **Card rating*:
⭐⭐⭐⭐**

######  _* Card rating is based on the opinion of TPG's editors and is not
influenced by the card issuer._

* * *

The Citi Premier Card is one of the best earners on a wide variety of everyday
spending. It accrues 3 points per dollar with airlines, hotels, gas stations,
restaurants and supermarkets — an excellent slate of bonus categories for a
card with a $95 annual fee.

Not only that, but the points you earn with the Citi Premier are among the
most versatile rewards currencies and are worth 1.8 cents each by [TPG
valuations](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/monthly-
valuations/). That's because Citi has improved the [ThankYou Rewards
program](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/redeeming-
citi-thankyou-points-maximum-value/) substantially over the years to compete
with [American Express Membership
Rewards](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/ultimate-
guide-amex-membership-rewards/) and [Chase Ultimate
Rewards](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/redeeming-
chase-ultimate-rewards-maximum-value/).

Here's what else you should know about this card — and why you might want to
apply now.

## Citi Premier welcome offer

LUKE CHAN/GETTY IMAGES

The [Citi
Premier](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/apply-
now/citi-thankyou-premier-card/?tid=post) is currently offering 60,000 bonus
points after you spend $4,000 on purchases within the first three months of
account opening. According to our valuations, that bonus is worth $1,080.
However, we have seen a public offer for 75,000 points for the same spending

for a Citi Premier account in the past 48 months.

## Redeeming points on the Citi Premier

The [Citi
Premier](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/apply-
now/citi-thankyou-premier-card/?tid=post) is also a top choice for travelers,
thanks to its participation in the [ThankYou Rewards
program](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/redeeming-
citi-thankyou-points-maximum-value/), which currently has 14 [airline transfer
partners](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/citi-
thankyou-transfer-times/) and two hotel partners.

While you'll notice that many of the frequent flyer programs are those of
international airlines, there are still some high-value (and easily
redeemable) awards to be booked through them.

ZACH GRIFF/THE POINTS GUY

For instance, you can leverage [Air France-KLM Flying
Blue](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/deals/air-
france-business-class-awards-paris-and-beyond/) miles for decent business-
class award availability on those airlines and their SkyTeam partners, get
some phenomenal deals on Star Alliance awards using [Avianca
LifeMiles](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/everything-
you-need-to-know-avianca-lifemiles/) and snag some great Oneworld tickets with
[Qatar Privilege
Club](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/news/qatar-
airways-partner-awards/).

You can also redeem your ThankYou Points for travel directly through the Citi
portal at a rate of 1 cent each, but that's well below [TPG's valuation of
ThankYou
points](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/monthly-
valuations) at 1.8 cents each, a number derived largely from Citi's extensive
list of transfer partners.

## Citi Premier benefits

JT GENTER/THE POINTS GUY

Much of the [Citi
Premier's](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/apply-
now/citi-thankyou-premier-card/?tid=post) value proposition comes from its
ability to earn bonus points in many categories without charging a hefty
annual fee. As such, you shouldn't expect an overwhelming number of perks, but
there are several benefits of this card that can help you recoup your annual
fee:

  * [**$100 annual hotel credit**](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/citi-premier-hotel-credit/)**:** You'll enjoy a $100 credit once per calendar year for single-stay hotel bookings of $500 or more (excluding taxes and fees) made through the Citi travel portal.
  * [**Extended warranty protection**](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/best-cards-for-extended-warranty/)**:** This adds 24 months to a manufacturer's warranty when you purchase a covered item using your card.
  * [**Damage and theft protection**](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/best-cards-purchase-protection/)**:** This can reimburse you for repairing or replacing an eligible item damaged or stolen within 90 days of purchase (up to $10,000 per incident and $50,000 per year).
  * [**World Elite Mastercard benefits**](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/world-elite-vs-world-mastercard/)**:** As a World Elite Mastercard, the Citi Premier confers several travel-related perks that consumers might not know about. These include a $5 monthly Lyft credit after taking three rides in a calendar month and access to the Mastercard luxury hotels and resorts portfolio for on-property perks like complimentary breakfast and room upgrades upon availability. Through the World Elite Mastercard program, you'll also enjoy perks like [cellphone protection](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/credit-cards/phone-protection/) and Global Emergency Services.
  * [**Citi Entertainment**](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/news/citi-entertainment-guide/)**:** This program gives cardholders special access to purchase tickets to thousands of events — from concerts and sporting events to unique dining experiences and movie screenings — before the general public can buy them.
  * [**No foreign transaction fees**](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/top-cards-with-no-foreign-transaction-fees/): Like any good travel card, the Citi Premier allows you to travel freely without incurring a fee on international spending.

## Which cards compete with the Citi Premier?

As an affordable travel rewards card, the Citi Premier has a few direct
competitors that might be a better fit for you:

  * **If you prefer Chase cards:** The [Chase Sapphire Preferred Card](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/apply-now/chase-sapphire-preferred-card/?pid=22125056&tid=post), with an annual fee of $95, competes directly with the Citi Premier. While [Chase's transfer partners](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/redeeming-chase-ultimate-rewards-maximum-value/) (including [United MileagePlus](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/maximizing-redemptions-with-united-mileageplus/), [Southwest Rapid Rewards](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/southwest-rapid-rewards/) and [World of Hyatt](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/ultimate-guide-world-of-hyatt/)) might be more beneficial for many travelers, there are also some overlaps, including Singapore KrisFlyer, Emirates Skywards and Virgin Atlantic Flying Club. For more information, read our [full review of the Sapphire Preferred](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/chase-sapphire-preferred-review/).
  * **If you want a flat earnings rate** : Try the [Capital One Venture Rewards Credit Card](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/apply-now/capital-one-venture-rewards-credit-card/?tid=post) (see [rates and fees](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/apply/capital-one-venture-rewards-credit-card-rates-and-fees/?tid=post)). It, too, charges a $95 annual fee, but this card comes with a less lucrative (but much simpler) earning rate of 2 miles per dollar on all purchases. Instead of an annual hotel credit, you'll get up to a $100 [Global Entry or TSA PreCheck](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/credit-cards/global-entry/) application credit once every four years with the Venture. You'll even get two annual lounge visits per year to [Capital One's lounges](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/capital-one-lounge/) — or you can use those passes at over 100 [Plaza Premium lounge locations](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/news/capital-one-plaza-premium-lounges/). For more information, read our [full review of the Venture Rewards card](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/capital-one-venture-card-review/).
  * **If you want added perks:** One of the Citi Premier's closest competitors over at American Express is the [American Express® Gold Card](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/apply-now/the-american-express-gold-card/?tid=post), which earns 4 points per dollar at restaurants, 4 points per dollar at U.S. supermarkets (on the first $25,000 in purchases per calendar year, then 1 per dollar), 3 points per dollar on flights booked directly with airlines or through [Amex Travel](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/amex-travel-guide/), and 1 point per dollar on other eligible purchases. The Amex Gold Card also comes with up to [$10 in monthly dining credits](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/register-amex-gold-dining-credit/) at select restaurants (up to $120 in annual statement credits) and up to [$10 in monthly U.S. Uber Cash](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/news/amex-gold-uber-benefit/) (up to $120 annually), offsetting the majority of the [$250 annual fee](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/amex-gold-card-review/) (see [rates and fees](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/apply/american-express-gold-card-rates-fees/?tid=post)). Enrollment is required for select benefits. For more information, read our [full review of the Amex Gold](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/credit-cards/american-express/amex-gold-card-review/).

## Bottom line

Despite a bevy of competitors, the Citi Premier's long list of bonus
categories and solid sign-up bonus make it one of the most lucrative points-
earning cards in its price range. The wide range of ThankYou Rewards transfer
partners makes this card compelling, especially for travelers who desire the
flexibility to redeem points with various loyalty programs. It's [worth a
look](http://web.archive.org/web/20230905134045/https://thepointsguy.com/guide/citi-
premier-worth-annual-fee/) if you're partial to Citi or looking for a
versatile travel card to add to your wallet.
