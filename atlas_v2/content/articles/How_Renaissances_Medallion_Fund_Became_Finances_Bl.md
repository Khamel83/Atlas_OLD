**Sixty miles east** of Wall Street, a spit of land shaped like a whale’s tail
separates Long Island Sound and Conscience Bay. The mansions here, with their
long, gated driveways and million-dollar views, are part of a hamlet called
Old Field. Locals have another name for these moneyed lanes: the Renaissance
Riviera.

That’s because the area’s wealthiest residents, scientists all, work for the
quantitative hedge fund Renaissance Technologies, based in nearby East
Setauket. They are the creators and overseers of the Medallion Fund—perhaps
the world’s greatest moneymaking machine. Medallion is open only to
Renaissance’s roughly 300 employees, about 90 of whom are Ph.D.s, as well as a
select few individuals with deep-rooted connections to the firm.
