Dissatisfaction with performance appraisals is pervasive. They are seen as time-consuming, demotivating, inaccurate, biased, and unfair. A [McKinsey survey](https://www.mckinsey.com/business-functions/people-and-organizational-performance/our-insights/harnessing-the-power-of-performance-management) indicates most CEOs don’t find the appraisal process in their companies helps to identify top performers, while over half of employees think their managers don’t get the performance review right. A [Gallup study](https://www.gallup.com/workplace/238064/re-engineering-performance-management.aspx) is more negative: Just one in five employees agreed that their company’s performance practices motivated them.
