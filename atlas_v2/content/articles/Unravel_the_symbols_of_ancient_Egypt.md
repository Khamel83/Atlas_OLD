Today marks the anniversary of the discovery of the [Rosetta
Stone](http://web.archive.org/web/20250518075251/https://g.co/arts/kwdy1xTvfWSfNuaS9),
the tool that first unlocked the mystery of ancient Egyptian hieroglyphs.
Ancient Egyptians used this writing system more than 4000 years ago to record
their stories, but only a select group knew how to read and write them.

But today, thanks to the new Google Arts & Culture tool
[Fabricius](http://web.archive.org/web/20250518075251/https://artsexperiments.withgoogle.com/fabricius/),
anyone can interactively discover this fascinating language by means of three
dedicated gateways: First, you can “Learn” about the language of ancient Egypt
by following a short educational introduction in six easy steps. Secondly,
Fabricius invites you to “Play” and translate your own words and messages into
hieroglyphics ready to be shared with your friends and family.
