# The Stash List

## 11th Edition

by [Bret
Sayre](/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/author/bret_sayre/)

**The Graduates:[Andrew
Heaney](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=99874)
(1), [Miguel
Gonzalez](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/player_search.php?search_name=Miguel+Gonzalez)
(20)**  
Heaney’s major-league debut went very well, albeit against a not-so-great Mets
offense—as he allowed five base runners in six innings while striking out
three. I’ve been talking about him for a while here, and I also had the
opportunity to put my money where my mouth is. [Mike
Gianella](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/author/mike_gianella)
and I won the FAAB bidding for Heaney this week in mixed LABR with a heavy-
handed $21 entry. There will be no time wasted in getting him into our lineup
this week as he gets two starts against the Phillies and Athletics. With
Miguel Gonzalez now healthy, the Orioles are going with a temporary six-man
rotation for as long as they have the roster flexibility to do so. Eventually
they’re going to have to cut one, and Gonzalez still may be furthest out on
the bubble.

**The Departed:[Francisco
Lindor](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=70399)
(24), [Joba
Chamberlain](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=55707)
(HM), [Casey
Kelly](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=58343)
(HM)**  
There’s nothing wrong with what Lindor is doing in the minor leagues—he’s
still an excellent prospect and fine fantasy one as well—but the Indians are
shaping up to hang around the AL Wild Card race and [Asdrubal
Cabrera](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=45398)
is playing reasonably well. At this point, it will take an injury to make room
for the future star shortstop. Chamberlain is also not a victim of his own
failures, but a victim of [Joe
Nathan](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=461)’s
success—at least recently. Nathan has put together two very strong outings
after making some mechanical adjustments, and the storyline is built for him
to run with it. He’s still worth keeping a peripheral eye on, but Nathan has
more rope now than a week ago. There’s a large soft spot in my heart for Casey
Kelly, and I still think he is going to be a good major league pitcher. But
this just appears to be another lost year for the athletic right-hander, who
just can’t get past these elbow setbacks. Don’t worry, Casey—I don’t give up
that easily.

**And the ones who are still left waiting:**

**1)[Kevin
Gausman](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=68557),
RHP, [Baltimore
Orioles](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=BAL)
(Previous Rank: NR)**  
Yes, it’s a little cheap to put Gausman back in here because the Orioles sent
him down to Triple-A as part of very smart roster management and not LOL
ORIOLES like most initially assumed. But it’s my list. In the brief time he’s
spent in the Orioles’ rotation, he’s been the best pitcher they have—and I
like him to continue to be their best pitcher the rest of the season. He is a
must-own in all leagues.

**2)[Taijuan
Walker](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=67148),
RHP, [Seattle
Mariners](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=SEA)
(Previous Rank: 2)**  
This week’s start was a little bit of a setback on paper, but it came in
Colorado Springs, so I think we can all take a deep breath about the stat
line. He’s proving that he is healthy and [Erasmo
Ramirez](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/player_search.php?search_name=Erasmo+Ramirez)
pitching in Seattle is a thing that we are all living through right now. It’s
only a matter of time before he’s up if the Mariners want to take themselves
seriously as contenders. Of course, if they don’t, we may very well see
another month or two of Erasmo.

**3)[Oscar
Taveras](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/player_search.php?search_name=Oscar+Taveras),
OF, St Louis Cardinals (Previous Rank: 3)**  
Back to biding his time in Triple-A, Taveras awaits an injury, a trade or the
desperation that would lead to him playing center field. Also, don’t be swayed
by any disappointing minor-league stats during this holding period—he’s ready
to hit at the majo- league level and he knows it.

**4)[Dillon
Gee](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=56334),
RHP, [New York
Mets](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=NYN)
(Previous Rank: 4)**  
On Tuesday, Gee will make his first rehab start since his lat strain back in
May. He’s had no issues leading up to this point, but at least two minor
league starts should be expected before he returns. This puts him on schedule
to come back and get two games under his belt before the All-Star break. Gee
will return to a 2.73
[ERA](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/glossary/index.php?search=ERA)
and 1.06
[WHIP](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/glossary/index.php?search=WHIP)—and
his status jumps from decent play to must-start at home.

**5)[Tyler
Skaggs](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=61000),
LHP, [Los Angeles
Angels](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=ANA)
(Previous Rank: 7)**  
After throwing a four-inning simulated game on Saturday, Skaggs appears ready
to take his rotation spot back later this week—although the Angels could opt
to have him take one rehab start before returning. There’s really no excuse
for him to be owned in only five percent of ESPN leagues—I like him to be a
top-50 starter the rest of the way barring further injury.

**6)[Mookie
Betts](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=70430),
2B/OF, [Boston Red
Sox](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=BOS)
(Previous Rank: 9)**  
Betts continues to rise up this list, but not because his ceiling (which is
currently being overstated in fantasy circles) is increasing. It’s because the
probability of him having fantasy value this year is getting greater by the
day. After another two-hit game last night, Betts is hitting .321 with two
homers and five steals in 20 games at Triple-A. Even with [Shane
Victorino](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=16634)
returning, the Red Sox outfield situation is murky enough that it would no
longer be a surprise to see him in the first half.

**7)[Jimmy
Nelson](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=65895),
RHP, [Milwaukee
Brewers](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=MIL)
(Previous Rank: 5)  
8) [Joc
Pederson](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=67072),
OF, [Los Angeles
Dodgers](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=LAN)
(Previous Rank: 6)  
9) [Noah
Syndergaard](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=67132),
RHP, New York Mets (Previous Rank: 8)**  
The injuries have certainly set Syndergaard back this year, but he still has
top-shelf talent, and it’s going to present itself at the major-league level
before long. Even if everyone stays healthy in the Mets’ rotation from this
point on, I would still expect him to replace deGrom in July or August—though
an injury would be the fastest way to get him up, especially in light of
[Rafael
Montero](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=69637)’s
injury.

**10)[Arismendy
Alcantara](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=66268),
2B, [Chicago
Cubs](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=CHN)
(Previous Rank: 14)**  
It only took 11 weeks, but [Javier
Baez](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=70387)
is no longer the highest-ranked Cub on this list. The part that may even be
more shocking is that it’s Alcantara and not Bryant who has passed him. With
Baez still scuffling and Bryant getting a near kiss of death from Theo
Epstein, Alcantara stands the best chance to be up in July. Also, after a
mini-slump a few weeks back, Alcantara is hitting .343/.413/.537 with eight
extra-base hits and seven steals in his last 18 games. Whether it’s at second
base or center field, the Cubs could use him.

**11)[Derek
Holland](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=56468),
LHP, [Texas
Rangers](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=TEX)
(Previous Rank: 10)  
12) Javier Baez, SS, Chicago Cubs (Previous Rank: 11)  
13) [Matt
Wisler](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=70497),
RHP, [San Diego
Padres](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=SDN)
(Previous Rank: 18)**  
There was definitely an adjustment period in Triple-A, as his 13.50 ERA in his
first four starts is now all but a memory as he’s put up a 2.72 ERA in his
last six starts, spanning 36 1/3 innings. That’s even more impressive when you
remember that El Paso (and that entire league, essentially) is a pretty tough
place to pitch. With [Eric
Stults](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=43330)
being Eric Stults and both [Andrew
Cashner](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=58136)
and [Robbie
Erlin](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=60740)
nursing injuries, the opening is there. It’s not like [Odrisamer
Despaigne](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=103737)
is a roadblock—regardless of how cool his name might be.

**14)[Archie
Bradley](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=70431),
RHP, [Arizona
Diamondbacks](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=ARI)
(Previous Rank: 16)**  
Bradley’s first official appearance since late April is now in the books, as
he threw four innings in the Arizona League on Saturday, allowing two runs and
five hits in four innings while striking out six. With the Diamondbacks going
nowhere fast this year, they’ll take their time getting Bradley back into
form, but I would still expect him to see 8-10 starts at the major-league
level before the year is out—and the strikeouts should be there.

**15)[James
Paxton](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=68467),
LHP, Seattle Mariners (Previous Rank: 13)  
16) [Corey
Hart](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=31370),
1B/OF, Seattle Mariners (Previous Rank: 21)  
17) [Alex
Meyer](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=70760),
RHP, [Minnesota
Twins](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=MIN)
(Previous Rank: 15)  
18) [Kris
Bryant](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=68520),
3B/OF, Chicago Cubs (Previous Rank: 12)**  
As I mentioned earlier, it was nothing on the field this week that dropped
Bryant in the ranks this week, it was simply a quote from Team President Theo
Epstein. The exact words were fantasy owners worst fears: “I don't foresee a
scenario where he [Bryant] would be up this year.” Of course, a quote is just
a quote, which is why he’s down the list and not off it. Bryant continues to
hit bombs everywhere, even though his Triple-A line is extremely
exaggerated—in his first four games at the level, he had a .188/.188/.750
line. This is what happens when every ball you hit goes 500 feet and every
ball you don’t goes into the catcher’s mitt.

**19)[Jeremy
Hellickson](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=46155),
RHP, [Tampa Bay
Rays](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=TBA)
(Previous Rank: 17)  
20) [Josh
Reddick](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=56609),
OF, [Oakland
Athletics](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/team_audit.php?team=OAK)
(Previous Rank: 23)**  
The plan is still for Reddick to be back in Oakland on Tuesday, and fantasy
owners are holding out for a better second act in his 2014 season. The talent
is still there for 12-14 homers and a .250 average the rest of the way, so he
could be a shrewd acquisition right now.

**21)[Marco
Gonzales](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=68759),
LHP, St Louis Cardinals (Previous Rank: NR)**  
And Gonzales wins the prize this week for most surprising call up of the week.
With [Michael
Wacha](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=70641)
and [Jaime
Garcia](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/player_search.php?search_name=Jaime+Garcia)
hitting the DL with shoulder maladies, Gonzales and his 38 2/3 innings in the
high minors gets the call to take a spot. In a great coincidence, he gets to
make his major league debut very close to his hometown—which is undoubtedly
exciting for him. However, with his hometown being Fort Collins, Colorado,
that does mean he’ll be toeing the rubber at Coors. With that said, in deep
leagues, I wouldn’t avoid using him in this matchup despite its level of
difficulty. The combination of the element of surprise and a true out pitch in
his changeup, Gonzales could surprise here. And given the nature of the recent
rotation injuries, along with [Joe
Kelly](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/player_search.php?search_name=Joe+Kelly)’s
not-really-a-starter-ness, Gonzales has a better shot than you might think to
stick.

**22)[Dylan
Bundy](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=70753),
RHP, Baltimore Orioles (Previous Rank: 22)**  
If only there weren’t six pitchers in front of him right now. Even so, he’ll
see the majors this season—it just may be as a reliever.

**23) Jaime Garcia, LHP, St Louis Cardinals (Previous Rank: NR)**  
Sad shoulder, sad Bret.

**24)[Chris
Taylor](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=100496),
SS, Seattle Mariners (Previous Rank: 19)**  
With [Brad
Miller](http://web.archive.org/web/20171003231348/http://www.baseballprospectus.com/player_search.php?search_name=Brad+Miller)
finally playing like a major leaguer again, Taylor no longer appears to be on
the cusp of getting a chance in Seattle. However, he could surface somewhere
in the second half if the Mariners decide to part with him for an upgrade in
the rotation. Cough, Mets, Cough.
