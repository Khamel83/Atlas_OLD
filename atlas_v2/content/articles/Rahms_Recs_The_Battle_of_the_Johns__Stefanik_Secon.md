Democrats are also starting to confront something uncomfortable: Those ads highlighting Harris’s past support for taxpayer-funded transgender prison surgeries? They were a killer. That ’04 **Kerry** race has been at the top of my mind since Tuesday. After Kerry’s loss, Dems were shell-shocked. That race was supposed to be retribution for the stolen election of 2000, and the Iraq fiasco. But Dems lost after being tarred as the party of elites, unpatriotic softies, out-of-touch Hollywood puppets. The loss was so bad and so total that it felt existential. Democrats and urban liberals blamed red state voters as rubes for voting against their interests ( *What’s The Matter With Kansas?* ). They leaned into a new media ecosystem (the blogosphere!). D.N.C. chairman **Howard** **Dean** launched a “50-state strategy” to figure out how to connect with Bubbas in pickup trucks. Two of the putative 2008 presidential hopefuls were Southern white dudes, **John** **Edwards** and **Mark Warner** .     But the path back to power was in no way predictable. **George W. Bush** stumbled early into his second term, with a civil war exploding in Iraq and Hurricane Katrina exposing his ineptitude. The guy who eventually became the next Democratic president, **Barack Hussein Obama** , was very much not a Southern white dude and wasn’t interested in the party’s pecking order or *waiting his turn* . But before that, there were the midterms of 2006, which resulted in sweeping Democratic gains up and down the ballot and a takeover of Congress. The 2026 midterms have to be the next target for despondent Democrats, even if voters out there in the country aren’t very excited about what the party is selling.     Rahm, who helped engineer the party’s comeback in 2006 as head of the Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee, recruited candidates who didn’t fit neatly into the usual Jell-O mold. ​“I remember distinctly being told when I was reaching out to candidates that *these aren’t real Democrats* ,” he told me Monday. “But that was the whole premise! If you are going to flip a district, you have to recruit people who are going to make a cultural connection in that district. The goal wasn’t to make you feel comfortable in Washington or the Upper West Side or Beverly Hills. It’s about winning, you dummies.” | | |  | | --- | | **Punching Left** | | | Democrats, Rahm continued, need to put aside purity tests and find candidates who aren’t afraid to piss off the language police and progressive scolds that seem to spook so many operatives in the party. Emanuel sounded exasperated when he recalled how progressives savaged Biden after he used the term “illegal alien” in his State of the Union address earlier this year, a comment he was forced to apologize for. “You are illegally coming across the border, and you are an immigrant,” said Emanuel. “I mean, come on.”     Emanuel said that Democrats need to remind voters that they are the party of the little guy, standing up to big banks and billionaires. He also put a blunter edge on a sentiment I’d heard a lot from pragmatic strategists in recent years: That Democrats can only win when they start *punching left* and pushing back against the orthodoxies that have spread from elite campuses into party committees and big-city newsrooms. Grad school concepts about identity, gender, race, or crime that are either unpopular, inscrutable, or far removed from people’s daily lives.     That’s one reason certain Senate candidates, including members of the ’06 class such as **Jon** **Tester** and **Sherrod** **Brown** , outran Harris in red states, he said. And it explains why winning candidates **Tammy** **Baldwin** , **Ruben** **Gallego** , and **Jacky** **Rosen** have white-knuckled their way to victory. They didn’t let their races get nationalized, and they worked overtime to talk less about Trump and the culture wars, and more about the kitchen table. “We don’t understand that working-class people are more than just the summation of their wallet,” Emanuel said. “They have kids that go to school. They care about public safety. Like, we are telling people crime is going down, but they go to CVS and everything is locked up and no one is working there? That doesn’t compute, I’m sorry. Save your academic presentations for the Aspen Ideas Festival.”     Punching left, of course, is more easily said than done, as Congressman **Seth Moulton** learned over the weekend when he spoke to *The* *New York Times* with unusual honesty about trans athletes playing in women’s and girls sports. “I have two little girls, I don’t want them getting run over on a playing field by a male or formerly male athlete, but as a Democrat I’m supposed to be afraid to say that.” One of Moulton’s staffers resigned in protest, and he was condemned by the head of the Massachusetts Democratic Party. At the same time, in polls, large majorities of the country agree with Moulton’s take.     There’s no simple blueprint here. The only consensus I’ve been able to pick up on from Democrats, however vague, is that the party needs to get back to listening to voters rather than talking at them. Congresswoman **Marie Gluesenkamp Perez** , a flannel-wearing Democrat who won reelection last week in a southwestern Washington district that also voted for Trump, has been doing the media rounds the last few days explaining how she pulled out her race while other Democrats stumbled in what was a terrible political environment. But when I talked to her Monday, even she acknowledged there were limits to how much national Democrats could learn from her win. “I don’t want to be a poster child or a cardboard cutout,” Gluesenkamp Perez told me. “If there is a model here, it’s just that you have to fight for where you come from, to show up and do the work. That has to be the motivator, and it’s how you break the ideological map.” | | | |  | | --- | |  | | | |  | | --- | | FOUR STORIES WE’RE TALKING ABOUT | | |  |  |  |  |  | | --- | --- | --- | --- |
