The ideal way to travel from North America to South Africa is on Delta’s daily
non-stop flights between Atlanta (ATL) and Johannesburg (JNB) and South
African Airways’ non-stop/direct flights between New York (JFK) and JNB and
Washington-Dulles (IAD) and JNB.

If you have ever tried to use Delta SkyMiles for the ATL – JNB route, you know
that you probably have a better chance of traveling to the moon with miles
than of finding award availability on said flights, at least at the saver
level. The same applies for availability on Delta’s partners. It is not as
difficult to find award availability on South African Airways IAD – JNB and
JFK – JNB flights, but still not easy by any means. As a result, most people
traveling to South Africa from North America end up having to make connections
in Europe and/or the Middle East.

**Award Travel Options to South Africa**

Since both airlines are Star Alliance members, United miles can be used for
South African Airlines IAD – JNB and JFK – JNB flights. Furthermore, United
allows travel to South Africa via any European, Middle-Eastern or African
city.

American Airlines does not partner with Delta or SAA, so if you want to use
American Airlines AAdvantage miles to travel to South Africa from North
America, your only options, pursuant to their award rules, are via London on
BA (fuel surcharges) or via Doha on Qatar Airways (inconsistent availability).

US Airways and SAA are partners, [so you can use your US Air Dividend Miles to
travel on SAA’s
flights](http://web.archive.org/web/20170912064321/http://www.frugaltravelguy.com/2014/06/using-
us-airways-miles-to-book-awards-on-their-former-star-alliance-partners.html).
You can also use US Air miles for travel to South Africa with their Oneworld
partners via any European or Middle-Eastern city.

**The “Unconventional” Way**

No matter which rewards currency you are trying to use, finding ideal award
availability to South Africa via Europe or the Middle East is a complicated
task. However, there is one often overlooked way to travel to South Africa
that works with numerous rewards currencies: via São Paulo, Brazil.

SAA has one daily flight between São Paulo International (GRU) and JNB and
offers a second flight between these cities four times a week (for a total of
11 weekly flights). Best of all, award availability for these flights is
generally excellent and from JNB you can continue to numerous other African
destinations served by SAA.

If you are using United miles, it should not be difficult to find availability
from GRU to the U.S. with Star Alliance partners Avianca and Copa via Bogotá
and Panama City, respectively. United also flies itself from GRU to Chicago,
Houston, Newark and Washington-Dulles, although availability for these flights
might be a little harder to find.

The key to finding award availability from South Africa to North America via
GRU is to search segment by segment, as the United website will rarely offer
up this routing as an option, even when there is award availability.

**Combined Rewards Currencies Alternatives**

While you cannot technically use AA miles for travel on a single award between
South Africa and North America, you can use a combination of United miles for
the JNB – GRU segment (45k in business class) and then AA miles for the GRU –
North America segment (50k in business class).

In addition to AA’s numerous flights from GRU, you can also use AA miles for
travel to the U.S. with oneworld partner TAM airlines.

Therefore, if combining United and AA miles, the total rate for a business
class award would be 95k miles per person. Considering that an exclusively-
United business class award between North America and South Africa would
require 80k miles per person, and an exclusively-AA business class award
between North America and South Africa would require 75k miles per person,
using a combined 95k miles is not a bad deal, especially if there are no other
options available.

Please note that the rates for a combined award in economy class are horrible
(50-60k miles per person), so this should not be considered as an option.
Also, it may be possible to combine United miles with BA Avios and US Air
miles or Aeroplan (Air Canada) miles and AA miles, US Air miles or BA Avios at
similar rates.

It is also worth noting that Aeroplan (American Express and Starwood Preferred
Guest partners) does not impose fuel surcharges on SAA, Avianca, Copa or
United flights, so these are all particularly great alternatives on which to
use Aeroplan miles. Keep in mind, though, that in order to be able to book a
one-way award with Aeroplan, the flight must originate or end in North
America. Therefore, you cannot use Aeroplan miles for only the GRU – JNB
segment.

**Bottom Line**

The next time you need to book awards to South Africa, if the non-stop/direct
flights are not available, even if there is availability via Europe or the
Middle East, remember to search for availability via São Paulo as that might
prove to be the best or only option.
