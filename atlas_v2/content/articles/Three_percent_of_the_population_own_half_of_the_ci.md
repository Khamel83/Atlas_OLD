Stephen Paddock stockpiled an
[arsenal](http://web.archive.org/web/20250613051200/https://qz.com/1092545/las-
vegas-shooting-heres-what-we-know-about-stephen-paddocks-weapons-arsenal) of
weapons before committing the deadliest mass shooting in modern US history in
Las Vegas. The bloodshed and the scope of Paddock’s weaponry, extreme as it
may seem, have returned [gun control
](http://web.archive.org/web/20250613051200/https://qz.com/1095742/las-vegas-
shooting-the-nra-is-calling-on-the-us-government-to-review-the-legality-of-
bump-stocks)to the forefront of the US national debate.

Just how common is it for Americans to own multiple guns? And who owns them?

Here is a look at the distribution of gun ownership in America:

## **Do Americans own a lot of guns?**

In both absolute and relative terms, the answer is yes. The US tops the list
of countries with the most guns, owning about half the world’s guns while
making up only 5% of the world population.

In relative terms, the US has the highest number of guns per capita. There
were an estimated 89 to 100 guns for every 100 Americans in 2013–around [one
firearm per
person](http://web.archive.org/web/20250613051200/http://www.pewresearch.org/fact-
tank/2013/06/04/a-minority-of-americans-own-guns-but-just-how-many-is-
unclear/).

The country with [the second highest
number](http://web.archive.org/web/20250613051200/http://www.gunpolicy.org/firearms/region/united-
states) per capita is Serbia, at 75.6 guns per 100 people.

## **How many people own guns in America?**

Answering this requires a more nuanced look at the gun ownership. Only a
quarter of Americans own guns, according to numbers from [General Social
Survey and
Gallup](http://web.archive.org/web/20250613051200/http://www.pewresearch.org/fact-
tank/2013/06/04/a-minority-of-americans-own-guns-but-just-how-many-is-
unclear/) in 2013. This means that many American gun owners have more than one
gun.

## **How many guns does an average American gun owner possess?**

The average American gun owner owns three guns, according to [a 2015
survey](http://web.archive.org/web/20250613051200/https://www.theguardian.com/us-
news/2016/sep/19/us-gun-ownership-survey) conducted by Harvard and
Northwestern University. More than a half of them own just one or two, whereas
14% of them–7.7 million or 3% of the US population–own anywhere between eight
to 140 guns. This 3% of the population owns half of the civilian guns in the
US. The police [found 47 guns
](http://web.archive.org/web/20250613051200/http://thehill.com/homenews/news/353772-authorities-
recover-47-firearms-in-connection-with-las-vegas-shooter)associated with
Paddock in three different locations two days after the massacre, which puts
him among the 3%.

A note of caution: Gun ownership surveys always contain a degree of
inaccuracy. As long as Americans can still legally purchase guns in many
states without a permit or license, there doesn’t seem to be a systematic
method of tracking the population of US gun owners.
