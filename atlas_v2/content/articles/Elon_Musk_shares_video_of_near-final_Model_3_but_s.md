Tesla CEO Elon Musk today shared the first video of what he calls a “release candidate” version of Tesla’s upcoming Model 3 electric car. We’ve seen Model 3 prototypes [before](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/http://www.theverge.com/2017/3/10/14885358/tesla-model-3-prototype-video-spacex), and this five-second clip doesn’t really show us anything new — but Musk enjoys feeding the hype train.

He also noted that calling the new car the “Model 3” was [sowing confusion](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845278449811939328) among customers about whether it was the “next version” of Tesla’s car, like Apple going from the iPhone 2 to iPhone 3 — “not true,” Musk said.

He [called himself](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845283534948392960) “a dumb idiot” for naming it Model 3, saying he “didn’t realize at the time that it would cause confusion.” Instead, he says customers should think of the Model 3 as similar to the BMW 3 Series or Audi A4, while the Model S is [like a BMW 5 or 6 Series](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845282298291339267) — different cars but sold side by side. Musk noted that he will [keep driving](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845291536480813056) the Model S even after the Model 3 arrives.

In an anecdote we’ve known for a while, Musk [reminded followers](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845280754871418880) that the Model 3 was originally going to be named the Model E (“for obvious dumb humor reasons” — meaning the cars would be Models S, E, and X), but a Ford trademark lawsuit prevented that from happening. Next up, naturally, [is Model Y](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845281547368349696). S3XY.

As part of his tweetstorm this morning — which occurred [during a flight](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845294161125269505) to Cape Canaveral for a SpaceX rocket launch — Musk [replied](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/with_replies) to a number of current and potential Tesla customers about what’s coming from the company in the coming months. Among other things, he said:

* The first Model 3 cars will [all be rear-wheel drive](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845284390397665280), much like the Model S was, to make production a bit simpler. All-wheel drive / dual-motor versions will come [six to nine months](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845287981652963328) after launch.
* [Performance versions](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845293157977767937) of the Model 3 will come “probably a year from now,” though the Model S will remain faster because it has room for a larger battery.
* [“More cowbell”](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845287688882184192) will be coming in the Tesla 8.1 software update, which is expected next week. It’s a reference to a Will Farrell *Saturday Night Live* skit of which Musk is a fan. Previous versions of Tesla’s vehicle operating system have included Easter eggs [referencing cowbell](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/http://www.theverge.com/2016/4/11/11409638/tesla-autopilot-rainbow-road-super-mario-kart).
* The Model 3 [will have a single screen](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845285846936825856) combining instrument cluster and infotainment information in one place. Hinting at possible increased autonomy, Musk asked how often riders look at the instrument panel in a taxi.

* The Model S will [remain the fastest Tesla](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845282934546350080) until the next-generation Roadster is released — though that car is “a few years away.”
* A Tesla [semi-truck](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/http://www.theverge.com/2016/8/3/12371504/tesla-semi-truck-mini-bus-reveal-2017-musk) [will beat sports cars](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845290493130919936) from zero to 60 mph.
* Tesla and SolarCity will [begin taking orders](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/845293825715154945) for its [“solar roof” product](https://web.archive.org/web/20241208014707/http://www.theverge.com/2016/10/28/13463236/tesla-solar-roof-battery-new-elon-musk) next month.
