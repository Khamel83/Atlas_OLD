Most poor kids in America never make it to the middle class

In a perfect meritocracy, where people who work equivalently hard are just as likely to get ahead, our parents’ income should have no impact on our own. 80th of the poorest fifth of kids should escape that share of the income distribution of adults. 20 percent should make it to the top fifth.

So do they? Not even close. A mere 4 percent of kids born into the bottom quintile make it into the top one. Click “Know More” to read an important new report by Pew Charitable Trusts’s Economic Mobility Project on why that’s still the case.
