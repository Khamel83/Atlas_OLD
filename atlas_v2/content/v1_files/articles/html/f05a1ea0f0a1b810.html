Seeing the World in a Weekend – Cairo, Egypt [EXPIRED]

December 10, 2014
Christine Krzyszton

(Offers on this page are now expired and are no longer available.)
There’s obviously a debate as to whether it’s safe to travel to Egypt right now, but from all the reports we read prior to our trip, violence had subsided and the country is encouraging tourists to return. While conditions could change overnight, we decided to go ahead with our Thanksgiving trip to Cairo and that decision turned out to be the right one. As two women, we didn’t know exactly what to expect — we had been warned of harassment, scams, and the dangers of the city. Our experience was nothing like the warnings and our weekend turned out to be one of our favorites.

Our priorities were to experience the Pyramids, visit one of the oldest Bazaars and see the site of the Citadel complex. Selecting a hotel within the city of Cairo allowed us to easily secure a taxi to the Pyramids and remain close to the attractions within the city. Although our hotel choice, The Conrad, was not within walking distance of any attractions, it was a gem of a hotel and we were treated like royalty. In addition, there was a club lounge offering a free happy hour with hors d’oeuvres and an elaborate breakfast buffet was included. The hotel met all our needs and the service was over the top!

The Pyramids

Seeing the Pyramids on camelback was certainly a dream come true. As an added bonus, we nearly had the place to ourselves due to tourism at an all-time low in Cairo. Hiring a driver to get to the site was easy and affordable. To have a driver take us to the Pyramids, wait for us for hours, then return us to our hotel was a total of $60. You could get there by regular taxi for less. We hired camels with a tour guide for $20 per person and spent the afternoon exploring the area. Riding a camel is probably the roughest way to go but it’s traditional, exotic and the most fun by far. Our experience was better than we could have imagined as we ventured out to view the panoramic view of all six Pyramids, circled around the major landmarks and a came in for a close up of the Sphinx. It was a special day.

The Old City and the Citadel

Since our time was limited, we selected sites we truly wanted to see (the others will have to wait until our next visit). The Al Qalaa Citadel was one of the important sites on our list. The site includes museums, mosques, a palace and the most incredible panoramic views of the city — on a clear day you can even see the Pyramids. The Mohammed Ali Mosque was so dynamic it was worth the visit to the Citadel site alone. Although we didn’t spend time in the Old City, we did spend a lot of time driving through it, as traffic does not allow otherwise. The area is truly the heart of Cairo and the street action is exhilarating. Exploring its historic streets and buildings is near the top of our list when we return.

The Bazaar

Another attraction on our short list was the Khan El-Khalili Bazaar. Hawking at the Bazaar was far worse than Morocco, Istanbul or similar markets we have visited.  It was impossible to even glance at an item without an exhaustive sales pitch. Although there were many interesting items, we grew weary of the shopping experience very quickly and retreated to an ancient looking café. We sipped on mint tea and caught a refreshing break. The café was so amazing that my friend commented that being there was if we were watching an old movie unfold around us. I think it was this moment that helped us understand the intense mood of this city, so exotic and complex, so chaotic and emotionally challenging, yet somehow pulling us in and asking us to get to know it better. We topped our morning off with a traditional lunch of falafel, fresh tomato salad, tahini and Arabic bread purchased from the baker. Overlooking the square and the chaos that is Cairo, we were thankful to be sitting there watching it all transpire.

Planning a trip to the Pyramids is quite simple and probably more affordable than you might expect. Book flights to Cairo with miles or select one of the cheap fares available right now, secure a hotel just about anywhere in the city area with hotel points, take a taxi to the site and hire a camel, horse or carriage, then sit back and savor the moment. There is no better time than now to go.
