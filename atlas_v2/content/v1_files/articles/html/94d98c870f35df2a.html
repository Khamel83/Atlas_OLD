Does Plenti Offer Plenty of Rewards? [EXPIRED]

June 9, 2015
Joe Cortez

(Offers on this page are now expired and are no longer available.)

No matter where we turn, the opportunity to earn points and miles seems endless. To make things even more convoluted, it seems like new points programs launch weekly, each vying for our attention (and, more importantly, our dollars) while touting themselves as the superior program.

The latest new rewards program is Plenti, offering points for purchases at many common retailers and service providers. The program is operated by American Express, the same company that brings you the revamped Premier Rewards Gold Card from American Express, and the (soon to be refurbished) Starwood Preferred Guest Credit Card from American Express.

Once frugal travelers sign up for free on the Plenti website, they can utilize the card to earn Plenti points at some of their favorite retailers, such as Rite Aid and Macy’s. In addition, the program also offer points for monthly spending at service providers as well, like AT&T and Nationwide Insurance. The points hold an approximate value of one cent per point.

On the surface, Plenti looks like a great way to earn a bonus points, but does Plenti really offer a strong return for the frugal traveler? The answer may surprise you.

One of the big upsides about Plenti is that it is a value-added and stackable reward for recurring purchases, such as AT&T and Nationwide Insurance. This allows frugal travelers to double-dip on their purchases, earning Plenti points on top of their regular points earnings. In addition, it may be possible to stack Plenti points with portal shopping points, for an even bigger return. Your experience may vary.

While the partners are currently limited, there are ways that frugal travelers can earn points on travel expenses. Currently, travelers can earn points on rental car reservations with Alamo, Enterprise, or National Car Rental and have their status rewards forward along with their reservation. Complimenting that, travelers can also earn points for fuel purchases at Exxon and Mobil.

While Plenti offers certain rewards, the program has some major shortcomings. In addition to the small number of partnerships, earning opportunities at certain partners are also limited. For example, those using Plenti at Macy’s earn one point per $10 in spending when not using a Macy’s co-branded American Express credit card. In comparison, that same individual can earn two United MileagePlus miles when utilizing the MileagePlus Shopping portal. In this example, utilizing the shopping portal offers a better return for the spend.

So what can frugal travelers do with all those Plenti points? So far, the redemption methods are very narrow. Plenti points can be used for direct savings at Macy’s and Rite Aid, with a minimum of 200 points per transaction. Points can also be used at at Exxon or Mobil stations with stipulations. According to the frequently asked questions on the Plenti website, users may be forced to use their entire balance in one transaction, up to 2,500 points. Currently, points cannot be used towards rental cars at Alamo, Enterprise or National Car Rental.

At the end of the day, Does Plenti offer plenty of points? Frankly, there are plenty of better ways to earn points that travelers can redeem elsewhere. Because I use AT&T phone service and switch between Alamo and National Car Rental, I will personally use Plenti as a secondary program to my primary points earning methods. However, until Plenti offers more points redemption opportunities or more partnerships, by no means will this become my primary rewards program.
