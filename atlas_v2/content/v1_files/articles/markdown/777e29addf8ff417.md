Montreal is a great alternative to a European vacation. You’ll experience the
same ambiance of a French city without the jetlag. And instead of paying a
premium for the euro, you’ll enjoy the benefit of the strong U.S. dollar
currently sitting at nearly $1.32 to the Canadian dollar.

**_Getting There_**

Montreal has brutal winters and crowded summers, so utilize late spring and
early fall for the best combination of comfort and cost.

**Using miles/points or purchasing outright:** Flights to Montreal from the
West Coast, Midwest, and Northeast are currently running $450, $300 and $250,
respectively. Round trip award tickets for late March/early April for are as
low as 25,000 miles from ORD/LAX and 20,000 miles (UA) from NYC.

Try pricing one-way fares and one-way award tickets as well; combining the two
can result in the best of both options. For instance, if you can find a low-
level 10K award ticket one-way and a fare less than half the round trip price
for the reverse direction, combine the two for the best value. If you can
extend your stay, an excellent redemption would be the [SPG Nights and
Flights](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://www.frugaltravelguy.com/2015/08/2-spg-
cards-weeklong-vacation.html) option.

**Try a train/plane combination:** If you have a day to spare, consider flying
to New York, purchasing a one-way train ticket to Montreal for $68 ($58 for
age 62+) and enjoy [a scenic day on the
rails](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://www.seat61.com/Canada.htm)
before your weekend in Montreal. Toronto is another option for taking the
train, [5.5 hours and $44 on VIA
Rail](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://www.viarail.ca/en/explore-
our-destinations/trains/ontario-and-quebec/toronto-montreal). You can build
the trip with a one-way flight to New York/Toronto, a train ticket on Amtrak
or VIA Rail and a one-way airline ticket from Montreal to your home airport,
and you’d actually spend the same amount as a round trip plane ticket
depending on the dates.

**_The Hotel_**

**Using hotel loyalty points:** This is one city where the points required for
hotel nights seem out-of-sync in some cases, so you’ll want to do your
research carefully. For instance, the W, a Starwood property, runs 12,000
points or $364. However, the Westin, also a Starwood property, runs the same
12,000 points but for only $112! A club-level room with lounge access at the
Intercontinental, an IHG property, costs 45,000 IHG points but can be
purchased for $150. Lounge access alone at an Intercontinental is usually
$90/night. Bottom line, using loyalty points may or may not be the best value,
so research is a must. Rates quoted here are for March/early April 2016.

**Paying for the hotel outright:** With the favorable exchange rate for U.S.
dollars, hotels can be a bargain in Montreal. The exchange translates into a
hotel room priced at $132 CAD converting to around $100 USD. There hasn’t been
a better time in a long time to visit Canada. You can stay at premium hotels
like the Intercontinental for $107, the Hyatt Regency for $122 and the Westin
for $112. With value this good, consider going for luxury.

**_Activities in Montreal_**

**The gem of Montreal:** No matter your religion, you’re sure to appreciate
the incredible [Notre Dame
Basilica](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://www.basiliquenddm.org/en/events/)
in Montreal. Take the tour of this magnificent structure, learn about its
history, attend a concert or opt for the spectacular light show. I was
fortunate to attend mass on Easter Sunday and even though it was spoken in
French and I am not Catholic, it was truly enjoyable.

**Old Montreal:** Yes, it’s a little touristy but no more than Paris is
touristy. You’ll see the souvenir shops, but you can also take in amazing
architecture, shop a little, walk along the cobblestone streets then rest at
an outdoor café for coffee and some people watching.

**_Montreal Food Scene_**

**Poutine, maple syrup, and crepes** : If you want the real French Canadian
food experience, you’ll have to try all of these food options. Poutine is a
simple dish: French fries, cheese curds and gravy. But [Resto La
Banquise](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://labanquise.com/)
goes above and beyond and serves up this unique dish [30 different
ways](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://labanquise.com/en/poutine-
menu.php), so you’re sure to find one you like. For everything maple syrup,
stop by [Maple
Delights](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://www.mapledelights.com/maple-
syrup/contact-us/bistros-location.aspx), located in the heart of Old Montreal.
They serve everything from their famous maple gelato to maple taffy (made by
throwing a hot maple syrup concoction on a bed of crushed ice). Whether you
like sweet crepes or prefer savory ones like I do, you’ll find the best
creperies in Montreal. For a list with plenty of customer reviews, [start
here](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://www.yelp.com/search?find_desc=Best+Crepes&find_loc=Montr%C3%A9al%2C+QC).

**Eating Polish in a sea of French Restaurants** : One of my favorite fun
restaurants in Old Montreal is the [Stash
Cafe](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://www.yelp.com/biz/stash-
caf%C3%A9-montreal-2). Start with their great traditional Polish food, add the
lively music of a piano player and feel the warmth of the blazing fireplace —
you’ll feel like you’re in the heart of Poland!

**_Montreal Transportation_**

**From the airport to your hotel** : A taxi will set you back as much as $50,
but the [747 Airport
Express](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://www.stm.info/en/info/networks/bus/shuttle/more-
about-747-aeroport-p-e-trudeau-centre-ville-shuttle) bus is less than $8 USD
and has free Wi-Fi. The ticket is available at the airport and is then good
for 24 hours on the entire bus/metro system. You can also purchase a one-way
747 Airport Express bus pass with cash/credit card at stations throughout
Montreal for your return trip to the airport. The bus makes stops [convenient
to most downtown
hotels](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://www.stm.info/sites/default/files/planibus/en/747.pdf).

**Metro your way around the city:** Montreal has an efficient and [expansive
metro
system](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://www.stm.info/en), and
a weekend pass runs around $10 USD.

**_Montreal Wildcard_**

Are you ready for [zip lining in urban
Montreal](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://mtlzipline.com/en/)?
For an amazing view of the city and Bonsecours Island, try the short zip
lining experience from the [Old Port of
Montreal](http://web.archive.org/web/20151112231610/http://mtlzipline.com/en/find-
us/). For around $15, you can fly like a bird and savor a day or nighttime
view.

For a European vacation close to home that won’t cost a fortune, Montreal is
the perfect choice.
