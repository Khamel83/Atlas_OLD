Earlier this year, Astro Teller, a ponytailed scientist and science fiction
writer, gave a TED Talk.

It was a first for Teller, but not for X, or Google X, as the research lab he
runs used to be known. The lab has been a fixture on the conference circuit
for years. In 2011, Sebastian Thrun, X’s founder, took the TED stage and
predicted that driverless cars would put an end to traffic fatalities. In
2013, Sergey Brin, Google’s co-founder, showed up wearing X’s wearable
computer, Google Glass, and argued that face-mounted devices were a natural
successor to the smartphone. In 2015, Chris Urmson, the technical lead of X’s
autonomous vehicle program, argued that driverless cars should operate with no
human oversight at all. By February 2016 it was Teller’s turn.
