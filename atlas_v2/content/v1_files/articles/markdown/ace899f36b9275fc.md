As 2019 wound down, well-known press figures sounded alarms about the “erosion
of truth.” MSNBC’s Chuck Todd spoke to [our own Peter
Wade](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.rollingstone.com/politics/politics-
news/how-disinformation-spreads-according-to-chuck-todd-interview-929912/)
about the “epidemic” of “disinformation.” _Washington Post_ editor Marty Baron
and _New York Times_ editor Dean Baquet joined Todd [on _Meet The
Press_](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.nbcnews.com/meet-
the-press/searching-truth-era-alternative-facts-disinformation-age-
trump-n1108246) to talk about how the Internet and [Donald
Trump](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.rollingstone.com/t/donald-
trump/) and Russia are all combining to create an era in which “crazy
conspiracy theories” and “absolute falsehoods and lies” can proliferate.

This isn’t a new idea. Since 2016 especially, a wide array of American
politicians and think-tanks have been hammering the idea that the free media
of the “post-Cold War democratic order” is under outside threat. Russia,
China, Venezuela, Syria and “other authoritarian states” are said to be
undermining our most sacred values. As Freedom House [put
it](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://freedomhouse.org/sites/default/files/June2017_FH_Report_Breaking_Down_Democracy.pdf)
after Trump’s election, they seek a universe where:

“…facts are irrelevant, international treaties are obsolete, and sovereignty
is a matter of power rather than law.”

Under this interpretation, the “war on facts” in America is a foreign-inspired
problem, aided by Trump Republicans, the solution to which is redoubled faith
in traditional media outlets like the ones represented on _Meet the Press._

You’d have to smoke a _lot_ of crack to look back at 2019 and think this was
true. By almost any measure this was one of the worst years in the history of
commercial news media, performance-wise, and the problems were far from
exclusive to the political right.

This was a year in which chickens came home to roost in the fully divided
landscape of the corporate press. Screw-ups became routine, as news agencies
targeting all types of audiences began to realize their demographics would not
punish them for getting things wrong, so long as underlying messages were
pleasing. Corrections went out of vogue for the same reason.

Some of the worst trends in media from last year:

**1\. TALK-AND-TWEET**

Fox News was once the most dependable home of fact-bending shenanigans on TV,
with the birther controversy a particular low point. The network’s new trick
is offshoring factual offenses to Viewer-in-Chief Donald Trump. The usual
cycle involves a segment on _Fox and Friends_ that indulges in lurid
speculations (especially about minorities or immigrants), followed by a Trump
tweet that hits the same themes but ups the ante factually and is also
seen/read by a significantly larger audience. A classic example was a segment
about Rep. Elijah Cummings in which “Republican strategist” Kimberly Klacik
showed images purporting to be of the Baltimore district Cummings represents.
Klacik said “a lot of people said he hasn’t even been there for a while.”

Erik Wemple of the _Washington
Post_[described](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/2019/07/29/even-
saturday-morning-fox-news-cannot-hide/) what happened next: Trump ripped
Cummings [in a
tweet](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://twitter.com/realDonaldTrump/status/1155073965880172544?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw%7Ctwcamp%5Etweetembed%7Ctwterm%5E1155073965880172544&ref_url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.washingtonpost.com%2Fopinions%2F2019%2F07%2F29%2Feven-
saturday-morning-fox-news-cannot-hide%2F), saying, “if he spent more time in
Baltimore, maybe he could clean up this very dangerous & filthy place.”
Meanwhile, no one ever had to source where the claim about Cummings, who
passed away in October, and his lack of visits really came from.

In the age of Trump, this tends to be how misinformation works on the
Republican side. The president, a one-person news organization, sees bits and
pieces of news on Fox, adds a personal spin, and sends it out either in
speeches or on social media. Before long, the “squad” is foreign, a hurricane
is bearing down on Alabama, and Democrats want “virtual
[immunity](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.politifact.com/truth-
o-meter/statements/2019/aug/02/donald-trump/trumps-false-charge-democrats-
want-virtual-immunit/)” for immigrant murderers.

**2\. BOMBSHELLS**

Errors have been compounded in recent years by the practice of announcing to
readers the extreme importance of stories as they’re released. As a result,
countless tales pitched as “bombshells” – often pegged to anonymous sources
promising to produce proof later on – have turned into super-errors in
hindsight.

Reports by Special Counsel Robert Mueller and Justice Inspector General
Michael Horowitz exploded claims that Trump lawyer Michael Cohen had [met with
Russian hackers in
Prague](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.mcclatchydc.com/news/investigations/article229424084.html),
that [probable cause
existed](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-
security/fbi-obtained-fisa-warrant-to-monitor-former-trump-adviser-carter-
page/2017/04/11/620192ea-1e0e-11e7-ad74-3a742a6e93a7_story.html) to believe
Trump aide Carter Page was a foreign agent, that Russia and Trump were
communicating via a [secret Internet
server](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://time.com/5592739/donald-
trump-petr-aven-alfa-bank/), that evidence existed of Russian efforts to
[sexually blackmail
Trump](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.nytimes.com/2017/01/11/world/europe/donald-
trump-russia.html), that Russians had vetoed [Mitt Romney as Secretary of
State](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://thehill.com/homenews/administration/376746-russia-
says-it-stopped-mitt-romney-from-becoming-secretary-of-state), and many
others.

Other bombshells, like that Trump had [directed Cohen to
lie](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://theintercept.com/2019/01/20/beyond-
buzzfeed-the-10-worst-most-embarrassing-u-s-media-failures-on-the-trumprussia-
story/) to Congress (one of the first big stories of 2019) or that the Trump
campaign had [repeated
contacts](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.nytimes.com/2017/02/14/us/politics/russia-
intelligence-communications-trump.html) with Russian intelligence, died on the
launch pad. Two constant media preoccupations of the past three years – that
Mueller was “[closing
in](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.salon.com/2018/03/05/robert-
mueller-is-closing-in-on-president-trump-heres-proof/)” on proof of a Putin-
Trump conspiracy that would result in Trump’s imminent
[resignation](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.newsweek.com/donald-
trump-resign-2019-family-immunity-1276990) or
[indictment](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.realclearpolitics.com/video/2019/03/05/brennan_predicts_mueller_report_more_indictments_on_friday_i_dont_have_inside_knowledge.html)
– also blew up this year, prompting near-zero reflection in an industry that
secured [record
ratings](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.adweek.com/tvnewser/nov-2019-ratings-
msnbc-delivered-some-of-the-largest-weekday-audiences-in-network-
history/422536/) and banked billions in profits humping these themes.

MSNBC’s Lawrence O’Donnell this summer reported Trump’s business loans had
“Russian co-signers,” saying this “explains every kind word Trump has ever
said about Russia and Putin.” He was taken to task when sourcing proved shaky,
but O’Donnell’s error was a rare show of modern-era ethics, as he [copped to
the
mistake](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://twitter.com/Lawrence/status/1166800943272353792?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw%7Ctwcamp%5Etweetembed%7Ctwterm%5E1166800943272353792&ref_url=https%3A%2F%2Fsharylattkisson.com%2F2019%2F01%2F50-media-
mistakes-in-the-trump-era-the-definitive-list%2F) and apologized. Wemple, the
_Post_ media watchdog, took a significant step this month when he launched a
[multi-part
series](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/2019/12/26/rachel-
maddow-rooted-steele-dossier-be-true-then-it-fell-apart/) that among other
things ripped MSNBC’s Rachel Maddow for having “rooted” for dubious Russia
stories to be true. On the whole, though, mainstream outlets have committed to
a strategy of not yet re-examining the last three years of “bombshells,”
preferring instead to feed audiences streams of [new
ones](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.msnbc.com/the-
beat-with-ari/watch/bombshell-trump-doj-may-indict-fbi-official-who-ran-
russia-probe-68808773795).

**3\. PLAYING FAVORITES**

The 2019 portion of the 2020 presidential campaign cycle was marked by a
crisis of pundit confidence. Old practices like the “[invisible
primary](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invisible_primary)”
were rendered meaningless, thanks to an electorate that no longer flocks to
media-endorsed frontrunners. Nonetheless, outlets spilled enormous quantities
of ink and devoted oceans of airtime hyping establishment candidates whose
“electability” arguments were mostly fictional. The magazine covers for [Beto
O’Rourke](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.vanityfair.com/news/2019/03/beto-
orourke-cover-story) and [Kamala
Harris](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://time.com/5691643/kamala-
harris-2020/) were one thing, but there was real weirdness in the mania over
“momentum” [for Amy
Klobuchar](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.cnn.com/videos/politics/2019/12/23/amy-
klobuchar-iowa-dnt-lah-newday-vpx.cnn), whose recent “Klobucharge” a whole
point or so [up in the
polls](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.realclearpolitics.com/epolls/2020/president/us/2020_democratic_presidential_nomination-6730.html)
recalled some of the sillier stories of the 2016 cycle (i.e.
“[Marcomentum](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.npr.org/2016/02/04/465500505/signs-
of-marco-mentum-for-rubio-in-new-hampshire)”).

Bernie Sanders in particular led a Trotsky-like existence in coverage in 2019,
i.e. he was often all but physically cut out of headlines. When he held a huge
rally in Los Angeles, the _L.A. Times_ headline was about Biden winning the
“[electability
primary](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://twitter.com/FL4Bernie2020/status/1208886222900355073/photo/1?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw%7Ctwcamp%5Etweetembed%7Ctwterm%5E1208886222900355073&ref_url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.truthdig.com%2Farticles%2Fthe-
bernie-blackout-is-just-getting-ridiculous-now%2F).” CNN ran [five
stories](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://twitter.com/ryangrim/status/1189235477506867200?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw%7Ctwcamp%5Etweetembed%7Ctwterm%5E1189235477506867200&ref_url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.commondreams.org%2Fnews%2F2019%2F11%2F05%2Fexamples-
mount-sanders-campaign-accuses-corporate-media-deliberate-attempt-erase) about
a New Hampshire poll showing Sanders in the lead, and none mentioned Sanders
in the headline. These things can be in the eye of the beholder, and a lot of
innocent oversights look intentional when you’ve invested hopes in a campaign,
but the clearest evidence of how the media shaded Sanders coverage (to say
nothing of often-detestable treatment of some other, lesser-polling
candidates) is that he hasn’t yet suffered the backlash that usually comes
with being hyped as a possible nominee. Brace yourself for an avalanche of
insane propaganda if the wrong candidate does well in New Hampshire or Iowa.

**4\. INSIDER TV**

In the Bush years, the sudden explosion of ex-military figures as paid or
regular contributors on cable news was a conspicuous enough phenomenon that it
provoked widespread criticism [from media
watchdogs](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://fair.org/extra/the-
military-industrial-media-complex/). Anti-war voices were scarce in the run-up
to the Iraq war, while stars and bars were everywhere.

A decade-plus later, the craze is ex-intelligence officials. The last few
years have seen an explosion of hires of prominent intelligence figures as TV
talking heads, including many who have been prominent in ongoing news
controversies, like [former FBI deputy director Andrew
McCabe](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/cnn-
hires-former-acting-fbi-chief-andrew-mccabe-drawing-fire-from-trumps-son-
campaign-and-
others/2019/08/23/77ece630-c5b0-11e9-9986-1fb3e4397be4_story.html) (hired this
year), former CIA chiefs [John
Brennan](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.politico.com/magazine/story/2018/02/06/john-
brennan-james-claper-michael-hayden-former-cia-media-216943), and former
Director of National Intelligence James Clapper. Networks, incidentally, have
stopped bothering to tell audiences that their on-air contributors may have
roles in stories they
[are](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.realclearpolitics.com/video/2019/12/16/clapper_comeys_mistake_was_trusting_in_the_procedures_for_fisa_warrants.html)
[commenting
upon](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/justice-
department/ag-barr-expands-controversial-review-origin-russia-
investigation-n1068971). Asha Rangappa, Frank Figliuzzi, and James Gagliano of
the FBI and more than a dozen
[others](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://twitter.com/mtaibbi/status/1205610508134555648?lang=en)
round out an amazing collection of ex-spooks now on the air.

Meanwhile, ex-Trump officials like [Sarah Huckabee
Sanders](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://www.businessinsider.com/19-people-
who-have-worked-at-fox-news-trump-administration-2019-5) and [Corey
Lewandowski](http://web.archive.org/web/20250725181038/https://money.cnn.com/2016/06/23/media/corey-
lewandowski-cnn/) have joined the TV ranks (Fox also has a significant number
of ex-Trump figures in management), enhancing the impression that government
and the broadcast media are merging. As the media becomes more politicized,
the value of traditionally independent media lifers has decreased. If the
primary product on either Fox or MSNBC is political messaging, why not hire
officials directly? They offer the raw product; reporters just get in the way.
Whether the subject is the Russia story or impeachment or the presidential
campaign, broadcast news is being replaced by a 24-hour cycle of insiders, a
show that in 2019 became all but unwatchable.
