#  Wealth inequality explained in charts

Wealth inequality is a real thing that is complex and a result of various
factors. It’s difficult to capture everything in one chart, so Urban Institute
[explained wealth inequality in nine charts
instead](http://web.archive.org/web/20231128101720/http://datatools.urban.org/Features/wealth-
inequality-charts/).

They start with an animated chart that shows growing inequality over the years
(basically a rising spike on the right) and then move into demographic
breakdowns for age and race. Then they go into more detail for possible
explanations for why certain groups lag behind others.

Finishing with a handful of policy recommendations that might shrink wealth
inequality, the piece is a nice progression of overview first to more details
later.
