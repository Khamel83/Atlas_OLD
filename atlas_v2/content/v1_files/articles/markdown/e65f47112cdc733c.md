Currently, when you open the **[Chase Sapphire
Reserve](https://millionmilesecrets.com/go/ChaseSapphireReserve100K201611/)**
card, you’ll earn **100,000 Chase Ultimate Rewards points** after you spend
$4,000 on purchases in the first 3 months from account opening.

You’ll also get:

  * **3X Chase Ultimate Rewards** points on travel & dining
  * **1X Chase Ultimate Rewards** points on all other purchases
  * **$300 annual credit** for travel purchases such as airfare and hotels
  * **$100 statement credit** for [Global Entry](https://millionmilesecrets.com/guides/get-a-same-day-walk-in-appointment-at-these-global-entry-enrollment-centers/)
  * [Priority Pass Select](https://www.prioritypass.com/en/Select) for access to [airport lounges](https://millionmilesecrets.com/guides/are-airport-lounges-worth-it/)
  * No foreign transaction fees
  * [Visa Infinite](https://www.visa.com/infinite/) benefits like $25 food and beverage credit at the [Luxury Hotel Collection](https://www.visaluxuryhotelcollection.com/) and complimentary car rental elite status with Silvercar

Starting **January 12, 2017** , the sign-up bonus will be reduced to **50,000
Chase Ultimate Rewards points** after meeting the same minimum spending
requirement.

However, according to [The Points Guy](https://thepointsguy.com/2017/01/chase-
sapphire-reserve-100k-online-bonus-ending/), you’ll still be able to earn the
higher bonus if you **apply in-branch before March 12, 2017.**

### What If You’re Not Eligible?

**Link:[What to Do If You’re Not Eligible for the Chase Sapphire Reserve
Bonus](https://millionmilesecrets.com/guides/cant-get-chase-sapphire-
reserve/)**

Because the [Chase Sapphire
Reserve](https://millionmilesecrets.com/go/ChaseSapphireReserve100K201611/)
falls under the [Chase “5/24”
rule](https://millionmilesecrets.com/guides/chase-5-24-rule/), [many folks
who’ve been in the miles and points hobby for a while aren’t eligible for the
card](https://millionmilesecrets.com/guides/cant-get-chase-sapphire-reserve/).

That means if you’ve opened ~5+ cards from **any** bank ([except certain
business cards](https://millionmilesecrets.com/guides/best-amex-business-
cards/)) in the past 24 months, it’s unlikely you’ll be [approved for most
Chase cards](https://millionmilesecrets.com/guides/chase-5-24-rule/).

That said, [some folks have had luck being pre-approved for the card in-
branch](https://millionmilesecrets.com/guides/chase-sapphire-reserve-approval-
tips/), even if they’re above 5/24.
