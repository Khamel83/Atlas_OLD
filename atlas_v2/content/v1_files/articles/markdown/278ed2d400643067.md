While we are used to seeing lists ranking the best hotels, best hotels with a
view, best hotel pools, best hotel spas, among numerous other “best of”
categories, I do not recall ever seeing a list of the destinations that
actually had the highest-rated hotels in the world.

[Via
CNN](http://web.archive.org/web/20160326003656/http://www.cnn.com/2014/11/11/travel/world-
best-hotel-destinations/index.html): based on a study which analyzed seven
million customer reviews, the city with the highest rated hotels in the world
is Cape Town, South Africa, with an average score of 8.43 out of a possible
10. This surprised me, as I certainly do not think of South Africa as a
country offering top-notch hotel accommodations, other than some of the ultra-
luxurious safaris.

The destination that took second place was no surprise at all: the Maldives,
with an average score of 8.33. The Maldives is the type of destination were
people travel to so that they can spend days relaxing at their hotels,
sometimes without even leaving the hotel premises. Therefore, you would expect
to see the Maldives ranked high on such a list.

As for the complete top 25 list for the destinations with the highest-rated
hotels in the world:

  1. **Cape Town, South Africa**
  2. **The Maldives**
  3. **Zermatt, Switzerland**
  4. **Queenstown, New Zealand**
  5. **Seychelles**
  6. **Budapest, Hungary**
  7. **Santorini, Greece**
  8. **Bruges, Belgium**
  9. **Gdansk, Poland**
  10. **Hoi An, Vietnam**
  11. **Sofia, Bulgaria**
  12. **Crete Island, Greece**
  13. **Verona, Italy**
  14. **Rotorua, New Zealand**
  15. **Khao Lak, Thailand**
  16. **Hualien, Taiwan**
  17. **Sunshine Coast, Australia**
  18. **Edinburgh, Scotland**
  19. **Cuzco, Peru**
  20. **Chicago**
  21. **Krakow, Poland**
  22. **Nagano, Japan**
  23. **Durban, South Africa**
  24. **Grenada, Spain**
  25. **Marrakech, Morocco**

I am shocked to see Durban on this list for the same reasons as Cape Town. In
fact, if you visit the [TripAdvisor
section](http://web.archive.org/web/20160326003656/http://www.tripadvisor.com/Hotels-g312595-Durban_KwaZulu_Natal-
Hotels.html) for Durban Hotels, you will probably draw the same conclusion.
And not a single Canadian or Hawaiian city on the list?

Chicago has probably the highest hotel standards among major U.S. cities, so
it makes sense that it is the only U.S. city on the list.

As for specific categories reviewed, the winners were the following:

  * Value for money: Budapest, Hungary
  * Location: Sunshine Coast, Australia
  * Staff performance: Cape Town, South Africa
  * Hotel condition/cleanliness: Zermatt, Switzerland
  * Room comfort/standard: Cape Town, South Africa
  * Food/dining: Bruges, Belgium

Did your experience at any of these destinations support the inclusion of any
particular city in the list?
