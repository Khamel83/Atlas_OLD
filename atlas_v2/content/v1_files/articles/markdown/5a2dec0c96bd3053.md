## New York Mets

**neil:** We’re here to talk about the NL East, which [seemed to
be](https://cbswashington.files.wordpress.com/2015/03/sports-illustrated-
cover1.jpg) Washington’s for the taking a year ago but instead went to the
upstart New York Mets. Do we think that order persists into 2016? Or was it a
perfect storm of Washington underperforming — and New York’s young roster
playing to the maximum of its abilities — both of which might signal a
reversal this season?

**heynawl-enten:** Well, all the advanced statistical projection stuff I’ve
seen ([FanGraphs](http://www.fangraphs.com/coolstandings.aspx), [Baseball

Mets finishing in first place. I’m not so sure, but the Mets have many of the
same parts returning from their pennant-winning [2015
roster](http://www.baseball-reference.com/teams/NYM/2015.shtml). In fact,
their starting rotation will be one of, if not _the_ strongest in the major
leagues.

**rob:** I agree with Harry. The Mets are legitimately good. The Nationals are

## Washington Nationals

**rob:** It’s hard to ignore the way in which the Nationals [flamed
out](https://fivethirtyeight.com/features/washington-nationals-bryce-harper-
jonathan-papelbon/) last year. I, like most analytically inclined baseball
observers, am not a big believer in team chemistry. But to whatever extent
chemistry exists and affects outcomes on the field, the Nationals do not seem
to have it (see, for example, their surly closer [choking their best
player](http://m.mlb.com/video/topic/0/v505505983/phi-wsh-harper-papelbon-
separated-after-dispute)).

**heynawl-enten:** This, to me, is an amazing fact: Bryce Harper was flat-out
amazing last season — he [put up](http://bbref.com/pi/shareit/Jky6a) a .460
on-base percentage, a .649 slugging average and 42 home runs — yet, it still
wasn’t enough for Washington to make the playoffs.

**rob:** Right, Harper had not only an [MVP-caliber
season](https://fivethirtyeight.com/features/bryce-harper-nl-mvp-mlb/), but
one of the [best seasons in baseball
history](http://bbref.com/pi/shareit/VC4GY) last year: 11.2 [wins above
replacement](https://www.baseballprospectus.com/glossary/index.php?search=WARP),
[per Baseball
Prospectus](http://www.baseballprospectus.com/card/card.php?id=66018). Amazing
as Harper is, he’s unlikely to repeat that feat. So even if he’s great again
this year — in near-MVP form, let’s say — they will still have to replace four
to five WAR just because of his [regression to the
mean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regression_toward_the_mean).

**neil:** And that number will be subtracted from a team-wide performance
that, like Harry said, just wasn’t good enough.

**heynawl-enten:** Now, here’s a question. The 2015 Nationals also
underperformed given their runs scored and allowed. In fact, their
[Pythagorean record](http://www.fangraphs.com/library/principles/expected-
wins-and-losses/) was 89-73 — the [same as the Mets](http://www.baseball-
reference.com/leagues/NL/2015-standings.shtml#expanded_standings_E::13). Do
the Nationals do better by regressing to where their run differential
suggested they should have been last year?

**rob:** Yes, and that’s around where both Baseball Prospectus and FanGraphs
have them projected: 87 wins from [from
BP](http://www.baseballprospectus.com/fantasy/dc/), 90 [from
FG](http://www.fangraphs.com/coolstandings.aspx). But I don’t think that will
be enough to beat out the Mets.

**neil:** Here’s something that I don’t think matters, but I’ll bring it up
anyway: Outgoing manager Matt Williams was (probably rightly)
[criticized](https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/nationals/manager-matt-
williams-lost-the-clubhouse-will-he-lose-his-
job/2015/09/26/5244beca-647b-11e5-9757-e49273f05f65_story.html) for the way he
handled that team last season; any chance [replacing him with Dusty
Baker](http://m.mlb.com/news/article/156337016/dusty-baker-hired-as-nationals-
new-manager) helps with some of those aforementioned chemistry issues?

**heynawl-enten:** I mean Baker has been a “winning” manager [in the
past](http://www.baseball-reference.com/managers/bakerdu01.shtml). And he
[helped to invent the high-five](http://www.espn.com/video/clip?id=11253247).

**rob:** Maybe, yeah. If chemistry exists, it doesn’t seem to be very
consistent year-to-year. So maybe getting Baker in there will reverse the bad
juju.

On the other hand, Baker [isn’t a great
manager](http://www.firejoemorgan.com/search?q=dusty+baker) in most of the
conventional tactical ways that we can measure, so that deficiency will likely
undo whatever benefit they may get from his chemistry-enhancing abilities.

**heynawl-enten:** Well, that’s why so many National fans were worried about
that signing, if I recall. Many people [wanted Bud Black
instead](http://m.mlb.com/news/article/155897446/bud-black-a-good-fit-as-
nationals-manager).

**rob:** Bud Black would have been a better choice.

**neil:** At least they don’t have any especially stellar young pitching for
Baker to [burn out and run into the
ground](http://bleacherreport.com/articles/367793-got-young-pitching-keep-it-
away-from-dusty-baker). Even Stephen Strasburg is getting older now.

**heynawl-enten:** Remember when Baker basically put Mark Prior into the
ground?

**rob:** I do {sobs}.

**neil:** Poor Rob.

**neil:** Speaking of Strasburg, he had a somewhat [disappointing
year](http://www.fangraphs.com/blogs/whats-going-on-with-stephen-strasburg/)
in [2015](http://bbref.com/pi/shareit/tacwA). Will he ever reach the lofty
heights [everyone was predicting for him](https://s-media-cache-
ak0.pinimg.com/736x/85/05/d8/8505d8a99264b81b7441747ea714f40b.jpg) all these
years?

**rob:** One of these years, I think he’ll turn in an amazing season. This
could be it, although PECOTA predicts [only 2.7

At the same time, great pitching is more and more [a young man’s
game](http://www.beyondtheboxscore.com/2014/2/25/5437902/pitching-aging-
curves), and Strasburg is now 27 with clearly [fading
velocity](https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/fancy-stats/wp/2015/05/13/why-
is-stephen-strasburg-struggling-because-every-pitch-is-failing-him/) and a

**heynawl-enten:** I will say, of course, that Strasburg’s [fielding-
independent pitching](http://www.fangraphs.com/library/pitching/fip/) (FIP)
the last two seasons was [under 3.00](http://bbref.com/pi/shareit/mT6pi). In

very good pitcher. There’s been some bad luck. One real question is, can he
stay healthy?

**rob:** He did have a solid FIP (and he’s a very good pitcher, no question).
But he also had problems controlling [exit

Also, [losing Jordan
Zimmermann](http://m.mlb.com/news/article/158390022/tigers-sign-jordan-

**neil:** So it sounds like Washington’s pitching isn’t quite at the great
level we thought they’d be before last season. And we also mentioned Harper’s

**heynawl-enten:** I do admit I have a love for Ben Revere. Why? I have no

**neil:** Maybe because you and I were on hand to witness a rare [Ben Revere

**heynawl-enten:** That lineup looks pretty “meh” to me. I mean [Jayson

**rob:** Murphy _could_ be the [Mets Playoff

**heynawl-enten:** I guess the Nationals can hope that Ryan Zimmerman [stays

**rob:** I was also going to say that [Trea

## Atlanta Braves
