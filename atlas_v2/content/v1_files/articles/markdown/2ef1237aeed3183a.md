## This is the best-ever photo of Pluto. Tomorrow’s will be MUCH better.

This morning, the New Horizons probe zinged safely1 past Pluto. Before it did,
it transmitted the best photo we’ve seen of Pluto so far…the last one we’ll
get [before we get the really good
stuff](http://web.archive.org/web/20250514174537/http://kottke.org/15/07/what-
well-see-from-new-horizons-flyby-of-pluto). Look at
[this](http://web.archive.org/web/20250514174537/http://www.planetary.org/blogs/emily-
lakdawalla/2015/07140911-new-horizons-best-look-at.html):

![Pluto](/web/20250514174537im_/https://kottke.org/cdn-
cgi/image/format=auto,fit=scale-
down,width=1200,metadata=none/plus/misc/images/pluto-closer-up.jpg)

The probe’s “I’m OK!” message will reach Earth around 9pm ET tonight and we’ll
start seeing photos from the flyby Wednesday afternoon…there’s a NASA press
conference scheduled for 3pm ET on July 15. So exciting!

**Update:** The photo above is also the best full-disk image of Pluto that we
will get…the rest will be close-ups and such. So that’s the official Pluto
portrait from now on, folks.
