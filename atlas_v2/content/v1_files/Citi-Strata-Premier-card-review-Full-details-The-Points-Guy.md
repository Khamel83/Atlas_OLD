Citi Strata Premier benefits
----------------------------

Much of [Citi Strata Premier](https://thepointsguy.com/oc/10652?name=Citi%20Strata%20Premier%C2%AE%20Card&pid=10652?tid=post)'s value proposition comes from its ability to earn bonus points in many categories without charging a hefty annual fee. As such, you shouldn't expect an overwhelming number of perks.

JT GENTER/THE POINTS GUY

However, there are several benefits of this card that can help you recoup your annual fee:

* **$100 annual hotel credit**: Enjoy a $100 credit once per calendar year for single-stay hotel bookings of $500 or more (excluding taxes and fees) made through the Citi travel portal.
* **World Elite Mastercard benefits**: As a World Elite Mastercard, the Citi Strata Premier confers several travel-related perks that consumers might not know about. These include a $5 monthly Lyft credit after taking three rides in a calendar month and access to the Mastercard luxury hotels and resorts portfolio for on-property perks like complimentary breakfast and room upgrades upon availability. Through the World Elite Mastercard program, you'll also enjoy perks like cellphone protection and Global Emergency Services.
* **Citi Entertainment**: This program gives cardholders special access to purchase tickets to thousands of events — from concerts and sporting events to unique dining experiences and movie screenings — before the general public can buy them.
* **No foreign transaction fees**: Like any good travel card, the Citi Strata Premier allows you to travel freely without incurring a fee on international spending.

The [Citi Strata Premier](https://thepointsguy.com/oc/10652?name=Citi%20Strata%20Premier%C2%AE%20Card&pid=10652?tid=post) also helps you travel with ease of mind, providing the following travel protections:

* Car rental coverage
* Lost or damaged luggage
* Trip cancellation and interruption
* Trip delay

I always make sure to use the $100 annual hotel credit as it's a sure way to offset the annual fee. However, apart from this, there aren't many other benefits to take advantage of for substantial savings.

Earning points on the Citi Strata Premier
-----------------------------------------

The [Citi Strata Premier](https://thepointsguy.com/oc/10652?name=Citi%20Strata%20Premier%C2%AE%20Card&pid=10652?tid=post)'s earning structure is where the card shines.

The card offers 10 ThankYou points per dollar spent on hotels, car rentals and attractions booked through the Citi Travel portal.

Cardholders also earn 3 points per dollar on purchases in the following categories, with no caps or annual limits:

* Air travel
* EV charging stations
* Gas stations
* Hotels
* Supermarkets
* Restaurants (including takeout)

The card earns 1 point per dollar on all other purchases.

FG TRADE/GETTY IMAGES

These earning rates are among the most impressive of any travel rewards card, especially considering the Citi Strata Premier's affordable annual fee. This is my go-to card for gas purchases as it earns an unlimited 3 points per dollar, which lessens the sting of the high gas prices in California.

I also often use this card to earn bonus points at supermarkets because, unlike the [American Express® Gold Card](https://thepointsguy.com/oc/6344?name=American%20Express%C2%AE%20Gold%20Card&pid=6344?tid=post), there is no spending cap on this category.

Which cards compete with the Citi Strata Premier?
-------------------------------------------------

As an affordable travel rewards card, the [Citi Strata Premier](https://thepointsguy.com/oc/10652?name=Citi%20Strata%20Premier%C2%AE%20Card&pid=10652?tid=post) has a few direct competitors that might be a better fit for you:

* **If you prefer Chase cards**: The [Chase Sapphire Preferred® Card](https://thepointsguy.com/oc/22125056?name=Chase%20Sapphire%20Preferred%C2%AE%20Card%20PID&pid=22125056?tid=post) (see [rates and fees](/tc/221210132?pid=221210132&name=Chase%20Sapphire%20Preferred%C2%AE%20Card&data-testid=ratesFees)) competes directly with the Citi Strata Premier with the same $95 annual fee. While Chase's transfer partners (including United MileagePlus and World of Hyatt) might be more beneficial for many travelers, there are also some overlaps (like Singapore KrisFlyer and Virgin Atlantic Flying Club). For more information, read our [full review of the Sapphire Preferred](https://thepointsguy.com/credit-cards/reviews/chase-sapphire-preferred-review/).
* **If you want a flat earning rate**: Try the [Capital One Venture Rewards Credit Card](https://thepointsguy.com/credit-cards/capital-one-venture/). It also charges the same $95 annual fee, but this card earns a flat 2 miles per dollar on most purchases instead of offering bonus categories. For more information, read our [full review of the Venture Rewards card](https://thepointsguy.com/credit-cards/reviews/capital-one-venture-card-review/).
* **If you want added perks**: If you dine out frequently, you might prefer the [American Express® Gold Card](https://thepointsguy.com/oc/6344?name=American%20Express%C2%AE%20Gold%20Card&pid=6344?tid=post). It has a higher earning rate of 4 points per dollar at U.S. supermarkets (on the first $25,000 in purchases per calendar year, then 1 per dollar) and at restaurants worldwide (on up to $50,000 in purchases per calendar year, then 1 point per dollar). It also comes with valuable credits that can more than offset the substantial $325 annual fee (see [rates and fees](/tc/6344?pid=6344&name=American%20Express%C2%AE%20Gold%20Card&data-testid=ratesFees)). For more information, read our [full review of the Amex Gold](https://thepointsguy.com/credit-cards/reviews/amex-gold-card-review/).

For additional options, check out our [full list of the top travel rewards cards](https://thepointsguy.com/credit-cards/travel/).
