CM:Exactly, and so aside from the fact that it actually kneecapped some of those businesses and kept them from being able to do advertising and things like that, what it really did was quietly train the customer to believe that Apple was the only company that they could trust, and it’s extraordinary how well that has worked to the point that Apple customers are much, much more likely to say, “I will let Apple peek into my emails, and texts, and personal contacts, and even my wallet in order to do the stuff I want, but man oh man, I would never let Meta do that, or I would never let Google do that because those guys are evil”. CM:The other argument was “No, this is a Section 7 Clayton Act violation that’s illegal tying that says you can’t force somebody to take product B in order to get product A”, and those two competing theories were never actually tested in court and I don’t mean in the last year or two, I mean over the last 30 years.
