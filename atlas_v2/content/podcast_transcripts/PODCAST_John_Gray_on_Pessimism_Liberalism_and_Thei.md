for his critical examination of liberalism, atheism, and the human
condition. His unique perspective is shaped over a decades-long
career, during which he has authored influential books on topics
ranging from political theory to what we can learn from cats about
on how to live a good life. His latest book,The New
Leviathans: Thoughts After Liberalism, delivers a provocative
examination of the 2020s' political landscape, challenges liberal
triumphalism with a realistic critique of ongoing global crises and
the persistent allure of human delusions.Tyler and John sat down to discuss his latest
book, including who he thinks will carry on his work, what young
people should learn if liberalism is dead, whether modern physics
allows for true atheism, what in Eastern Orthodoxy attracts him,
the benefits of pessimism, what philanthropic cause he’d invest a
billion dollars in, under what circumstances he’d sacrifice his
life, what he makes of UFOs, the current renaissance in film and
books, whether Monty Python is still funny, how Herman Melville
influenced him, who first spotted his talent, his most unusual work
habit, what he’ll do next, and more.Donateto
