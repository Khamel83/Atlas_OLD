Subscribe wherever you get your podcasts.All EpisodesMelissa Dell on the Significance of PersistenceMelissa Dell on the Significance of PersistenceJul 15, 2020Explaining 10 percent of something is not usually
cause for celebration. And yet when it comes to economic
development, where so many factors are in play—institutions,
culture, geography, to name a few—it’s impressive indeed. And
that’s just what Melissa Dell has accomplished in her pathbreaking
work. From the impact of the Mexican Revolution to the different
development paths of northern and southern Vietnam, her work
exploits what are often accidents of history—whether a Peruvian
village was just inside or outside a mine’s catchment area, for
example—to explain persistent differences in outcomes. Her work has
earned numerous plaudits, including the John Bates Clark Medal
earlier this year.On the 100th episode ofConversations with
Tyler, Melissa joined Tyler to discuss what’s behind Vietnam’s
economic performance, why persistence isn’t predictive, the
benefits and drawbacks of state capacity, the differing economic
legacies of forced labor in Indonesia and Peru, whether people like
her should still be called a Rhodes scholar, if SATs are useful,
the joys of long-distance running, why higher temps are bad for
economic growth, how her grandmother cultivated her curiosity, her
next project looking to unlock huge historical datasets, and
more.Read afull
