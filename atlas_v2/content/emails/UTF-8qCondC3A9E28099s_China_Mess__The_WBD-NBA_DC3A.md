Welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon assortment of Puck’s best new reporting.

First up today, Lauren Sherman deciphers Vanity Fair’s seemingly strange decision to drop its annual star-studded Hollywood Issue three months early—a potentially financially motivated decision that has stoked renewed skepticism of Condé Nast C.E.O. Roger Lynch’s strategy for weathering a rough ’24 and the company’s ongoing headwinds in China.

Plus, below the fold: John Ourand rings up TNT Sports chairman and C.E.O. Luis Siberwasser for a candid postmortem on the NBA-WBD rights scuffle and WBD’s bundle of concessions. Peter Hamby has exclusive data on Dems’ media handicap and Trump’s perplexing goodwill from voters. And Scott Mendelson identifies a handful of silver linings in Red One’s relatively catastrophic box office
performance.

On The Grill Room, Dylan Byers talks to Infatuation C.E.O. Paul Needham and entrepreneur Alison Roman about the food media business. And on The Powers That Be, Abby Livingston and Peter evaluate Matt Gaetz’s confirmation odds in the Senate.
