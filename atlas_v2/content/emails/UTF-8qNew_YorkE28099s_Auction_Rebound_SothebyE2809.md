The art market has begun to heal. That’s the main takeaway from last week’s
sales in New York, where $50.6 million worth of art works were sold for an average price of $95,000. In the end, 73 percent of the 530 lots offered were sold, with just under a quarter selling at prices above the estimate range. Meanwhile, about one-third had to be sold at compromise prices, where the consignor did not get bids to their estimates.
