If you're a high performer, you likely got to where you are because of your raw talent and ability to execute.


Now that your scope has grown, though, your ability to increase impact depends on how well you influence other people--often without formal authority. In other words, you need people to do what you want them to do--enthusiastically and collaboratively.


Unfortunately, no one teaches us how to do this beyond a basic level.


So your day-to-day involves avoidable back-and-forth, friction, pushback, apathy, random questions, skepticism, or confusion about areas you believe are important.


But it doesn't have to be this way.


You and your team can move faster. You can quickly understand each other. You can worry less about cajoling skeptics. You can spend less time figuring out how to say what you want to say. You can accomplish more of what you want--and have more fun in the process.


To get to the next level, you can't simply "do" more yourself. You must extend your impact beyond your own work. You must get genuine cooperation and true buy-in from those around you. This is what you will learn to do in this course.


WHAT TO EXPECT:


This is a highly-interactive course with examples, Q&A, hands-on exercises, and tactics you can apply immediately. There are 9 hours of live instruction, all live on Zoom taught by Wes. We will focus on both the "why" and tactical pieces around the "how."


Each day features four hands-on exercises (created by Wes, exclusively available in the course) that include students working silently for 7-8 minutes, reviewing other students' work for a few minutes, then hearing live teardowns/feedback from Wes on a select exercise submissions everyone can learn from. You'll hone your intuition and standards on "what great looks like."


The skills you need to become more effective CANNOT be solved through brute force. In these 2 days, you will come away with new tools.


Course content

• How to be direct and clear, without damaging relationships

• How to perform well in high-stakes real-time conversations

• How to sharpen your ability to prevent/overcome skepticism

• How to anticipate stakeholder concerns and prevent 90% of pushback before it happens

• How to articulate your POV when strong rationale can make or break the outcome

• How to advocate for your ideas the right way

• How to instill a sense of urgency so your ideas are heard and acted on quickly

• How to avoid common communication patterns that diminish your impact

• How to diagnose the question-behind-the-question so folks are satisfied by your responses

The goal isn't simply to learn the concepts--it's to internalize them. If you put effort into the course, you will make a major leap ahead.

WHO THIS IS NOT FOR:


- This is not for you if you are looking for 1:1 coaching.

- This is not for you if you believe there's not much for you to learn about sharpening your communication.

- This is not for you if you want step-by-step instructions for every situation you might encounter. My goal is to help sharp operators develop even better judgment, not to cover directions for every potential situation.

- This is not for you if you expect to become excellent in communication overnight. Becoming excellent at anything requires time and ongoing practice.

- This is not for you if you want a slower-paced course over several weeks. This is a 2-day intensive designed for busy operators who want to reduce context switching between work and the course.

- This is not for you if you are primarily looking for a course on public speaking or delivery (removing "ums" and "ahs", making eye contact, projecting your voice, etc). In my experience, public speaking is not the bottleneck for most operators. Instead, the course focuses on improving your underlying ability to think clearly, which enables you to communicate clearly.

What you’ll get out of this course

Present to your CEO to maximize highly-visible moments

You might only have a few minutes of your leadership team's attention. We will review tactics for how to move fast, frame the conversation upfront, handle unexpected questions, prevent "insecure vibes," etc. Get senior leaders to seek (and truly listen to) your input.

Managing up in day-to-day interactions

The more senior you become, the more you'll need to manage up. Hint: It's not about sending a weekly recap of your contributions. We will review real examples and practice how to reduce cognitive load for your manager, share your rationale, focus on the punchline, and more.

Frame your ideas and give the right amount of context in any situation

How do you get buy-in with less back and forth? How do you know the right level of detail (and which details) to share? How can you be more concise w/o sacrificing context? We will dissect these nuances and you will have a chance to rewrite examples to put lessons into practice.

Identify ways to improve your own writing

While writing well takes years to master, we'll focus on simple ways to improve and edit your work. You'll practice during in-class exercises & edit your own writing as homework. You'll see other students' writing, which gives rare visibility into different approaches and styles.

Influence up and sideways to get things done

Some principles (like reducing cognitive load) apply regardless of your audience--while other situations require you to understand power dynamics, leverage, etc. We will practice "reading" situations and subtext with more accuracy so you can make better decisions.

Get enthusiastic buy-in and collaboration from colleagues outside your team

You may have trust with your team, but how do you advocate for ideas with other teams? We will discuss frameworks to tackle this, so you can get inside the heads of individuals/teams you want to appeal to more quickly.

See your own communication more accurately and spotting ways to improve

The goal of the course is to help you see, think, and act differently. We often have default behavior with how we communicate, without questioning what's effective & what to do better. Alumni often say, "I thought I did this well, but had no idea how much I could improve."
