30+ Push Notification Statistics (New 2025 Data)

Push notifications, or app notifications, have become commonplace on everyone’s screen.

These notifications allow marketers to attract more customers and increase traffic on their app or website.

Studies show that 88% of users were more engaged on an app platform and had a 12% click rate due to push notifications.

In this blog, we will look at key push notification statistics from recent years that can serve as a reference for the coming years.

Top Highlights

In the US, smartphone users get about 46 push notifications a day.

On phones and computers, 60% of people sign up for push notifications, while on tablets, it’s 10%.

eCommerce, media, and blogging businesses send the most push notifications, with almost 40% using them successfully.

Push notifications have high open rates, reaching up to 90%.

Business and finance notifications have the highest open rates at 5.46%.

About 28% of mobile app users who click on a push notification end up making a purchase.

Around 60% of people agree to receive push notifications.

Most people (95%) get push notifications through the Chrome browser.

On Android 13, the highest opt-in rate for push notifications was 74% in the BSFI sector in 2023.

Between 55% and 60% of app users agree to receive push notifications.

Key Push Notification Statistics

Push notifications are a part of daily mobile phone usage. All apps installed on our devices send push messages.

Here are some important stats about push notifications.

 1. Tuesday sees the highest engagement for push notifications at 8.4%.

3. According to Avada, The Business and Finance industry enjoys the highest open rates at 5.46%.

This may be due to the relevance and urgency of this industry. The finance world goes through a lot of changes every hour, and to keep up with them, users opt for relevant apps and websites.

4. The Entertainment and Events industry enjoys 5.04% open rates.

Why are notifications opened or ignored?

Open rates are influenced by a variety of factors that affect the app user’s decision.

They are mainly based on what the broadcast messages are about when they are delivered, user preference, and technical influence.

Relevance

Push notifications place a lot of importance on the relevance of the messages.

Most are about current trending topics that the majority of users are interested in.

If the content of the notification is not relevant to the user, most push notifications are likely ignored and disabled.

Timing

The timing of the message is also important. Studying when most users are online and likely to view push notifications affects the click-through rate.

Studies show that mobile use peaks between 9 am and 7 pm.

Source

Personalization

Push notifications that cater to a user’s personal preference or needs are more likely to be opened.

In-app marketers need to analyze their target audience and create push notifications accordingly.

FAQ's

The main difference between these is that they all are sent from different places.

Push notifications are sent by apps installed on your mobile devices, and text messages that use your mobile number for communication and marketing emails are, naturally, sent by email addresses.

Push notifications are short, eye-catching, and under 10 words.

Text messages can be a bit long, ranging between 20-100 words, whereas emails can be quite long, listing products or services you may find interesting.

No, push notifications and general mobile notifications are not the same.

Though the format appears to be the same, push notifications are meant for marketing purposes while mobile notifications are used to notify app users of something they are currently signed up for.

For example, you may get a notification of a message you received on WhatsApp, whereas WhatsApp may send you a push notification about a new feature they are rolling out.

Open rates determine when a user opens an app or notification whereas CTRs come into play when the user engages with the notification.

For example, When a user opens Amazon due to push notification for sale, it is considered an Open rate, while when the user engages with the screen and clicks on add to cart, it is a CTR.

Some of the best practices for effective push notifications are:

  • Rich push notifications that use text, images, and emojis. Rich media can cause users to have a push notification reaction and open an app.
  • Actionable content generates CTR and increases the opening rate.
  • Reminding users to complete an action. This can increase app engagement.
  • Playing into a user’s curiosity can increase sign-up rates.
  • Use push notification automation to ensure that your brand makes fairly consistent appearances on the user screen.

These practices can make users interact and click on push notifications.
