Where Are the Highest-Rated Hotels in the World Located?

November 12, 2014
Miguel R. Quinones | Google+

While we are used to seeing lists ranking the best hotels, best hotels with a view, best hotel pools, best hotel spas, among numerous other “best of” categories, I do not recall ever seeing a list of the destinations that actually had the highest-rated hotels in the world.

Via CNN: based on a study which analyzed seven million customer reviews, the city with the highest rated hotels in the world is Cape Town, South Africa, with an average score of 8.43 out of a possible 10. This surprised me, as I certainly do not think of South Africa as a country offering top-notch hotel accommodations, other than some of the ultra-luxurious safaris.

The destination that took second place was no surprise at all: the Maldives, with an average score of 8.33. The Maldives is the type of destination were people travel to so that they can spend days relaxing at their hotels, sometimes without even leaving the hotel premises. Therefore, you would expect to see the Maldives ranked high on such a list.

As for the complete top 25 list for the destinations with the highest-rated hotels in the world:

  1. Cape Town, South Africa
  2. The Maldives
  3. Zermatt, Switzerland
  4. Queenstown, New Zealand
  5. Seychelles
  6. Budapest, Hungary
  7. Santorini, Greece
  8. Bruges, Belgium
  9. Gdansk, Poland
  10. Hoi An, Vietnam
  11. Sofia, Bulgaria
  12. Crete Island, Greece
  13. Verona, Italy
  14. Rotorua, New Zealand
  15. Khao Lak, Thailand
  16. Hualien, Taiwan
  17. Sunshine Coast, Australia
  18. Edinburgh, Scotland
  19. Cuzco, Peru
  20. Chicago
  21. Krakow, Poland
  22. Nagano, Japan
  23. Durban, South Africa
  24. Grenada, Spain
  25. Marrakech, Morocco

I am shocked to see Durban on this list for the same reasons as Cape Town. In fact, if you visit the TripAdvisor section for Durban Hotels, you will probably draw the same conclusion. And not a single Canadian or Hawaiian city on the list?

Chicago has probably the highest hotel standards among major U.S. cities, so it makes sense that it is the only U.S. city on the list.

As for specific categories reviewed, the winners were the following:

  • Value for money: Budapest, Hungary
  • Location: Sunshine Coast, Australia
  • Staff performance: Cape Town, South Africa
  • Hotel condition/cleanliness: Zermatt, Switzerland
  • Room comfort/standard: Cape Town, South Africa
  • Food/dining: Bruges, Belgium

Did your experience at any of these destinations support the inclusion of any particular city in the list?

  • LoveThisGame

    I stayed in Zermatt few times in 2013 and 2014 for skiing. Service was impeccable. Everything delivered in the whole village is like a Swiss clock and the whole village is like a postcard. People have early breakfast there and then head to the slopes by 8 AM. They come back starting 4 or 5 PM. So, the hotels are mainly empty during the day. Most hotels have pool size Jacuzzi (some of them extending to the outside snowy surrounding!!). People relax in them until 7-8 PM and then they head to dinner. Most hotel rates include breakfast and dinner. People want to save time and sleep early to head early to the slopes the next day.
    By the way, skiing is not expensive in Zermatt (compared to any other Alpine resort) and there is NO line. However, hotels could be expensive specially during peak period (holidays, school vacations, …).
    I could not find though a single hotel chain to redeem points & miles

      Thanks for your comment LTG. I have to admit, I had not heard a lot about Zermatt before writing this article. After doing a little research, it looks like a stunning destination. It is a shame there are no options to redeem points!

  • LoveThisGame

    I visited Seychelles few weeks ago with the family. The Islands have plenty of very nice resorts that can be booked using miles and points (Hilton, SPG, …). The different islands are stunning with different vegetation depending on the altitude of their mountains/hills (up 905 m), the higher they are, the more rain they get even though they are very close to each other. The people are very nice but really laid back. So, productivity gets affected, i.e., not obvious to get great service. But I guess that the kindness of people can make up for some of it.
    I am bit surprised about the rating as the Islands get less than 175,000 tourists per year (Maldives gets over 1 million, Mauritius almost 1 million). How could it statistically make such rating?
    In my opinion and after visiting many islands in the Indian ocean, they are the best islands to visit in that ocean. They offer much more with unspoiled tropical wilderness in the granite moutains and great beaches/coral reefs. They are much nicer to visit than the Maldives (not much to do beside the beach and most corals are dead due to El-nino more than a decade ago) or Mauritius where the sugar plantations have decimated the nature.
