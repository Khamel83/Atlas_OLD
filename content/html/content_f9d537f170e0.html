Tinder Has Microtransactions Now

By  Josh Coulson
Published Nov 15, 2021
Follow
Followed
Link copied to clipboard
Sign in to your TheGamer account

The newest generation of consoles, which turned one just last week, was the first to usher in $70 games. Not every developer has jumped aboard this bandwagon, but there are a fair few triple-A titles out there now which will set you back 70 big ones if you want to own them brand new. However, the games making the most money, for the most part, are the ones that are free-to-play. Fortnite, Apex Legends, you know the ones.

The main way developers make money from games you can play without spending a penny is by loading them up with in-game purchases and microtransactions. Stuff you don't have to buy, but let's be honest, you're probably going to. This phenomenon isn't exclusive to the video game world, of course. In fact, even Tinder has microtransactions now.

RELATED: Pokemon: Here's What Every Kanto Gym Leader's Tinder Profile Would Look Like

That's right, the app designed to help people find dates, and perhaps even your significant other, has in-app purchases. They're called Tinder Coins, and while they won't be available everywhere until 2022, some regions have them right now. Tinder Coins can be bought in the Tinder app and spent on features called Boost and Super Like, both of which will apparently help you match with more people. It seems if you're not willing to pick up some Tinder Coins of your own, someone might swoop in and steal yo' girl.

Match, Tinder's parent company, outlined the rollout of Tinder Coins during its latest earnings call earlier this month. It also discussed plans for a datings metaverse, a social media and video gaming buzzword right now. This comes after Facebook changed its own name to Meta and Mark Zuckerberg outlined his company's goals in terms of a Facebook metaverse.

While meeting a potential partner virtually before seeing them in person might actually be a good idea, especially in the current climate, it's hard to imagine anyone will like the idea of their dating app having microtransactions. Well, apart from those at Match making money from it. The word is usually met with a sign and an eye roll in the gaming world, and now the dating world can share that sentiment.
