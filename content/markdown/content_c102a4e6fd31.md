<html><body><div><article class="b-article-body"><p class="c-paragraph sections-facilities">Aramark Sports + Entertainment has been selected as the food and beverage service provider for <a href="/Articles/2025/04/18/what-happens-in-vegas-the-athletics-striking-stadium-design-responds-directly-to-natural-and-man-made-environments/" rel="noreferrer" target="_blank" title="https://www.sportsbusinessjournal.com/Articles/2025/04/18/what-happens-in-vegas-the-athletics-striking-stadium-design-responds-directly-to-natural-and-man-made-environments/">the proposed new A’s ballpark in Las Vegas</a>, according to a half-dozen industry sources. </p><p class="c-paragraph sections-facilities">The win didn’t come cheaply for the Philadelphia-based, publicly traded concessionaire. As part of a 20-year deal, Aramark’s total outlay is at least $175M, sources said, with others indicating it could be more. The $175M includes an equity investment into the team of at least $100M and a capex investment commitment of at least $75M. The deal hasn’t been finalized yet, and neither the A’s nor Aramark could comment. </p><p class="c-paragraph sections-facilities">A total investment of $175M (or higher) would almost certainly be the biggest financial investment in a team and its stadium by an F&amp;B provider in sports business history. Valuations of the A’s earlier this year ranged from $1.57B (Sportico) to $1.8B (Forbes); based off those, Aramark’s investment would be worth approximately between 5.5% and 6% of the team. But with the A’s scheduled to open their new stadium just off the Las Vegas strip in 2028, their valuation in this deal was likely higher than any public estimates. </p><p class="c-paragraph sections-facilities">Five of the <a href="/Articles/2024/06/24/food-fight/" rel="" target="_blank" title="https://www.sportsbusinessjournal.com/Articles/2024/06/24/food-fight/">sports venue F&amp;B industry’s six biggest companies</a> -- Aramark Sports + Entertainment, Delaware North, Legends, Levy, and Sodexo Live -- competed for the A’s’ business, beginning late last year. At least four of those were willing to consider the A’s equity stake request. Oak View Group, which doesn’t have any baseball clients and is already engaged in Las Vegas with its <a href="/Articles/2025/03/03/what-just-happened-in-vegas-between-the-raiders-oak-view-group-and-levy/" rel="" target="_blank" title="https://www.sportsbusinessjournal.com/Articles/2025/03/03/what-just-happened-in-vegas-between-the-raiders-oak-view-group-and-levy/">recent takeover of Allegiant</a> Stadium, was the only major player that didn’t compete. F&amp;B consultant <b>Jonathan Harris</b> ran the RFP process for the A’s.</p><p class="c-paragraph sections-facilities">Delaware North, Levy, and Aramark emerged with the highest/best offers in the last month of the RFP process, which concluded in April. Aramark’s proposal included the involvement of restaurateur <b>Will Guidara</b>, co-owner of Eleven Park Madison in N.Y., which was once named the best restaurant in the world. His exact involvement or role moving forward isn’t yet clear. </p><p class="c-paragraph sections-facilities"><a href="/Articles/2024/10/23/oakland-athletics-john-fisher-minority-sale/" rel="" target="_blank" title="https://www.sportsbusinessjournal.com/Articles/2024/10/23/oakland-athletics-john-fisher-minority-sale/">Galatioto Sports Partners</a> (GSP) brokered the equity deal on behalf of the A’s. GSP namesake <b>Sal Galatioto</b> declined to comment when asked about the deal. </p><p class="c-paragraph sections-facilities">The A’s Las Vegas stadium project hasn’t been completely finalized, though it is progressing toward an <a href="https://www.reviewjournal.com/sports/athletics/we-have-essentially-broken-ground-construction-on-as-strip-ballpark-on-schedule-3376421/" rel="" target="_blank" title="https://www.reviewjournal.com/sports/athletics/we-have-essentially-broken-ground-construction-on-as-strip-ballpark-on-schedule-3376421/">official groundbreaking in June</a>. Aramark’s equity investment will undoubtedly be used toward financing the <a href="/Articles/2025/04/18/what-happens-in-vegas-the-athletics-striking-stadium-design-responds-directly-to-natural-and-man-made-environments/" rel="" target="_blank" title="https://www.sportsbusinessjournal.com/Articles/2025/04/18/what-happens-in-vegas-the-athletics-striking-stadium-design-responds-directly-to-natural-and-man-made-environments/">$1.75B A’s stadium</a>. A’s owner <b>John Fisher </b>is reportedly looking to close a stadium funding gap of at least $500M. <a href="/Articles/2025/04/30/report-aramark-had-talks-to-invest-in-as-ballpark/" rel="" target="_blank" title="https://www.sportsbusinessjournal.com/Articles/2025/04/30/report-aramark-had-talks-to-invest-in-as-ballpark/">Bloomberg first reported</a> in late April that the A’s and Aramark had held discussions about a potential equity investment. </p><p class="c-paragraph sections-facilities">While F&amp;B deals in some sports are moving toward shorter, more flexible terms, MLB teams are still regularly inking decades-long deals. Should the A’s’ Las Vegas ballpark reach the top-tier of MLB stadiums that produce $100M or more in food and beverage revenue annually, then the account could conceivably produce $2B in F&amp;B revenue over the 20-year span. It’s not known if the deal includes exit clauses for the team and concessionaire; given the length of the deal and Aramark’s equity piece, that would make sense if the relationship didn’t work out.</p><p class="c-paragraph sections-facilities">The A’s give Aramark a second big MLB account win in as many years after it picked up the S.F. Giants last year following an <a href="/Articles/2024/03/21/aramark-sports-entertainment-oracle-park-giants/" rel="" target="_blank" title="https://www.sportsbusinessjournal.com/Articles/2024/03/21/aramark-sports-entertainment-oracle-park-giants/">unusually abbreviated RFP process</a> just before the 2024 MLB season started. In addition to the A’s and Giants, Aramark S+E’s MLB portfolio includes PNC Park (Pirates), Daikin Park (Astros), Kauffman Stadium (Royals), Fenway Park (Red Sox), Coors Field (Rockies), Citizens Bank Park (Phillies), and Citi Field (Mets). Aramark worked with the A’s as their concessionaire at Oakland Coliseum. </p><p class="c-paragraph sections-facilities"><a href="/Daily/Morning-Buzz/2023/05/09/aramark-selling-its-stake-in-san-antonio-spurs/" rel="" target="_blank" title="https://www.sportsbusinessjournal.com/Daily/Morning-Buzz/2023/05/09/aramark-selling-its-stake-in-san-antonio-spurs/">Ownership in a team</a> is not unheard of for Aramark, which, held a stake in the NBA’s Spurs from 2001 until last year when it was sold. That sale contributed to a 121% increase in free cash flow at the end of Aramark’s 2024 fiscal year, according to SEC filings. Capex investment represented 3.3% of the company’s total FY24 revenue ($17.4B). </p><p class="c-paragraph sections-facilities">Legends is operating the A’s’ food and beverage service for the next few years at Sutter Health Park in West Sacramento while the team waits for its new Las Vegas stadium to be built. On that subject, the A’s announced they’re opening <a href="/Articles/2025/04/30/as-to-open-experience-center-in-vegas-to-preview-planned-ballpark/" rel="" target="_blank" title="https://www.sportsbusinessjournal.com/Articles/2025/04/30/as-to-open-experience-center-in-vegas-to-preview-planned-ballpark/">an experience sales center</a> this fall and they’re looking to officially break ground this summer. Clark County (Nevada) <a href="https://has%20announced%20it%E2%80%99s%20opening%20an%20experience%20sales%20center%20this%20fall" rel="" target="_blank" title="https://has announced it’s opening an experience sales center this fall">recently put an agreement</a> in place for what happens to the site if the stadium project isn’t finished, though that’s normal procedure for a project of this magnitude, especially for valuable land near the Las Vegas Strip. </p><p class="c-paragraph sections-facilities"><i>Chris Smith contributed reporting to this story.</i></p><section><div aria-label="The A's Las Vegas stadium is supposed to break ground on construction in 2025.
