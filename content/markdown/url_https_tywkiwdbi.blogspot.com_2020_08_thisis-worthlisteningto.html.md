And then something else happened. The candidates were talking about health care. At first, Biden sounded strong, confident, presidential: &#8220;My plan makes a limit of co-pay to be One. Thousand. Dollars. Because we&#8212;&#8221; He stopped. He pinched his eyes closed. He lifted his hands and thrust them forward, as if trying to pull the missing sound from his mouth . &#8220;We f-f-f-f-further support&#8212;&#8221; He opened his eyes. &#8220;The uh-uh-uh-uh&#8212;&#8221; His chin dipped toward his chest. &#8220;The-uh, the ability to buy into the Obamacare plan.&#8221; Biden also stumbled when trying to say immune system.&nbsp; Fox News edited these moments into a mini montage. Stifling laughter, the host Steve Hilton narrated: &#8220; As the right words struggled to make that perilous journey from Joe Biden&#8217;s brain to Joe Biden&#8217;s mouth , half the time he just seemed to give up with this somewhat tragic and limp admission of defeat.&nbsp; Several days later, Biden&#8217;s team got back in touch with me. One of his aides gingerly asked whether I&#8217;d noticed the former vice president stutter during the debate. ..&nbsp; In Biden&#8217;s office, the first time I bring up his current stuttering, he asks me whether I&#8217;ve seen The King&#8217;s Speech . He speaks almost mystically about the award-winning 2010 film. &#8220;When King George VI, when he stood up in 1939, everyone knew he stuttered, and they knew what courage it took for him to stand up at that stadium and try to speak&#8212;and it gave them courage &#8230; I could feel that. It was that sinking feeling, like&#8212;oh my God, I remember how you felt. You feel like, I don&#8217;t know &#8230; almost like you&#8217;re being sucked into a black hole.&#8221;..&nbsp; In addition to periodically stuttering or blocking on certain sounds, he appears to intentionally not stutter by switching to an alternative word &#8212;a technique called &#8220;circumlocution&#8221;&#8212;&#173;which can yield mangled syntax. I&#8217;ve been following practically everything he&#8217;s said for months now, and sometimes what is quickly characterized as a memory lapse is indeed a stutter. As Eric Jackson, the speech pathologist, pointed out to me, during a town hall in August Biden briefly blocked on Obama, before quickly subbing in my boss. The headlines after the event? &#8220;Biden Forgets Obama&#8217;s Name. &#8221; Other times when Biden fudges a detail or loses his train of thought, it seems unrelated to stuttering, like he&#8217;s just making a mistake. The kind of mistake other candidates make too, though less frequently than he does. I admit to having misunderstood some of Biden's known speaking errors as signs of possible dementia.&nbsp; I stand corrected. The King's Speech , btw, is a superb movie. Email This BlogThis!
