He calls them âhoney potsâ because they leave so much information behind. Mud, sand, soft dirt, and snow are great examples of track traps. Bodies of water or oil spills can be track traps too. A target who steps in water or oil will likely leave footprints on the ground after stepping in the fluid.</p></blockquote><p><strong>Aerial Indicators (above your ankle)</strong></p><p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-60086" src="https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads//2016/10/Spider-Web-1.jpg" alt="Broken spider web between two trees how to track a person illustration." width="462" height="500" srcset="https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Spider-Web-1.jpg 600w, https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Spider-Web-1-320x346.jpg 320w, https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Spider-Web-1-400x433.jpg 400w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 462px) 100vw, 462px" /></p><ul><li>Broken cobwebs</li><li>Detached or missing leaves</li><li>Broken branches that point in the direction of the target</li><li>Scratches or scuffs on trees</li><li>Cut or broken vegetation</li><li>Tall grass or vegetation pushed down into an unnatural position</li><li>Clothing fabric in branches</li><li>Hair in branches</li></ul><p><strong>Litter Indicators (objects discarded intentionally or unintentionally)</strong></p><ul><li>Cigarette butts</li><li>Candy and food wrappers</li><li>Spent ammo casings</li><li>Used medical supplies</li><li>Gum/tobacco</li><li>Clothing</li></ul><p><strong>Blood Indicators</strong></p><p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-60078" src="https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads//2016/10/Blood-1.jpg" alt="Blood indicators what type of wound identification illustration." width="508" height="500" srcset="https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Blood-1.jpg 600w, https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Blood-1-320x315.jpg 320w, https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Blood-1-400x394.jpg 400w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 508px) 100vw, 508px" /></p><p>Your target might be injured and consequently leaving blood stains. The color of the blood stain can tell you a lot about the injury he or she has and how long itâs been since they left the blood stain there.</p><ul><li><em>Dark red drops of blood</em>. Indicates a venous wound. Non-life-threatening.</li><li><em>Bright red streaks of blood</em>. Indicates a possible arterial wound. Life-threatening.</li><li><em>Pink, frothy blood</em>. Indicates a possible wound to the lungs.</li><li><em>Light red, foul-smelling blood</em>. Indicates a possible wound to the stomach.</li></ul><p>Blood changes color over time as itâs exposed to the elements. Initially, blood spots will be brighter but will eventually fade to a brown or rust color.</p><p><strong>Bodily Discharge Indicators</strong></p><p>Vomit, poop, pee, snot &#8212; these bodily discharges may not be pleasant to contemplate, but they can not only help lead you in the direction of your target, but also paint a picture of his current condition.</p><p>Vomit and poop can tell you what sort of food your targetâs been eating. If thereâs a lot of liquid, clear vomit or you see diarrhea, thereâs a chance he could be dehydrated.</p><p>If you find a urine spot that has a very fragrant ammonia smell, the target is likely dehydrated. If it has a fruity smell, thereâs a chance heâs diabetic.</p><div id="attachment_60082" style="width: 610px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-60082" class="wp-image-60082 size-full" src="https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Likely-Man-1.jpg" alt="How to track a person man's urine on the ground illustration." width="600" height="419" srcset="https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Likely-Man-1.jpg 600w, https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Likely-Man-1-320x223.jpg 320w, https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Likely-Man-1-400x279.jpg 400w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /><p id="caption-attachment-60082" class="wp-caption-text">Man peeing</p></div><div id="attachment_60083" style="width: 610px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-60083" class="wp-image-60083 size-full" src="https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Likely-Woman-1.jpg" alt="How to track a person woman's urine on the ground illustration." width="600" height="419" srcset="https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Likely-Woman-1.jpg 600w, https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Likely-Woman-1-320x223.jpg 320w, https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Likely-Woman-1-400x279.jpg 400w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /><p id="caption-attachment-60083" class="wp-caption-text">Woman peeing</p></div><p>The relation of a urine stainâs position to a set of footprints can tell you if it came from a man or a woman. If the urine stain is in front of the footprints, probably a dude. If the stain is in the middle of the footprints or near the heels, likely a lady who popped a squat to do her business.</p><div class="htlad-Mobile_Inline_MedRec"></div><p><strong>Audio and Olfactory Indicators</strong></p><p>As a tracker, you canât just rely on your sight to track down your target. Sounds &#8212; heavy breathing, talking, crying, movement in brush, coughing, etc. &#8212; can provide insights as to where your target is.</p><p>Smells can also provide useful clues. The smell of smoke can lead you to a campfire where the target is currently or has been recently. If the targetâs been without a shower for a couple of days, he might also be giving off some pungent body odor.</p><p>Bottom line: as you scan and search with your eyes, donât take your nose and ears offline. They can provide useful information youâd be missing using sight alone.</p><h3><u>Identifying and Interpreting Footprints</u></h3><p>While you should be scanning and searching for signs and indicators like blood, trampled grass, and broken cobwebs, footprints will be one of your primary ways of following and tracking your target.</p><p>A professional tracker is so adept at tracking footprints that he can identify individuals simply with a glance at the impressions in the ground. They can also immediately tell if the person is running, carrying a load, carrying another person, or even walking backwards.</p><div class='code-block code-block-1' style='margin: 8px 0; clear: both;'><div class='htlad-Responsive_Content_Flex'></div></div><p>The ability to create this dossier on a target simply by looking at their footprints requires some careful observation. Hereâs what to look for when identifying and interpreting footprints.</p><h3>Collect Information on a Footprint Data Card</h3><p>A footprint data card is your police sketch of your targetâs footprint. (John has a template in his book.) Youâll draw the pattern of the sole of his footwear on the card, determine if itâs a boot, shoe, or sandal, and make measurements that include the length of the print overall, the width of its heel (and its length if itâs a boot), and the width of the ball of the foot. Youâll also note if the impression reveals any manufacturer or sizing labels and if the toe is rounded, square, or pointed. Youâll want to record the time and location you located the print and the direction of travel as well.</p><p>Hurth recommends putting a nickname on top of the footprint data card based on its salient characteristics. So if you see a âVibramâ logo in a print, you can call that print âVibram.â</p><p>If the target is barefoot, youâll want to note whether he has a high arch, regular arch, or is flat-footed. Youâll want to measure the width of the ball of the foot and the heel. Make notes about their toes too &#8212; missing digits? Hammer toe?</p><h3>Interpreting Footprints</h3><p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-60079 size-full" src="https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Footprints-1.jpg" alt="Determining meaning in footprints when tracking humans illustration." width="600" height="416" srcset="https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Footprints-1.jpg 600w, https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Footprints-1-320x222.jpg 320w, https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Footprints-1-400x277.jpg 400w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></p><p>If you look closely enough at footprints, they can tell you a lot about what your target was doing when he left them.</p><p>For example, the spacing and depth of the impressions can tell you about the targetâs gait &#8212; whether he was running or walking. Impressions that are far apart from each other and deeper in the toe or the heel indicate that the target was running; impressions that are shallower and closer together indicate walking.</p><div class="htlad-Mobile_Inline_MedRec"></div><p>If the gait is shorter and the impressions are deeper, the target was likely carrying a load like a backpack. Try to put together a story of what your target was doing, as this can help you develop a theory as to what heâs likely to do next.</p><h3>Determining the Number of People in a Group by Footprints</h3><p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-60084 size-full" src="https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Number-in-Group-1.jpg" alt="Determining number of people in a group with footprints illustration." width="600" height="424" srcset="https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Number-in-Group-1.jpg 600w, https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Number-in-Group-1-320x226.jpg 320w, https://content.artofmanliness.com/uploads/2016/10/Number-in-Group-1-400x283.jpg 400w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></p><p>Sometimes counting the number of people in a target group is easy because there are distinct sets of footprints that you can count. Divide the total print count by two, and youâll have a rough estimate of the number of people in your target group.</p><h3>Practice Reading Footprints</h3><p>Reading and interpreting footprints is a skill that can be acquired through practice. They can run, limp on one leg, carry each other, walk and then kneel, drag a body, pretend to fight each other, etc.</p><p>After theyâve left their tracks, go to the track trap and interpret what they did by looking at the tracks. By creating this story about your target as you collect evidence, youâll be in a better position to figure out where theyâre headed so you can find them.</p><div class="htlad-Mobile_Inline_MedRec"></div><p>_____</p><p><strong>Sources</strong></p><p><em><a href="https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00BJ8LQ64/ref=as_li_ss_tl?_encoding=UTF8&amp;btkr=1&amp;linkCode=sl1&amp;tag=artofmanliness03-20&amp;linkId=88c2acba529d479a2ccbff4eb173224e">Combat Tracking Guide</a>&nbsp;</em>by John Hurth (If you get the chance, <a href="https://tyrgroupllc.com/upcoming-courses">take a course from John</a>.
