So it would seem, Obama indicated in Turkey, saying he's ГўВҖВңgoing after every single terrorist network.ГўВҖВқ </p><div class="story-interrupt format-s pos-alpha predetermined  fixed-story-third-paragraph">

</div> <p>Yet actually naming this multi-headed enemy and devising a strategy against itГўВҖВ”a so-called counter-narrative to the appeal of radical jihadГўВҖВ”remains our biggest problem, just as it's been for 14 years since 9/11. In the Democratic debate Saturday night, Hillary Clinton played a game of three-card monte with enemy names, using the various terms ГўВҖВңradical Islamist ideologyГўВҖВқ and ГўВҖВңjihadistsГўВҖВқ before finally settling on the politically correct stance of the Obama administration: ГўВҖВңWe are at war with violent extremism,ГўВҖВқ she said. (This from a very smart person who thought about this issue for four years as secretary of State, and benefited from the best intelligence and countless expert discussions.) Sen. <p>RubioГўВҖВҷs analogy doesnГўВҖВҷt quite hold up, since in World War II America was engaged in a declared war with a nation that happened to be controlled by NazisГўВҖВ”we <i>were</i> at war with all Germans, in other words, in the way we are not with all Muslims. But that doesnГўВҖВҷt mean we still arenГўВҖВҷt very confused about who the ГўВҖВңtheyГўВҖВқ is, a decade and a half later. Obama only sowed more confusion on Monday when he again tried to make the case that ISIS has been somewhat &quot;contained&quot; ГўВҖВ”the group &quot;controls less territoryГўВҖВқ ГўВҖВ” but failed to explain how the group's minions, who were plainly not contained, could slaughter 130 people as far away as Paris. He also spoke of &quot;ending&quot; a war in Syria that is already global.</p>
<p>Sadly, none of this is new. In an infamous memo leaked in October 2003, Donald Rumsfeld lamented to his staff: &quot;We lack metrics to know if we are winning or losing the global war on terror.&quot; Twelve years on, there are still no ГўВҖВңmetrics.ГўВҖВқ How can there be, if you canГўВҖВҷt clearly ID the enemy?</p>
<p class="cms-textAlign-center">***</p>
<p><b>LetГўВҖВҷs go back to the beginning. </b>Immediately after 9/11 George W. Bush started out sensibly counterattacking the Taliban and al Qaeda in AfghanistanГўВҖВ”the ones who had actually attacked usГўВҖВ”then broadened the scope dramatically and began speaking of a ГўВҖВңglobal war on terrorismГўВҖВқ and ГўВҖВңaxis of evilГўВҖВқ to justify his Iraq invasion. He lumped all Iraqi insurgents together, Sunni and Shia alike, as terrorists on par with al Qaeda, and then lamented that we were living in a confusing world. In a leaderly quotation for the ages, Bush declared: ГўВҖВңWhen I was coming up, it was a dangerous world, and you knew exactly who they were. It was us versus them, and it was clear who them was. Today, we are not so sure who the they are, but we know they're there.ГўВҖВқ</p>
<p>Barack Obama, thinking Iraq a ГўВҖВңdumb war,ГўВҖВқ sought to clarify the enemy as soon as he took office, scaling back the fight to ГўВҖВңcore al QaedaГўВҖВқ again and trying to withdraw from other conflicts elsewhere, especially Iraq. But when the threat evolved into affiliated groups in the Mideast and North AfricaГўВҖВ”and finally ISISГўВҖВ”Obama muddied things up himself. He actually classified the names of those new enemiesГўВҖВ”the administration refused for a long time to identify the offshoot al Qaeda groups to the publicГўВҖВ”and at the same time stifled efforts within his own administration to characterize the enemy as ГўВҖВңradical jihadists,ГўВҖВқ speaking generally of ГўВҖВңviolent extremismГўВҖВқ instead so as to avoid offending Muslims and playing into the jihadist script of a &quot;clash of civilizations&quot; between the West and all of Islam (a trap that Rubio unfortunately <a href="http://web.archive.org/web/20211021222354/http://www.politico.com/story/2015/11/marco-rubio-paris-attacks-isil-215905" target="_blank">has fallen into</a> since Paris).</p>
<p>Ironically, no one has seemed more aware or fearful of the prospect of an endless, unidentified, virtually permanent war than Obama, who has regularly warned that America must get off a &quot;perpetual wartime footing.&quot; &quot;We must define the nature and scope of this struggle, or else it will define us,ГўВҖВқ the president said in a speech in 2013. ГўВҖВңUnless we discipline our thinking, our definitions, our actions, we may be drawn into more wars we don't need to fight, or continue to grant presidents unbound powers.&quot;</p>
<p>And yet that's what is still happening. Harold Koh, Obama's former State Department general counsel, calls it &quot;the Forever War.&quot; ItГўВҖВҷs not just the spread of Islamic State and its terror nodes in the West. Throughout the violence-torn Middle East and Central Asia, a reborn al-Qaeda is sprouting new affiliates. Despite Obama's pledge in that 2013 speech to &quot;refine, and ultimately repeal&quot; the original Authorization for the Use of Military Force from 2001, the White House and Congress are still squabbling over the issue.</p>
<p>Nor have we done a very good job of identifying who the ГўВҖВңweГўВҖВқ is in this conflict. Just who is getting attacked here, and who is fighting back? Is it just France and the United States? Apparently not. President Obama, in Turkey for a Group of 20 summit, called the terrorist atrocity in Paris ГўВҖВңan attack on the civilized world.ГўВҖВқ Jeb Bush described it as ГўВҖВңan organized effort to destroy Western civilization.ГўВҖВқ His brother George at one point identified our side a &quot;mighty coalition of civilized nations.&quot; Is it AmericaГўВҖВҷs duty to lead this coalition and counter the radical threat? Hillary Clinton, again sounding a bit befuddled Saturday night, appeared not to think so, saying oxymoronically, ГўВҖВңThis cannot be an American fight, although American leadership is essential.ГўВҖВқ Martin OГўВҖВҷMalley disagreed with that, calling it ГўВҖВңAmericaГўВҖВҷs fight,ГўВҖВқ and even Bernie SandersГўВҖВ”before returning to his preferred enemy, the big banksГўВҖВ”concurred, saying ГўВҖВңthis country will rid our planet of this barbaric organization.ГўВҖВқ </p>
<p class="cms-textAlign-center">***</p>
<p><b>If I were a jihadist,</b> IГўВҖВҷd be heartened by all this. More than that, IГўВҖВҷd feel confident, even cocky. Because if my enemy canГўВҖВҷt plainly define his adversaries in a conflict, how can he possibly know what the conflict is aboutГўВҖВ”much less think about how to end it? Moreover, if my enemy canГўВҖВҷt say plainly what he is fighting <i>for</i>, how can he overwhelm the opposition with anything but messy, brute force? The jihadists think <i>they </i>know what theyГўВҖВҷre fighting for, a certainty that gives their cause a narrative power for potential recruits, and which has proved able to survive air strikes and land assaults.</p><div class="story-interrupt pos-beta format-s">

<p>Say, just for the sake of argument, that we all simply agreed on this point: Our common enemy is animated by a millenarian Islamist desire to build a ГўВҖВңcaliphateГўВҖВқ based on one extreme interpretation of the Koran and early Islamic history, and which sees the modern world as its enemy simply because it is Western and non-MuslimГўВҖВ”or ГўВҖВңCrusaders,ГўВҖВқ as the jihadi rhetoric has it. If we could all agree on that, at least we would all know what to look for. But according to various sources who have been involved in the discussions, the Obama administration has shied away from any real effort to study the internal dynamics of radical Islamism, mainly for fear of offending Muslims by being perceived as ГўВҖВңprofilingГўВҖВқ their religion.</p>
<p>Even some Muslims say that excess of cautionГўВҖВ”the unwillingness to look at the political and religious narrative behind the violenceГўВҖВ”is wrong. &quot;You can't start at just violence because the trajectory is so dangerous,&quot; says Hedieh Mirahmadi, a Muslim community organizer based in Washington, D.C. &quot;You need to start at that radicalism. The ideology.&quot; Instead U.S. officials tend to speak non-religiously of extremists and to treat the problem as one of law enforcement. The Bush administration &quot;didn't get very far either. Conceptually, in terms of what we were dealing with, our definitions shifted all the time,ГўВҖВқ Scott Carpenter, a former senior State Department official under George W. Bush and now an executive with Google Ideas, told me a in an interview few years ago. ГўВҖВңWe weren't consistent. Islamism. Islamo-fascism. And so on.</p>
<p>ГўВҖВңIf youГўВҖВҷre countering violent extremism, then by definition you are only relating to the radicalization process after which the person becomes violent,ГўВҖВқ Carpenter told me. ГўВҖВңItГўВҖВҷs like treating an outbreak of the epidemic after itГўВҖВҷs already started. There has to be a recognition that Islamist ideology plays an important role. You canГўВҖВҷt have an effective prescription without an accurate diagnosis. Part of the problem is, because we havenГўВҖВҷt figured out a way to talk about this in anything but a politically correct way, we donГўВҖВҷt talk about it.ГўВҖВқ</p><p>Mirahmadi saw this ostrich-like behavior firsthand as part of the steering committee for a conference on radicalization sponsored by the State and Defense departments and the RAND Corporation in 2010. Throughout the discussions, the draft report on the meetingГўВҖВҷs minutes was titled a ГўВҖВңDefining a Strategic Campaign to ГўВҖВҰ Counter and Delegitimize Radical Islamism,ГўВҖВқ she told me. ГўВҖВңWe made it all the way through the day of printing with that title,ГўВҖВқ she said. ГўВҖВңThere were probably 15 drafts.ГўВҖВқ But when the report finally arrived, the title had been changed. The term ГўВҖВңradical IslamismГўВҖВқ had become ГўВҖВңviolent extremism,ГўВҖВқ even though the 97-page report itself dealt almost entirely with problems in the Muslim world.</p>
<p>Obama, repeatedly challenged on both his strategy and his level of aggressiveness at the news conference on Monday, appeared to concede inadvertently both that a powerful counter-narrative was needed and the U.S. doesn't yet have one. The president spoke mainly of the conflict in military terms even as he declined once again to send more troops to take out the Islamic State, saying such a move would only ensure &quot;permanent occupation ГўВҖВҰ if you do not have local populations [supporting you], and pushing back against ideological extremes, then theyГўВҖВҷd resurface.&quot; Instead, Obama said, his strategy was &quot;squeezing the space in which they can operate&quot;ГўВҖВ”presumably from the airГўВҖВ”&quot;until ultimately we defeat them.&quot; But most military experts would tell you victory from the air alone isn't possible; and if one is going to rely on local populations, one must win over their &quot;hearts and minds&quot; with an alternative narrative. </p>
<p>Like the Bush administration before it, the Obama administration also has routinely ignored or played down warnings from experts about the Islamization of the Arab countries following the Arab Spring uprisings. Many Mideast scholars had predicted<b> </b>for years that, after the toppling of dictators, whether Saddam Hussein or Hosni Mubarak, the Arab people would inevitably turn back to their traditional haven of social stability, Islam. Political progress was never going to come from secularizing Arab society, the dream of the neocons in the Bush administration, but from rediscovering a more tolerant IslamГўВҖВ”a version of the faith that<b> </b>had in fact prevailed for hundreds of years before the 20th century. Osama bin Laden and now the Islamic State have transmogrified the old idea of a caliphate<b>ГўВҖВ”</b>which for generations during Ottoman times had taken the form of a pluralistic, Muslim-ruled empire<b>ГўВҖВ”</b>into a monstrosity of super-strict religious law and anti-Western enmity. </p>
<p>WhatГўВҖВҷs needed now, say some observers like Amil Khan, a scholar of Islam and former journalist for Reuters and the BBC, is less a ГўВҖВңcounterГўВҖВқ narrative than a ГўВҖВңparallelГўВҖВқ narrative that seeks to reform Arab society and do a better job of acknowledging the concerns of moderate Islam while knitting them into modern economic life , thus creating an alternative path to disaffected Muslim youth inspired by the jihadists. <b> </b>ГўВҖВңWhat ISIS (and AQ before it) do is to acknowledge all the problems, and package up a reason and a solution in religious/political terms,ГўВҖВқ says Khan. ГўВҖВңIf ISIS/AQ are saying; ГўВҖВҳEverything is messed up! Stand up and fight,ГўВҖВҷ a counter narrative would then say; ГўВҖВҳEverything is fine. Sit down and take it easy.ГўВҖВҷ Clearly, that wonГўВҖВҷt work. The AQ/ISIS narrative starts from acceptance that everything is screwed up, and then offers a way forward. WhatГўВҖВҷs needed is an authentic Muslim alternative to genuinely felt frustrations.ГўВҖВқ</p>
<p>In thisГўВҖВ”supplying the Arab people with a genuine alternative-- <b> </b>the Obama administration has been, by its own admission, less than successful. As Hillary Clinton herself said last year, on no less than the Jon Stewart show: &quot;We did a much better job telling the world who we were back in the Cold WarГўВҖВҰ. We had a big information effort. We sent talent, we sent all kinds of poets and novelists and rock stars. I remember when Vaclav Havel, the great dissident and the first president of the Czech Republic told me that Lou Reed had been his inspiration. American culture, American ideas permeated the world. WII, fast forward. That ended, and we kind of thought, okay, fine, end of history, democracy won. You know that story. And in fact, we withdrew from the information arena.ГўВҖВқ</p>
<p class="cms-textAlign-center">***</p>
<p><b>The recent declarations of war </b>tend to ignore that weГўВҖВҷve spent nearly a decade and a half and at least nearly <a href="http://web.archive.org/web/20211021222354/http://www.forbes.com/sites/niallmccarthy/2015/02/03/the-war-on-terror-has-cost-taxpayers-1-7-trillion-infographic/" target="_blank">$2 trillion</a> on wars already that remain unresolved. By contrast, consider the way we handled previous wars. On Dec. 9, 1941, two days after the Pearl Harbor attack, Franklin Delano Roosevelt addressed the American people in a fireside chat. ГўВҖВңWe are going to win the war, and we are going to win the peace that follows,&quot; he said. FDR was as good as his word. Over the next three and a half years, he and then his successor, Harry Truman, clearly defined the conflict, created the Atlantic Charter and elucidated the Four Freedoms to lay out the stakes, cultivated alliances that shared the fighting and dying, oversaw the defeat of two hegemonic threats (Japan and Germany), and began to rebuild these former enemies into peaceful democratic allies. At the same time the two presidents created many of the institutions that still define the global system, including the United Nations, planning for which began in 1944. </p>
<p>It took FDR and Truman precisely 1,347 days, from Dec. 7, 1941, to the surrender of Japan on Aug. 15, 1945, to win WWII, pacify the enemy and largely secure the peace that followed. </p>
<p>To be fair to todayГўВҖВҷs American leaders,<b> </b>weГўВҖВҷre constantly reminded this isnГўВҖВҷt a war like World War II. Former Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld liked to say that al Qaeda is &quot;not going to be signing some sort of a surrender aboard the battleship Missouri.&quot; The Bush administration used to compare the fight more to the ideological conflict of the Cold War. But here too the comparison is not flattering. True, the Cold War was, like the conflict with jihadism, a long, diffuse struggle against an ideological enemy and its affiliates. But it was<b> </b>also won by design: It began with George Kennan's famous Long Telegram in February 1946, in which he described what he later called &quot;the sources of Soviet conduct,&quot; and culminated in NSC-68, the famous National Security Council white paper that laid out the strategy against the Soviet Union, in the spring of 1950. In one of the most celebrated analyses in American history, Kennan divined the internal sources of conflict within the Soviet empire and projected that, with time, it would destroy itself. The key was waiting it out, applying strategic pressure, andГўВҖВ”just as important ГўВҖВ“ putting forth an alternate ideal. &quot;The real question is: Which system travels better?&quot; John F. Kennedy said at the height of the Cold War. Who has the better set of ideas? </p>
<p>Containment doctrine identified the enemy precisely, and brought almost everyone on board, despite a few disastrous digressions, the worst of which was Vietnam. And NSC-68 was no boilerplate, like so many of the ГўВҖВңnational security strategiesГўВҖВқ we see coming out of the White House today: This internal policy document included precise requests for defense spending and projections for how America could outspend the Soviet Union. In just about four years, America had developed a strategy that ultimately prevailed.</p>
<p>Obama administration officials deny, of course, that they lack a strategy, or that they are ignoring opportunities to understand the deeper sources of radical Islam. They have sought out deeper contacts in Muslim communities, and they are studying a British program known formally as PVE, or &quot;Preventing Extremist Violence.&quot; And itГўВҖВҷs fair to point out that, as a matter of official policy, any program that promotes an alternative religious narrativeГўВҖВ”taking sides on behalf of a more tolerant Islam, for instanceГўВҖВ”runs up against the Constitutional separation of church and state, and would no doubt suffer court challenges. Indeed, a senior U.S. official concedes that the official policy is still not to talk about Islam or the abuse of Islam directly as a source of terror. Instead, he says, the policy is &quot;long-term engagement with the countries from which terror is emanating and through those countries to leading influencers [imams]. No. 2, weГўВҖВҷve been reaching out to the IT companies. Both Facebook and Twitter have become much more active in pulling down radical content.&quot; </p>
<p>But whatГўВҖВҷs being done now is not enough. And at the very least we can identify today clearly who <i>our </i>side isГўВҖВ”what ГўВҖВңthe civilized worldГўВҖВқ means, as we did in World War I, World War II and the Cold War. HereГўВҖВҷs a start:<b> </b>WeГўВҖВ”the United States, Europe and other major nationsГўВҖВ”are part of a vast, multidimensional global system that consists of trading rules, of international law, of norms for economic and political behavior, and a fundamental democratic consensus on basic values and freedoms. Even the countries that donГўВҖВҷt share some of these values and freedoms, like China and Russia, want to be part of this global system because theyГўВҖВҷre a lot more prosperous inside the system than outside it. </p>
<p>So it should be pretty clear: The people of Paris were on one side in this conflict, the civilization side, and the murderers who gunned them down and blew them up were on the otherГўВҖВ”the side of those who want to tear that global system down. ItГўВҖВҷs a war, and those are the stakes.</p>
<p>Even if the stakes were spelled out that simply, it would at least be the start of an effective ГўВҖВңcounter-narrative,ГўВҖВқ something to remind people what weГўВҖВҷre fighting for, and at the same time to remind them who the enemy is. And perhaps turn this into a ГўВҖВңwarГўВҖВқ we can actually win. </p><div class="story-share ">
