Vance&#039;s jibe. | Brendan Smialowski/AFP via Getty Images July 16, 2024 12:44 pm CET By Andrew McDonald LONDON — Labour ministers have rejected a suggestion by Donald Trump&#8217;s new running mate that the U.K. is an &#8220;Islamist country.&#8221; J.D. Vance, named Monday as the Republican vice presidential nominee, described Britain under the new Labour government as the world&#8217;s first &#8220;truly Islamist country&#8221; to have a nuclear weapon. It was a claim which came as a blow to Labour ministers, in office for less than a fortnight, who have been keen to curry favor both with Trump and Vance. Advertisement Advertisement Labour&#8217;s Deputy PM Angela Rayner told ITV: &#8220;Look, I don’t recognize that characterization, I’m very proud of the election success that Labour had recently. I think he said quite a lot of fruity things in the past as well.&#8221; Speaking days before being adopted by Trump as his vice-presidential pick, Vance, a junior senator from Ohio, told the National Conservatism Conference : “I was talking with a friend recently and we were talking about [how] one of the big dangers in the world, of course, is nuclear proliferation, though the Biden administration doesn’t care about it. &#8220;And I was talking about what is the first truly Islamist country that will get a nuclear weapon, and we were like, maybe it’s Iran, maybe Pakistan already kind of counts, and then we sort of finally decided maybe it’s actually the UK, since&nbsp;Labour&nbsp;just took over,&#8221; Vance added. Labour — which won July 4&#8217;s U.K. election in a landslide — dismissed Vance&#8217;s jibe. Junior Treasury minister James Murray, told Times Radio he didn&#8217;t &#8220;really know what [Vance] meant by that.&#8221; The jibe is troubling for new Foreign Secretary David Lammy, who has attempted to build bridges with Trump&#8217;s allies including Vance. Advertisement Advertisement Lammy in May described Vance as a &#8220;friend&#8221;, after meeting him on multiple occasions. He has talked up Vance&#8217;s book &#8220;Hillbilly Elegy,&#8221; telling telling POLITICO’s Power Play podcast in March that he recognized the stories of working-class life, left-behind communities and his strong faith in the &#8220;wonderful book.&#8221; Trump unveiled Vance, a former critic of his and staunch isolationist, as his running mate Monday evening. EU officials are concerned about what Vance as vice president would mean for Ukraine, after he had suggested the West could not afford to continue to arm the Kyiv government.
