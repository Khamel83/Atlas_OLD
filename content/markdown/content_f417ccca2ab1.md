                            The Sign-up Bonus Strategy                        </h2>
                                                <div class="post-meta header-3">
                                                            		<div class="auth_img" style="">
    									<img alt="" src="http://web.archive.org/web/20170422230214im_/http://2.gravatar.com/avatar/b0d295f9857bcc5e3800911570a7fa20?s=150&amp;d=mm&amp;r=g" srcset="http://web.archive.org/web/20170422230214im_/http://2.gravatar.com/avatar/b0d295f9857bcc5e3800911570a7fa20?s=300&amp;d=mm&amp;r=g 2x" class="avatar avatar-thumbnail wp-user-avatar wp-user-avatar-thumbnail alignnone photo"/>                              		</div>

                                                            <div class="img-holder img-cover" style="background-image:url('http://web.archive.org/web/20170422230214im_/http://www.frugaltravelguy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Credit-Cards.jpg')">
                                                                                                                                            </div>
                                                    <p>We all know how complicated this hobby can be. For example, you need to use <i>that card</i> when dining out, <i>this card</i> at Lowe’s, and an entirely different card when ordering anything online. Not only that, but you need to reconfigure all of that from time to time since category bonuses and offers change. Add a new card or two in there and it can become a huge headache, especially if you’re not organized.</p>
<p>I actually am organized, however. I love whipping out my Chase <a href="http://web.archive.org/web/20170422230214/http://www.frugaltravelguy.com/credit-cards/business-cards#inkplus" target="_blank">Ink Plus card</a> when I’m out at Staples or my husband’s <a href="http://web.archive.org/web/20170422230214/http://www.frugaltravelguy.com/credit-cards/business-cards#inkbold" target="_blank">Chase Freedom card</a> whenever I’m filling up my tank. Or strategizing how to get as many points as possible through the use of shopping portals or special offers.</p>
<p>My husband, on the other hand, <i>not so much.</i></p>
<p>I’m lucky that he lets me sign us up for five gazillion credit cards as it is, and I don’t want to push my luck by asking him to do too much. And that, my friends, is why we focus mostly on the sign-up bonus strategy these days.</p>
<p><b>The Sign-up Bonus Strategy</b></p>
<p>The sign-up bonus strategy is exactly what it sounds like it is. Instead of asking my husband to take time out of his busy day to rack his brain over what card to use, we only focus on one card at a time instead. That way we can pursue more sign-up bonuses than most, which are inarguably one of the most lucrative ways to score tons of points and miles. The benefits of this strategy:</p>
<ul>
<li><span style="color: #e46d33;"><b>More points and miles.</b></span> Credit card sign-up bonuses provide a quick and easy way to rack up some serious points and miles. And by focusing solely on this strategy, we continually score tons of points and miles in short spurts of time.</li>
<li><span style="color: #e46d33;"><b>It’s less complicated.</b> </span>Even though I sometimes whip out a specific card to score a category bonus, we usually just lump all of our spending on whatever card we’re trying to meet the minimum spending requirement on. I’ve found that this is far less complicated than the alternative since I can trade cards in and out of my husband’s wallet as it suits me.</li>
<li><span style="color: #e46d33;"><b>You can take advantage of opportunities.</b> </span>Since we’re always on the lookout for a banging’ sign-up bonus, we are usually in a position to take advantage of whatever comes along. Excellent offers come and go, yes, but there are times when you just have to go for it!</li>
</ul>
<p>The only downfall of pursuing this strategy is that you might end up with a <i>whole lotta credit cards</i>. <b><i>Guilty! </i></b>Then again, that is really only a problem if you’re disorganized or stressed out by the idea. I personally <a href="http://web.archive.org/web/20170422230214/http://www.frugaltravelguy.com/2014/06/how-i-organize-my-rewards-credit-cards-with-a-simple-spreadsheet.html" target="_blank">keep all of our credit card details in a simple</a> spreadsheet where I can track details like the day we signed up and received the card, when the annual fee is due, and when we earned any applicable bonus. It helps.</p>
<p>The bottom line is this: This hobby is only as complicated as you make it. If you hate digging through your wallet and scrutinizing every purchase, the sign-up bonus strategy is one way to avoid that drama while scoring the deals you want.</p>
<p>It’s also a great way to make sure this hobby doesn’t drive your spouse insane. <i>At least not completely.</i></p>
<p>Do you mostly pursue sign-up bonuses?  Or do you have a different strategy that works better?</p>
