Northwestern investigating hazing allegation in football program - The Athletic NCAAF Teams Scores &amp; Schedule Standings Podcasts Until Saturday Newsletter Recruiting Odds Picks All-136 Ranking Latest Playoff Projections Quizzes Rivalry Rankings Northwestern investigating hazing allegation in football program By Matt Fortuna and The Athletic Staff Jan. 11, 2023 Updated July 20, 2023 Share 41 Northwestern University has appointed an independent attorney to investigate allegations of potential hazing within its football program, the school confirmed in a statement to The Athletic on Wednesday. Athletic director Derrick Gragg informed the team of the investigation Wednesday, a person with knowledge of the situation told The Athletic . Advertisement “After the end of the 2022 season, Northwestern received an allegation of potential hazing in the football program,” the statement said. “While we do not yet know whether the allegations are true, hazing is prohibited by University policy, and we take these claims seriously. The health, safety and well-being of our students is the first priority.” The school said it appointed an independent attorney, Maggie Hickey , a partner at the ArentFox Schiff firm, to investigate the claims immediately after it was made aware of the hazing allegation. The inquiry is “in the very early stages and will be rigorously fair to everyone in this process,” the school said, adding that it expects the investigation to include interviews with players, coaches and staff. “It will focus on gathering facts and will not jump to conclusions,” per the statement. “Ms. Hickey has a wealth of experience investigating complaints, and she has the full cooperation of the University and the athletics department,” the statement said. “… The purpose of Ms. Hickey’s investigation is to find the underlying truth of the allegations — including the scope of any potential hazing activity or harmful culture.” Northwestern also said in the statement it “strongly supports members of our community who come forward with concerns and encourages anyone to report those concerns to the University.” The Wildcats finished 1-11 the past season (1-8 in the Big Ten), which marked their worst record since Pat Fitzgerald took over the program as head coach in 2006.
