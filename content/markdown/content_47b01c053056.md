><p>We use two different interventions to deliver chlorine treatment directly to water points at no cost to families and individuals.<br
/> <br
/>Our network of chlorine dispensers stretches across rural communities in Kenya, Malawi, and Uganda, in areas that aren’t reached by municipal systems. A community member goes to their water source, places their bucket under the dispenser, turns the valve to dispense the correct dose of chlorine, and then fills their bucket with water. The chlorine disinfects the water during their walk home, so by the time they arrive, it’s safe to drink.</p><p>In places with communal piped water systems — often in peri-urban and urban communities — we use in-line chlorination devices, which are installed directly on water tanks. As water flows into pipes, it’s automatically chlorinated in the correct dose, so it’s treated and safe to drink by the time it reaches someone’s home.</p><p>With these two complementary interventions in our toolbox, we’re able to provide access to safe water to additional communities with diverse types of water systems. This approach allows us to expand our reach, while also sharing operational resources to keep costs low.</p></div></div></div></div></div><div
