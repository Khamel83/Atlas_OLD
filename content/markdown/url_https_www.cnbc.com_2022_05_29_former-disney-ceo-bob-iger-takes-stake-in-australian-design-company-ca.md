Valerie Macon | AFP | Getty Images Former Disney CEO Bob Iger has acquired a stake in Australian design company Canva and agreed to be an advisor to the closely held company. &quot;We&#x27;re incredibly excited to welcome Bob Iger as an investor and advisor,&quot; said Canva spokesman Lachlan Andrews in a statement. &quot;Bob brings a wealth of experience after 15 years at the helm of one of the most loved and most creative brands in the world and we&#x27;re looking forward to working with him.&quot; Iger and Canva declined to comment on the size of the investment. Canva raised money in September, valuing the company at $40 billion. The graphic design software company earned the No. 4 ranking on CNBC&#x27;s annual Disruptor 50 list, released earlier this month. Canva has more than 75 million monthly users and was on pace to reach a $1 billion annualized revenue run rate in late 2021 , CNBC reported. The Sydney-based company helps people create presentations, videos and other designs, competing against Adobe, Microsoft and other graphic-design software firms. Since stepping down from Disney in December, Iger has been investing his own money in several companies, including delivery startup GoPuff and toy maker Funko.
