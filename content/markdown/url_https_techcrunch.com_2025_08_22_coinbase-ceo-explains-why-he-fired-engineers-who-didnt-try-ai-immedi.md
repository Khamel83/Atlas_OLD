But those who refused to try the tools when Coinbase bought enterprise licenses for GitHub Copilot and Cursor got promptly fired, CEO Brian Armstrong said this week on John Collison’s podcast &#8220;Cheeky Pint.&#8221; (Collison is the co-founder and president of the payments company Stripe.) After getting licenses to cover every engineer, some at the cryptocurrency exchange warned Armstrong that adoption would be slow, predicting it would take months to get even half the engineers using AI.&nbsp; Armstrong was shocked at the thought. “I went rogue,” he said, and posted a mandate in the company’s main engineering Slack channel. “I said, &#8216;AI is important. We need you to all learn it and at least onboard. You don&#8217;t have to use it every day yet until we do some training, but at least onboard by the end of the week. And if not, I&#8217;m hosting a meeting on Saturday with everybody who hasn&#8217;t done it and I&#8217;d like to meet with you to understand why.&#8217;&#8221;&nbsp; At the meeting, some people had reasonable explanations for not getting their AI assistant accounts set up during the week, like being on vacation, Armstrong said. “I jumped on this call on Saturday and there were a couple people that had not done it. Some of them had a good reason, because they were just getting back from some trip or something, and some of them didn&#8217;t [have a good reason].
