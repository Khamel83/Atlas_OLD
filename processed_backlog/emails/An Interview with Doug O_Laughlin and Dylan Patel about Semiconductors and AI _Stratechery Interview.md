GPU-poor vs. GPU-rich

*Well, Doug, we’ll get back to you. But as I think you agree, we have to start with Dylan, who this week, has been getting into spats with Sam Altman ( https://twitter.com/dylan522p/status/1696405806286279033?s=20 ) , amongst other folks.*

*You wrote an article about Google’s upcoming Gemini model ( https://www.SemiAnalysis.com/p/google-gemini-eats-the-world-gemini ) and how powerful it is relative to GPT. I mean I had to work really hard to establish Aggregation Theory or aggregators as a concept that people talked about in a regular way. You, though, drop this concept this week of GPU-rich versus GPU-poor, which people are already putting in their Twitter bios! *DP:* Sure, at least on the impact of it, I feel like there are two ways to look at it. One is like, “Hey, you guys are GPU-poor, you’re never going to be able to compete with the big dogs.” But that’s absolutely not what I’m trying to say. You don’t have enough GPUs to train the biggest model ever, how are you ever going to fight OpenAI, Google, Meta, et cetera? I don’t think that’s the right way to look at it.

The right way to look at it is, hey, you have significantly fewer chips, you have less data, so you need to be a lot more intelligent with what you do. Also, on top of that, the open source community, the whole “Google we have no moat” ( https://www.semianalysis.com/p/google-we-have-no-moat-and-neither ) , that whole open source community, is hyper-fixating on things that are destructive to them, while having fewer chips than the big competitors which hinders them significantly. When you look at just the raw numbers, if this is just a game of compute, Google will win. The only reason OpenAI won with GPT 3.5 and 4 was because they had focus, they targeted, and they made the correct architectural decisions. But if the cat’s out of the bag now, [and computer is all that matters,] Google will win and steamroll literally everyone because they have a compute cost that is one-fourth that of literally everyone else, given the fact that they designed their own chips and those chips are actually good, unlike Amazon’s and Microsoft’s upcoming and Meta’s. Everyone else has to pay the Jensen Huang 85% plus tax on gross margins, but Google wins because of that, so there’s a whole lot more that people need to do.

AI Startups
