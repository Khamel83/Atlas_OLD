Chapel Bill, WBD’s New Zazonomics, Gabbard’s Charm Campaign

Happy Friday and welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon dispatch featuring Puck’s best new reporting.

Before jumping in, we’re thrilled to announce that the legendary Hollywood journalist Kim Masters is joining Puck! Starting in February, Kim will reunite with Matt Belloni and Eriq Gardner on What I’m Hearing, while also breaking news and participating in our ever-growing suite of podcasts. (If you’re not signed up for What I’m Hearing for some crazy reason, remedy that now ( https://puck.news/newsletters/what-im-hearing/?utm_campaign=The+Daily+Courant+-+SUBSCRIBERS+%2812%2F13%2F24%29&utm_content=The+Daily+Courant+-+SUBSCRIBERS+%2812%2F13%2F24%29&utm_medium=email_action&utm_source=customer.io&utm_term=f6c60617da42abd12a ).) Welcome to the team, Kim!

On that note… Don’t miss Matt’s chronicle of the turbulence surrounding “Hollywood’s silliest awards show,” the Golden Globes, two years after
billionaires Todd Boehly and Jay Penske turned the organization that puts on the event into a for-profit business—a notoriously fraught deal that has been plagued by conflicts of interests and a state attorney general inquiry related to its charitable arm. More on that below the fold.

Also in today’s issue: Lauren Sherman charts an especially thrilling round of designer musical chairs after Chanel finally confirmed Matthieu Blazy as its creative director. John Ourand deciphers how ESPN might capitalize on Bill Belichick’s voyage to Chapel Hill. Tara Palmeri connects with veterans advocate Paul Rieckhoff to assess the very real concerns surrounding Pete Hegseth. On The Grill Room, Dylan Byers is joined by Bill Cohan to dissect the financial calculus behind Zaz’s decision to restructure WBD. On Impolitic, John Heilemann reunites with MSNBC
host (and his former Circus co-star) Alex Wagner. On The Town, Matt and the Entertainment Strategy Guy sort through the state of streaming. On Fashion People, Lauren and Skims C.E.O. Jens Grede discuss the brand’s rapid rise, their sold-out North Face collaboration, and the Kim Kardashian of it all. And on The Powers That Be, Ben Landy and Tara consider Tulsi Gabbard’s charm campaign on the Hill.
