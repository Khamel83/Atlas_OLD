A Musk Meme-Stock Merger, NBCU’s Prima Donna, Legacy Media’s Last Stand

Happy Thanksgiving, welcome back to The Daily Courant, and many thanks for spending a few minutes of your holiday respite with us. In the spirit of celebration, today we’re resurfacing a few of our favorite stories from the past month.

To ease into our holiday roundup, we hope you’ll curl up with Dylan Byers’ revelatory reporting on MSNBC’s nine-figure contract renegotiation with star talent Rachel Maddow—a contract that sent tremors through 30 Rock in the wake of Brian Roberts’ seismic decision to spin off NBCU’s cable networks.

Still hungry? Don’t miss Lauren Sherman’s thrilling reporting on Vanity Fair’s head-scratching choice to launch its annual star-studded Hollywood Issue three months early, and the skepticism surrounding Condé Nast C.E.O. Roger Lynch’s growth strategy.

What else… Bill Cohan crunches the numbers on the
mind-boggling murmurs of a combination between Musk’s worthless X and Trump’s overvalued meme-stock, DTJ. Matt Belloni chronicles the ascension of NBCU’s Donna Langley, who, with her new remit, has effectively become Hollywood’s most powerful woman. And Abby Livingston offers her bracing conversation with Senator-elect Elissa Slotkin about why U.S. intelligence officers are worried about Tulsi Gabbard.

But wait, there’s more! For those seeking to tune out the political clamor in the dining room, check out Dylan’s conversation with Axios co-founder Jim VandeHei on The Grill Room, in which they dissect the existential challenges facing The Washington Post, CNN, and legacy media writ large. Meanwhile, on The Varsity, John Ourand offers up his roving conversation with top ESPN broadcaster Mike “Greeny” Greenberg on how the market for on-air talent has changed over
the years.
