Good morning,

Today’s Daily Update Interview is with Michael Nathanson, media analyst and co-founder of MoffettNathanson . MoffettNathanson, which was founded in 2013, is a subscription-based research group primarily serving Wall Street (SVB financial group, the parent company of Silicon Valley Bank, acquired MoffettNathanson in 2021 ).

I previously interviewed Nathanson last June , and it is incredible how much about the streaming and media industries have changed since then. We spend a good amount of time on Netflix, and by extension the media industry: the reason to be bullish on the former is by being bearish on the latter. We also cover Disney’s CEO change, the future of bundles, and the intersection of tech companies and sports rights.

The Netflix Bull Case in 2023

I mean, at this point though, when it comes to the general entertainment, I mean, the long-term Netflix bull case seems pretty compelling. If the rest of the companies can’t get their act together to do a Hulu V1 strategy to put their stuff together in a rational way, they’re going to end up having to sell to Netflix. And Netflix is going to get their content at a discount because they’re going to have to pay their debts. I mean, that seems more likely than ever.

MN: Hence, my pivot to more optimism for that stock. I still think it’s ahead of itself. So, I’ll tell you what started that, Ben, is that when the year ended, S&P’s credit analyst, Naveen Sarma, downgraded AMC Networks, a small company that no one really cares about. It’s so small. He downgraded their debt. And in the debt downgrade, he started talking about default risk. I’m like, “Whoa, where’s this coming from?”

So his point on default risk is that firstly, if you look at EBITDA, which is a Wall Street metric for cash flow, it no longer represents cash flow anymore because there is so much content spending that’s mismatched with the amortization cost. There’s a huge mismatch. So, if you look at EBITDA, it’s not capturing the true cost of content because content has to be refreshed almost daily now in streaming. So, Naveen is hinting that maybe we should look at cash flow to debt. And by doing that, you realize these companies all are highly leveraged cash flow to debt. The only way you manage cash flow to debt is by spending less on content. And when you spend less on content, the whole flywheel breaks down.

So it hit me that, and he shot a warning blast across the industry by doing this to AMC and saying, “Look, you could all be next if you don’t get your house in order with cord-cutting and add a recession.” So, that’s why I started focusing on this idea.
