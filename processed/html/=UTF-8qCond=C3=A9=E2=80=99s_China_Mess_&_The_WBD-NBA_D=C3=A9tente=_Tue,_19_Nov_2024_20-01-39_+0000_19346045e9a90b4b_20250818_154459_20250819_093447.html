Chanel Succession Sweepstakes, The Rock’s Hard Place, A G.O.P. Senate Rebellion

Welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon assortment of Puck’s best new reporting.

First up today, Lauren Sherman deciphers Vanity Fair ’s seemingly strange decision to drop its annual star-studded Hollywood Issue three months early—a potentially financially motivated decision that has stoked renewed skepticism of Condé Nast C.E.O. Roger Lynch ’s strategy for weathering a rough ’24 and the company’s ongoing headwinds in China.

Plus, below the fold: John Ourand rings up TNT Sports chairman and C.E.O. Luis Siberwasser for a candid postmortem on the NBA-WBD rights scuffle and WBD’s bundle of concessions. Peter Hamby has exclusive data on Dems’ media handicap and Trump ’s perplexing goodwill from voters. And Scott Mendelson identifies a handful of silver linings in Red One ’s relatively catastrophic box office performance.

Meanwhile, in the Puck podcast universe: Tara Palmeri huddles up with Marc Caputo and Meridith McGraw on Somebody’s Gotta Win to make sense of Trump’s controversial cabinet picks. Matt and Lucas unpack Disney’s boffo quarter and Netflix’s live sports growing pains on The Town . On Fashion People , Lauren is joined by Emilia Petrarca to evaluate Chanel’s succession sweepstakes. On The Grill Room , Dylan Byers talks to Infatuation C.E.O. Paul Needham and entrepreneur Alison Roman about the food media business. And on The Powers That Be , Abby Livingston and Peter evaluate Matt Gaetz ’s confirmation odds in the Senate.

Vanity Fair’s Hollywood Issues

The decision to release VF’s annual star-packed Hollywood issue three months early—before Thanksgiving!—did little to quiet speculation about Roger Lynch & Co’s strategy to grapple with Condé’s rough ’24, an ongoing China problem, and expected layoffs.

The Shape of Silberwasser

JOHN OURAND

A candid chat with TNT Sports chairman and C.E.O. Luis Silberwasser on losing the NBA, winning back some concessions in the afterbidding market, and the mediaco’s realistic expectations for their accumulating grab bag of new rights—Roland Garros, Mountain West football, NASCAR, etcetera.

Pod Save MAGA?

PETER HAMBY

New proprietary polling from Echelon Insights, in partnership with Puck, shows that Donald Trump is entering office with goodwill from voters. It also illustrates that Democrats don’t simply have a political and coalition problem. They have a pretty severe media handicap, too.

‘Red One’ and the New Arithmetic of Flops

SCOTT MENDELSON

The Rock’s kidnapped Santa caper sure looks like a $253 million Christmas catastrophe for Amazon MGM Studios. It also represents the peril of a certain kind of genre and a limit to its star’s bankability.

Playing Trump Cabinet Roulette, With Marc Caputo & Meridith McGraw

TARA PALMERI

Tara is joined by The Bulwark’s Marc Caputo and Politico’s Meridith McGraw to discuss Donald Trump’s increasingly controversial cabinet selections. They shine a light on Trump’s new chief of staff, Susie Wiles; debate the viability of Matt Gaetz as his selection for attorney general; and speculate on what Trump's first days in office will look like.

Disney’s Streaming Turnaround & Netflix’s Boxing Debut

MATTHEW BELLONI

Matt is joined by Bloomberg’s Lucas Shaw to talk about Disney reaching profitability in streaming, how Bob Iger managed to turn the company around in his two years since returning as C.E.O., and whether this is the beginning of a larger turnaround for Disney. They also discuss Netflix’s Jake Paul vs. Mike Tyson boxing event, the glitchy live broadcast, and what this means for Netflix in general.

Peak Gift Guide

LAUREN SHERMAN

Lauren is joined by journalist Emilie Petrarca, writer of the excellent newsletter, Shop Rat. They talk Taylor Swift’s new watch hobby, LVMH heir Alexandre Arnault’s new job, the absolutely nuts barrage of holiday gift guides taking over our lives, and whether Bottega Veneta’s Matthieu Blazy is the right designer to lead Chanel. Plus, Lauren recaps Puck’s Stories of the Season event in Hollywood and has some Condé Nast updates.

Alison Roman Chops It Up

DYLAN BYERS

It’s a food media-themed doubleheader in The Grill Room! First, Dylan convenes with The Infatuation C.E.O. Paul Needham, who discusses the economics of his restaurant recommendation empire. Then, acclaimed food writer and entrepreneur Alison Roman stops by to discuss her journey from legacy media to entrepreneurship and why her defenestration from The New York Times turned out to be a blessing in disguise.

A G.O.P. Senate Rebellion

PETER HAMBY & ABBY LIVINGSTON

Abby Livingston joins Peter to dissect Trump’s latest power move in Washington: attempting to push his controversial cabinet picks through the Senate without confirmation hearings. They dive into the brewing battle, and chew over whether this will ignite a showdown between MAGA loyalists and the remnants of the G.O.P. establishment.
