Good morning,

This week’s Stratechery Interview is with Ben Bajarin . Bajarin is the CEO and Principal Analyst for Creative Strategies , one of the oldest technology-focused market research firms. Bajarin and Creative Strategies have a particular focus on semiconductors, and Bajarin also hosts The Circuit Podcast about the semiconductor industry with previous Stratechery Interview subject Jay Goldberg .

Speaking of Goldberg, he wrote a post entitled Intel — Reality in response to my Article Intel Honesty . I think it is an excellent articulation of the counter-case to my conclusion that Intel needs to split: yes, Intel is struggling, but that is because this transition is really hard, and the opportunity for a company that can provide more leading edge capacity is real and substantial — and oh-by-the-way, splitting up the company is far more difficult and increases the chances of failure.

To that end, after discussing Apple and Google in the context of smartphones and AI, Bajarin and I have what I’m calling an Intel Therapy session; I’m glad Goldberg wrote that piece, because I’m really uneasy and conflicted about what exactly should be done about Intel, for reasons we get into in this Interview. I don’t know if my prescription is right, but, as I noted in the title, I’m trying to be honest.

It feels like it could be moving in that direction, but we still don’t know.

Well, I do think this is the one thing Nvidia is really focused on, and what is one of Intel’s saving graces is Intel still dominates on-premises servers.
