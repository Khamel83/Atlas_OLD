Harris Postmortems, Media M&A Murmurs, Gala Season Dish

Welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon download of Puck’s freshest reporting.

First up today, Lauren Sherman digs into the latest corporate intrigues inside Gap Inc., where C.E.O. Richard Dickson has been rapidly pruning the current leadership structure, exiting executives, and doubling down on his Zac Posen experiment. (Even Posen, himself, might not be safe…)

Plus, below the fold: Peter Hamby offers a bracing prescription for the Democrats’ dark night of the soul. John Ourand teams up with LightShed’s Rich Greenfield to assess how the sports media business could transform under Trump . And Scott Mendelson previews the pre-Thanksgiving box office trifecta of Gladiator II , Wicked: Part One , and Moana 2 .

Meanwhile, in Puck’s podcast universe: Matt Belloni reunites with Bloomberg’s Lucas Shaw to discuss the latest WBD earnings and Zaslav ’s M&A euphoria. Axios co-founder Jim VandeHei joins Dylan Byers on The Grill Room to chew over the most pressing media plotlines following Trump’s victory. On Fashion People , Lauren Sherman and stylist Kate Young explore the fall gala season, the state of luxury, and the Taylor Swift of it all. And on Somebody’s Gotta Win , Tara Palmeri and pollster Evan Roth Smith re-examine Harris ’s most lethal campaign missteps.

King Richard

LAUREN SHERMAN

When Gap Inc. chief Richard Dickson landed at the company, he was goaded by a former executive to “fire everyone.” Now, just over a year into his tenure, he’s stripping management layers and acting on long-anticipated exits. Plus, updates on Behnaz Ghahramani, Zac Posen, and more San Francisco treats.

The Missing Resistance & The Tao of Rahm

PETER HAMBY

After the heartburn of 2004, Democrats rebuilt their party through a new set of candidates in the House, winning the midterms and paving the way for a generationally talented, constituency-realigning outsider to claim the presidency four years later. Rahm Emanuel, who helped engineer the party’s comeback, has a few ideas about what the Democrats need to do now in order to reclaim the presidency—with no #Resistance in sight.

Will Zaz’s Prayers Be Answered?

JOHN OURAND

Media C.E.O.s are hoping against hope that Trump 2.0 will usher in a new M&A permission structure allowing them to, among other things, finally offload those linear TV assets they should have bailed on 10 years ago, like Rupert did. But, as LightShed Partners’ Rich Greenfield argues, nothing is easy and everything takes forever.

Thanksgiving’s Refreshingly Old-School Box Office Battle

SCOTT MENDELSON

For the first time since ‘Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald’ opened days before ‘Ralph Breaks the Internet,’ in 2018, this year’s mid-November box office slate will mimic the pattern from the prior two decades. Everyone should win.

Netflix’s Greta Gerwig Situation, A Buried Clint Eastwood Movie & Streaming’s Good Week

MATTHEW BELLONI

Matt is joined by Bloomberg’s Lucas Shaw to discuss the latest Warner Bros. Discovery earnings and if streaming has finally turned a corner in becoming a viable business. Then they dig into Netflix recently granting Greta Gerwig’s upcoming Narnia movie IMAX theaters and if this marks the beginning of a new trend for Netflix.

Legacy Media’s Last Stand

DYLAN BYERS

Dylan is joined by Axios co-founder Jim VandeHei for a wide-ranging discussion about the most pressing media storylines following Trump’s victory: Musk’s ascension and the future of X, the balkanized media landscape, the Dems’ Joe Rogan problem, the existential challenges facing The Washington Post and CNN, and much, much more.

It’s Gala Season!

LAUREN SHERMAN

Lauren is joined by stylist Kate Young to talk about the fall gala season—from Baby2Baby to LACMA Art + Film—but also the state of luxury, the state of the world, and the state of Taylor Swift’s going-out clothes. Plus, Lauren has new details on a fashion micro-drama and checks in on Gap’s turnaround efforts.

Diagnosing the Democratic Condition

TARA PALMERI

Tara provides an update on Trump’s cabinet selections as his transition team prepares for Inauguration Day. She’s then joined by pollster Evan Roth Smith to go through the numbers and break down how Kamala Harris lost the presidential election. They examine which of the gettable voter bases leaned to the right on election day, and Evan offers some sobering advice to his Democratic colleagues on how to move forward as a party.
